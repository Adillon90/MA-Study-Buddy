### Q191 | 2.0 | Easy
The STARR acronym captures all five methods of handling risk. Which method does purchasing insurance represent?
- A) Sharing
- B) Transfer
- C) Avoidance
- D) Retention
**Answer: B** — Insurance is risk transfer — the insured shifts the financial burden of potential loss to the insurer.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q192 | 2.0 | Easy
The cause of a loss is called the:
- A) Hazard
- B) Peril
- C) Exposure
- D) Risk
**Answer: B** — A peril is the cause of loss (e.g., sickness, accident); a hazard is a condition that increases the chance of loss.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q193 | 2.0 | Easy
Pure risk is insurable because it involves:
- A) A chance of gain or loss
- B) Only the possibility of loss or no loss
- C) Catastrophic exposure to the insurer
- D) Speculative outcomes
**Answer: B** — Pure risk (loss or no loss only) is insurable; speculative risk (chance of gain) is not.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q194 | 2.0 | Easy
The mathematical foundation of insurance — that larger pools produce more predictable loss ratios — is called the:
- A) Principle of indemnity
- B) Law of large numbers
- C) Adverse selection principle
- D) Reinsurance doctrine
**Answer: B** — The law of large numbers states that the larger the pool of similar exposures, the more accurately losses can be predicted.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q195 | 2.0 | Easy
A mutual insurance company is owned by its:
- A) Stockholders
- B) Board of directors
- C) Policyholders
- D) State regulators
**Answer: C** — Mutual companies are owned by policyholders; they issue participating policies that may pay policy dividends.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### Q196 | 2.0 | Easy
Policy dividends from a mutual insurer are characterized as:
- A) Taxable investment income
- B) A return of excess premium — not taxable income
- C) A guaranteed contractual payment
- D) A guaranteed annual payment regardless of insurer experience
**Answer: B** — Policy dividends are treated as a return of excess premium and are not taxable income to the policyholder.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### Q197 | 2.0 | Easy
An independent insurance agent who represents several insurers owns the:
- A) Insurer's rate manuals
- B) Expirations (the book of business)
- C) Insurer's licensing rights
- D) Authority to set premium rates for any insurer she represents
**Answer: B** — In the independent agency system, the agent owns the expirations — the client relationship — unlike exclusive/captive agents.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### Q198 | 2.0 | Easy
A Kenyan insurer selling insurance in Massachusetts is classified in Massachusetts as a(n):
- A) Domestic insurer
- B) Foreign insurer
- C) Alien insurer
- D) Surplus lines insurer by default
**Answer: C** — Incorporated outside the United States means alien; incorporated in another U.S. state means foreign.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### Q199 | 2.0 | Medium
Gina, a physical therapist, leaves her treatment table slippery after a rain-soaked umbrella drips on it. A patient slips. The slippery table is an example of:
- A) A moral hazard
- B) A morale hazard
- C) A physical hazard
- D) A peril
**Answer: C** — A tangible physical condition (the slippery table) that increases the chance of loss is a physical hazard; the fall would be the peril.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q200 | 2.0 | Medium
A contractor knowingly inflates repair estimates to collect larger insurance checks. This is an example of:
- A) Morale hazard
- B) Physical hazard
- C) Moral hazard
- D) Speculative risk
**Answer: C** — Dishonesty or fraud intended to benefit from insurance is moral hazard; morale hazard is carelessness or indifference, not intentional dishonesty.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q201 | 2.0 | Medium
For a risk to be insurable, the loss must be all of the following EXCEPT:
- A) Due to chance and outside the insured's control
- B) Definite and measurable
- C) Catastrophic to the insurer
- D) Part of a large homogeneous group
**Answer: C** — An insurable risk must NOT be catastrophic to the insurer; all others (due to chance, definite, large group) are required elements.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q202 | 2.0 | Medium
The tendency of higher-risk individuals to seek and retain insurance more than lower-risk individuals is called:
- A) The law of large numbers
- B) Adverse selection
- C) Reinsurance
- D) Morale hazard
**Answer: B** — Adverse selection is the tendency of higher-risk people to disproportionately seek insurance; underwriting exists to manage it.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q203 | 2.0 | Medium
A ceding company transfers a portion of its catastrophic exposure to another insurer. This arrangement is called:
- A) Reinsurance
- B) Self-insurance
- C) Risk retention
- D) A risk purchasing group
**Answer: A** — Reinsurance is insurers insuring insurers; the ceding company transfers part of its risk to the reinsurer.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### Q204 | 2.0 | Medium
A consumer reasonably believes an agent has authority to commit the insurer based on the insurer's own actions and representations. When the agent acts beyond her actual express authority and the insurer is bound, the authority at issue is:
- A) Implied authority
- B) Express authority
- C) Apparent authority
- D) Fiduciary authority
**Answer: C** — Apparent authority arises when the insurer creates the appearance of authority; a reasonable consumer would believe the agent has it, and the insurer can be bound.
*Citation: Outline 2.3*

### Q205 | 2.0 | Medium
An insurance policy is a unilateral contract because:
- A) Only the insured makes promises
- B) Only the insurer makes a legally enforceable promise
- C) Neither party can sue the other
- D) Both parties exchange equal value
**Answer: B** — Unilateral means only one party (the insurer) makes a legally enforceable promise; the insured's premium payments are not legally compelled.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q206 | 2.0 | Medium
An insured knowingly conceals a material fact about his health when applying for coverage. This is:
- A) Misrepresentation
- B) Concealment
- C) Warranty breach
- D) Estoppel
**Answer: B** — Concealment is the intentional withholding of a known material fact; misrepresentation is stating something false.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q207 | 2.0 | Medium
The legal principle that prevents a party from reclaiming a right it previously waived is:
- A) Subrogation
- B) Indemnity
- C) Estoppel
- D) Adhesion
**Answer: C** — Estoppel bars the party from reasserting a right it has waived; waiver is the voluntary surrender, and estoppel is the legal consequence.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q208 | 2.0 | Medium
An employer group plan covers each worker individually and is not transferable by the worker to someone else. This characteristic is that the contract is:
- A) Conditional
- B) Personal
- C) Aleatory
- D) Unilateral
**Answer: B** — Insurance is a personal contract — it covers a specific person and is not assignable without the insurer's consent.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q209 | 2.0 | Medium
A Connecticut insurer sells a policy to a Massachusetts resident. In Massachusetts, this insurer is:
- A) A domestic insurer
- B) A foreign insurer
- C) An alien insurer
- D) A nonadmitted insurer by definition
**Answer: B** — A company incorporated in another U.S. state is foreign in Massachusetts; domestic means incorporated in this state; alien means incorporated outside the U.S.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### Q210 | 2.0 | Medium
Insurance coverage is conditioned on the insured paying premiums and filing proper proof of loss. This characteristic makes an insurance policy a:
- A) Unilateral contract
- B) Personal contract
- C) Conditional contract
- D) Aleatory contract
**Answer: C** — Conditional means the insurer pays only if the insured satisfies conditions (premium paid, proof of loss filed).
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q211 | 2.0 | Medium
Which of the following best illustrates the concept of utmost good faith?
- A) The insurer investigates all claims aggressively
- B) Both parties rely on each other's honesty — the insurer trusts the application; the insured trusts the policy
- C) The insurer's obligation to pay claims promptly without requiring excessive documentation
- D) The insurer drafts all policy language unilaterally
**Answer: B** — Utmost good faith requires honest dealing from both sides; neither party exploits information the other lacks.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q212 | 2.0 | Hard
A warranty in an insurance application differs from a representation in that a warranty:
- A) Is believed true to the best of the applicant's knowledge
- B) Is guaranteed absolutely true; any breach voids the policy regardless of materiality
- C) Is protected by the 2-year incontestability rule
- D) Is a statement about the future only
**Answer: B** — Warranties are guaranteed absolutely true; representations are believed true to the best of the applicant's knowledge and belief.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q213 | 2.0 | Hard
All of the following are elements of a legally enforceable insurance contract EXCEPT:
- A) Offer and acceptance
- B) Consideration
- C) A certificate of authority filed with the Commissioner
- D) Legal purpose
**Answer: C** — The four required elements are offer and acceptance, consideration, competent parties, and legal purpose; a certificate of authority is a regulatory licensing requirement, not an element of contract formation.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q214 | 2.0 | Hard
Under the doctrine of reasonable expectations, ambiguous policy language is resolved:
- A) In the insurer's favor because it drafted the contract
- B) In the insured's favor — consistent with what a buyer could fairly believe was covered
- C) By the Commissioner in a public hearing
- D) Against the party who first raised the ambiguity
**Answer: B** — The adhesion doctrine resolves ambiguities against the insurer; the related reasonable expectations doctrine protects the coverage a buyer could fairly expect.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### Q215 | 2.0 | Hard
A stock insurer issues a nonparticipating policy. This means the policyholder:
- A) May receive dividends if the insurer profits
- B) Has voting rights at the annual meeting
- C) Receives no dividends from the insurer's surplus
- D) Owns shares in the insurer
**Answer: C** — Stock companies are owned by stockholders and traditionally issue nonparticipating policies; policyholders receive no share of profits.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### Q216 | 2.0 | Hard
The consideration clause of a health insurance contract identifies the applicant's consideration as:
- A) The premium alone
- B) The applicant's statements in the application plus the first premium
- C) The promise to pay future premiums
- D) Insurable interest and consent
**Answer: B** — The consideration clause identifies the exchange: the applicant's statements plus the first premium, in return for the insurer's promise to pay.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*
