### Q066 | 7.0 | Easy
In group health insurance, the single document issued to the employer is the master contract, while each covered employee receives:
- A) An individual policy
- B) A certificate of coverage
- C) A rider to the master policy
- D) A copy of the master contract
**Answer: B** — One master contract goes to the group sponsor; employees are certificate holders, not policyowners.
*Citation: Outline 7.1*

### Q067 | 7.0 | Easy
Group coverage is generally cheaper and available regardless of an individual's health because the insurer:
- A) Underwrites each employee separately
- B) Does no individual underwriting — the group is underwritten as a unit
- C) Caps benefits at a low level
- D) Excludes anyone with prior claims
**Answer: B** — No individual underwriting is performed; the group is rated as a whole, which is why health status doesn't bar an employee.
*Citation: Outline 7.1*

### Q068 | 7.0 | Easy
Federal COBRA continuation rights apply to employers with at least:
- A) 15 employees
- B) 20 employees
- C) 50 employees
- D) 100 employees
**Answer: B** — COBRA's threshold is 20+ employees; Massachusetts covers smaller groups through its own Mini-COBRA statute.
*Citation: COBRA*

### Q069 | 7.0 | Easy
Under M.G.L. c. 175 § 110D, an insured who leaves the group keeps coverage automatically for:
- A) 31 days
- B) 39 weeks
- C) 18 months
- D) 60 days
**Answer: A** — Leaving the group for any reason continues coverage automatically for 31 days, unless similar benefits are available elsewhere.
*Citation: 175:110D*

### Q070 | 7.0 | Easy
Massachusetts continuation after an involuntary layoff or the death of the member runs for up to:
- A) 31 days
- B) 18 months
- C) 39 weeks
- D) 36 months
**Answer: C** — Section 110G provides a 39-week continuation clock measured in weeks, not months.
*Citation: 175:110G*

### Q071 | 7.0 | Easy
To be an eligible group for insurance purposes, the group must:
- A) Have at least 100 members
- B) Exist for a purpose other than buying insurance
- C) Be incorporated in Massachusetts
- D) Be a labor union only
**Answer: B** — Eligible groups must exist for a purpose other than obtaining insurance — primarily employment-related groups.
*Citation: Outlines 7.2–7.3*

### Q072 | 7.0 | Easy
Under M.G.L. c. 176J, an "eligible small business" is one with an eligible-employee count of:
- A) 1 to 25
- B) 1 to 50
- C) 2 to 100
- D) 1 to 10
**Answer: B** — Massachusetts defines the merged-market small employer as 1 to 50 eligible employees.
*Citation: 176J:1*

### Q073 | 7.0 | Easy
The federal Pregnancy Discrimination Act requires equal treatment of pregnancy in health plans of employers with at least:
- A) 15 employees
- B) 20 employees
- C) 25 employees
- D) 50 employees
**Answer: A** — Employers with 15+ employees must treat pregnancy and childbirth the same as any other medical condition.
*Citation: Outline 7.6*

### Q074 | 7.0 | Easy
A qualified beneficiary's COBRA election period runs for:
- A) 30 days from the qualifying event
- B) 60 days from the later of the qualifying event or the COBRA notice
- C) 90 days from termination
- D) 31 days from the loss of coverage
**Answer: B** — The election period is 60 days from the later of the qualifying event or the COBRA notice.
*Citation: COBRA*

### Q075 | 7.0 | Medium
A worker is covered as an employee under her own plan and as a dependent under her spouse's plan. For her own claims, coordination of benefits makes primary:
- A) Her spouse's plan
- B) The plan covering her as an employee
- C) Whichever plan has the lower deductible
- D) Both plans equally
**Answer: B** — A plan covering a person as an employee pays primary; the plan covering them as a dependent pays secondary.
*Citation: 211 CMR 38.01–.08*

### Q076 | 7.0 | Medium
Priya (born September 12) and Daniel (born April 5) each cover their son under their Worcester employers' plans. Which plan pays the son's claims first?
- A) Priya's, because mothers' plans are primary
- B) Daniel's, because his birthday falls earlier in the calendar year
- C) Whichever plan is older
- D) The plan with the higher benefit limit
**Answer: B** — The COB birthday rule looks to the parent whose birthday falls earlier in the calendar year, not the older parent.
*Citation: 211 CMR 38*

### Q077 | 7.0 | Medium
A Springfield employer switches group carriers mid-year. Under the no-loss no-gain principle, an employee who already met half her deductible:
- A) Must start a new deductible from zero
- B) Carries the satisfied deductible and coinsurance credit to the new plan
- C) Loses coverage for any ongoing claim
- D) Must re-satisfy a probationary period
**Answer: B** — Deductible and coinsurance credits already satisfied carry over, and ongoing claims transfer without fresh probationary exclusions.
*Citation: Outline 7.4*

### Q078 | 7.0 | Medium
Gerald is totally disabled by a covered condition on the day his Lowell employer's group plan terminates. The extension-of-benefits provision means he:
- A) Loses all coverage at termination
- B) Continues to receive benefits for that disabling condition for a limited extension period
- C) Must convert to an individual policy immediately
- D) Receives benefits for any new condition that arises
**Answer: B** — An employee totally disabled at termination keeps benefits for that disabling condition for a limited extension period.
*Citation: Outline 7.4*

### Q079 | 7.0 | Medium
Nathan and Olivia of Quincy divorce, and the judgment says nothing about health insurance. Olivia, covered as Nathan's spouse, under M.G.L. c. 175 § 110I:
- A) Loses coverage on the judgment date
- B) Remains eligible under Nathan's plan without additional premium or examination, until either remarries
- C) Must elect COBRA within 60 days
- D) Pays 102% of the group premium to stay covered
**Answer: B** — The former spouse stays eligible as if the judgment had not entered — no extra premium, no exam — until the remarriage of either party.
*Citation: 175:110I*

### Q080 | 7.0 | Medium
Marcus is laid off in Fall River and starts 39-week continuation under § 110G. His continuation ends earlier if he:
- A) Finds new employment of any kind, whether or not a group plan is offered
- B) Becomes eligible under another group plan
- C) Turns 65
- D) Moves out of Massachusetts
**Answer: B** — The 39 weeks ends sooner if he becomes eligible under another group plan, and can never exceed his most recent period of coverage.
*Citation: 175:110G*

### Q081 | 7.0 | Medium
Tanya is terminated from her Brockton job for ordinary performance reasons. Under COBRA she may continue coverage for ___ at up to ___ of the group premium.
- A) 36 months; 100%
- B) 18 months; 102%
- C) 29 months; 150%
- D) 12 months; 102%
**Answer: B** — Termination other than for gross misconduct is an 18-month event, with premium up to 102% of the group rate.
*Citation: COBRA*

### Q082 | 7.0 | Medium
The Okafor family's daughter turns 26 and loses dependent status under her father's plan. The COBRA continuation period available to her is:
- A) 18 months
- B) 29 months
- C) 36 months
- D) 39 weeks
**Answer: C** — A child aging out of dependent status is a 36-month qualifying event, along with death, divorce, and Medicare entitlement.
*Citation: COBRA*

### Q083 | 7.0 | Medium
When a Pittsfield small-group member's Mini-COBRA continuation expires, the carrier must offer enrollment in a conversion nongroup plan during the:
- A) 31-day window after expiration
- B) 60-day window after expiration
- C) 180-day period ending on the expiration date
- D) Next annual open enrollment
**Answer: C** — The conversion nongroup plan must be offered during the 180-day period ending on the expiration date.
*Citation: c. 176J § 9*

### Q084 | 7.0 | Medium

- A) The state where the employer is incorporated
- B) The state where the master policy is delivered

### Q085 | 7.0 | Medium
A large employer's group premiums reflect that group's own claims history. This pricing method is:
- A) Community rating
- B) Experience rating
- C) Modified community rating
- D) Manual rating only
**Answer: B** — Large groups are typically experience rated, with premiums driven by the group's own claims; community rating prices a broad pool the same.
*Citation: Outline 7.1*

### Q086 | 7.0 | Medium

- A) Medicare pays primary and the group plan is secondary
- B) The group plan pays primary and Medicare is secondary

- D) Only Medicare pays

### Q088 | 7.0 | Medium
A Massachusetts small-group plan renews at the employer's option except for statutory causes. Those causes include:
- A) The group filing a grievance
- B) Nonpayment, fraud, or market withdrawal

### Q089 | 7.0 | Hard
Under M.G.L. c. 175 § 110G, the duration of continuation coverage available after an involuntary layoff is measured in:
- A) 39 months

### Q090 | 7.0 | Hard
Under § 110I, after a divorce the former spouse's continuation has:
- A) A fixed 18-month limit
- B) A fixed 36-month limit
- C) No fixed clock — it runs to the remarriage of either party or as the judgment provides
- D) A 39-week limit
**Answer: C** — Unlike the other continuation rules, 110I has no fixed clock; it lasts until either remarries or as the judgment provides, at no extra premium.
*Citation: 175:110I*

### Q091 | 7.0 | Hard
All of the following are verified features of Massachusetts Mini-COBRA EXCEPT:
- A) Coverage must be identical to what similarly situated active members receive
- B) It may not be conditioned on evidence of insurability
- C) A disabled individual may be charged 150% of premium after month 18
- D) It applies to the employer's stand-alone dental and vision plans
**Answer: D** — Mini-COBRA does not apply to dental or vision plans; identical coverage, no evidence of insurability, and the 150% disability charge are all required features.
*Citation: c. 176J § 9*

### Q092 | 7.0 | Hard
Raymond is on COBRA after a reduction in hours and is determined disabled under the Social Security definition within the first 60 days of continuation. His maximum continuation extends to ___, during which premium may reach ___.
- A) 18 months; 102%
- B) 29 months; 150%
- C) 36 months; 102%
- D) 29 months; 102%
**Answer: B** — Disability during the first 60 days of an 18-month event extends continuation to 29 months, with premium up to 150% (not the standard 102%) during the disability-extension months.
*Citation: COBRA*

### Q093 | 7.0 | Hard
In Massachusetts merged-market small-group rating, the permitted age factor is capped at a ratio of ___ and the area factor must fall between ___.
- A) 3:1; 0.5 and 1.5
- B) 2:1; 0.8 and 1.2
- C) 2:1; 0.9 and 1.1
- D) 5:1; 0.7 and 1.3
**Answer: B** — Only enumerated factors are allowed: age capped at a 2:1 ratio and an area factor between 0.8 and 1.2, with health status and claims experience prohibited.
*Citation: 211 CMR 66.07*

### Q094 | 7.0 | Hard
Renata voluntarily quits her Cambridge job — no layoff, no death. The continuation that applies to her, and its length, is:
- A) Section 110G — 39 weeks
- B) Section 110D — 31 days, automatically
- C) Section 110I — until remarriage
- D) None — voluntary departures get no continuation
**Answer: B** — A voluntary departure triggers 110D's automatic 31 days; 110G's 39 weeks applies only to involuntary layoff or the member's death.
*Citation: 175:110D*

### Q095 | 7.0 | Hard
Because it cannot underwrite individuals, a group insurer evaluates all of the following EXCEPT:
- A) The group's industry and turnover (persistency)
- B) The employer's administrative capability
- C) Each individual employee's personal medical history
- D) The group's size and prior claims experience
**Answer: C** — The insurer underwrites the group's characteristics, not individuals' personal medical histories, since there is no individual underwriting.
*Citation: Outline 7.4*
