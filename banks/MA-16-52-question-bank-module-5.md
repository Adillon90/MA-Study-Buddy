### Q217 | 5.0 | Easy
The elimination period in a disability income policy is best described as:
- A) The benefit period
- B) A time deductible — the waiting period between onset of disability and the start of benefits
- C) The period during which premiums are waived
- D) The maximum period benefits will be paid
**Answer: B** — The elimination period is a time deductible; no benefits accrue during it, and a longer elimination period means a lower premium.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q218 | 5.0 | Easy
An "any occupation" definition of disability means the insured is unable to perform the duties of:
- A) Their specific specialty
- B) Any occupation for which they are reasonably suited by education, training, and experience
- C) Any occupation whatsoever, even unskilled labor
- D) Their most recent job only
**Answer: B** — Any-occ measures against a reasonable fit with the insured's background — stricter than own-occ, which measures only against the insured's own occupation.
*Citation: 211 CMR 42.05(1)(g); Outline 5.1*

### Q219 | 5.0 | Easy
Social Security disability benefits begin with which month of disability?
- A) The 1st month
- B) The 3rd month
- C) The 5th month
- D) The 6th month
**Answer: D** — There is a 5-month waiting period; benefits begin with the 6th month of qualifying disability.
*Citation: Outline 5.6*

### Q220 | 5.0 | Easy
DI insurers typically cap individual disability income benefits at what percentage of the insured's earned income?
- A) 40–50%
- B) 60–70%
- C) 80–90%
- D) 100%
**Answer: B** — Benefits are capped around 60–70% of earned income to preserve the financial incentive to return to work.
*Citation: Outline 5.3*

### Q221 | 5.0 | Easy
Which business DI product funds the buyout of a totally disabled owner's interest per the terms of the buy-sell agreement?
- A) Key person disability income
- B) Business overhead expense
- C) Disability buy-sell
- D) Disability reducing term
**Answer: C** — Disability buy-sell funds the purchase of the disabled owner's interest, usually as a lump sum after a long elimination period.

### Q222 | 5.0 | Easy
A nonoccupational disability policy excludes:
- A) Sickness arising outside work hours
- B) On-the-job disabilities (covered instead by workers compensation)
- C) Disabilities lasting fewer than 90 days
- D) All injuries regardless of origin
**Answer: B** — Nonoccupational policies exclude workplace disabilities, which belong to workers comp; occupational policies cover 24 hours a day.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q223 | 5.0 | Easy
The rider that inflates disability income benefits during a claim to keep pace with rising costs is the:
- A) Future increase option
- B) COLA rider
- C) Social insurance supplement
- D) Residual rider
**Answer: B** — The COLA (cost-of-living adjustment) rider inflates benefits during a claim; the FIO rider allows buying more coverage later without medical underwriting.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q224 | 5.0 | Easy
Workers compensation provides disability income benefits on a ___ basis, as the employee's ___ against the employer.
- A) Fault-based; primary remedy
- B) No-fault; exclusive remedy
- C) No-fault; secondary remedy
- D) Fault-based; exclusive remedy
**Answer: B** — Workers comp is no-fault and is the employee's exclusive remedy against the employer for occupational injuries and diseases.
*Citation: Outline 5.7*

### Q225 | 5.0 | Medium
Dr. Ferreira, a Worcester neurosurgeon, develops essential tremor ending his surgical career. Under an own-occupation policy, he is totally disabled even though he:
- A) Can still perform any job
- B) Takes a consulting position at a lower income
- C) Refuses all rehabilitation
- D) Does not have Social Security approval
**Answer: B** — Own-occ measures only against the insured's own occupation's duties; other employment, even at reduced income, does not defeat the claim.
*Citation: Outline 5.1*

### Q226 | 5.0 | Medium
A disability policy has a 180-day elimination period. The insured becomes disabled January 1. Approximately when does the first benefit check arrive?
- A) July 1 (day 181)
- B) Early August (around day 210)
- C) January 31
- D) February 1 (day 31)
**Answer: B** — After the 180-day elimination, benefits accrue from day 181 — but because they are paid in arrears, the first check arrives about a month later, approximately day 210.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q227 | 5.0 | Medium
After 90 days of total disability, Anita's premium is waived for the duration of her disability. The premiums she paid during those 90 qualifying days are:
- A) Forfeited
- B) Usually refunded when the waiver-of-premium kicks in
- C) Applied toward future premiums
- D) Applied to extend the benefit period
**Answer: B** — The waiver-of-premium provision typically refunds premiums paid during the qualifying period once the waiver takes effect.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q228 | 5.0 | Medium
Miguel's income drops 35% because of a partial disability — he can still work but not at full capacity. His residual disability benefit pays:
- A) A flat 50% of his total benefit
- B) 35% of his total benefit, proportional to his income loss
- C) Nothing — he must be totally unable to work
- D) The full total-disability benefit for 6 months
**Answer: B** — Residual benefits are proportional to earned income loss; a 35% income drop produces a 35% benefit — the modern alternative to a flat partial benefit.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q229 | 5.0 | Medium
The flat partial disability benefit typically pays ___ of the total benefit for a limited time when the insured can perform some but not all duties.
- A) 25%
- B) 50%
- C) 75%
- D) The same as total disability
**Answer: B** — A partial disability benefit is typically a flat 50% of the total benefit for a limited period, compared to residual which is income-loss-proportional.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q230 | 5.0 | Medium
A rider on Marcus's disability policy allows him to purchase additional monthly benefit at predetermined option dates as his income grows, without providing evidence of good health. This rider is called the:
- A) COLA rider
- B) Future Increase Option
- C) Social Insurance Supplement
- D) Waiver of premium
**Answer: B** — The FIO preserves the right to buy more coverage later without medical underwriting; financial underwriting still applies.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q231 | 5.0 | Medium
A Social Insurance Supplement (SIS) rider reduces dollar-for-dollar once:
- A) The benefit period expires
- B) Social Security or other social insurance starts paying
- C) The insured returns to work part-time
- D) The elimination period runs
**Answer: B** — The SIS provides extra income during the period before social insurance pays; it reduces when those benefits begin.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q232 | 5.0 | Medium
Key person disability income insurance is owned and paid for by the business. Benefits are received by:
- A) The disabled key employee
- B) The business
- C) The buy-sell trust
- D) The key employee's beneficiary
**Answer: B** — The business owns, pays for, and receives key person DI benefits to replace profits or cover the cost of a replacement while the key employee is disabled.

### Q233 | 5.0 | Medium
Business overhead expense (BOE) insurance reimburses which of the following while the owner is disabled?
- A) The owner's lost salary and living expenses
- B) Rent, utilities, and employee salaries

### Q234 | 5.0 | Medium
Disability reducing term insurance is designed to pay:

### Q235 | 5.0 | Medium
A short-term group disability policy typically features a benefit period measured in:
- A) Years to age 65
- B) Months or up to one year
- C) 5 to 10 years
- D) A fixed $50,000 lump sum
**Answer: B** — STD group plans have brief elimination periods (0–14 days) and benefit periods of weeks to a year; LTD plans carry long eliminations and benefit periods of years to age 65.

### Q236 | 5.0 | Hard
A Lowell attorney has an own-occupation policy that converts to any-occupation after 2 years. After 18 months of disability she can no longer practice law but could manage a legal staffing firm. During the first 2 years she is:
- A) Not disabled — she can perform any occupation
- B) Totally disabled — own-occ applies and she cannot perform her own occupation's duties
- C) Partially disabled under the residual rider
- D) Subject to the any-occ standard immediately
**Answer: B** — During the own-occ period, the test is only whether she can do her own occupation's duties — other available work is irrelevant until the policy converts to any-occ.
*Citation: Outline 5.1*

### Q237 | 5.0 | Hard
Presumptive disability provisions deem total disability upon all of the following EXCEPT:
- A) Loss of sight in both eyes
- B) Loss of use of two limbs
- C) Loss of speech
- D) Loss of sight in one eye
**Answer: D** — Presumptive triggers are sight in both eyes, hearing, speech, and use of two limbs; sight in one eye alone is not a listed trigger.
*Citation: Outline 5.1*

### Q238 | 5.0 | Hard
Social Security's disability definition — "unable to engage in any substantial gainful activity" — is stricter than both own-occ and any-occ DI definitions because it:
- A) Uses a shorter waiting period
- B) Requires the impairment to be expected to last at least 12 months or result in death, and tests against any gainful work — not a reasonable fit
- C) Allows benefits for partial income loss
- D) Applies only to physical impairments
**Answer: B** — The SSA standard is the strictest — any gainful activity, 12-month duration or death — compared to any-occ's "reasonably suited" standard.

### Q239 | 5.0 | Hard
An underwriter is evaluating an application for individual DI coverage from a Pittsfield electrician. The primary rating factor that sets both the premium and the benefit ceiling is:
- A) The applicant's age
- B) The applicant's occupation, classified by duties rather than title

### Q240 | 5.0 | Hard

- A) Key person disability income

### Q241 | 5.0 | Hard
A disability income policy requires the insured to be under the regular care of a physician as a condition of continued benefits. Under which circumstance is this requirement typically waived?
- A) After benefits have been paid continuously for 12 months
- B) When further medical care would be of no benefit to the insured's condition
- C) When the insured has satisfied the waiver-of-premium qualifying period
- D) When the insured is receiving Social Security disability benefits
**Answer: B** — The physician-care requirement is waived when further care would be of no benefit — acknowledging that some conditions (e.g., permanent limb loss) cannot be improved by ongoing treatment.
*Citation: Outline 5.2*

### Q242 | 5.0 | Hard
An insurer evaluating a marginal DI applicant may use all of the following as issuance alternatives EXCEPT:
- A) Rated premium
- B) Longer elimination period
- C) Shorter benefit period
- D) Unlimited benefit period at no extra charge
**Answer: D** — Issuance alternatives for marginal risks include a rated premium, longer elimination, shorter benefit period, or an exclusion rider — never expanded benefits at no extra cost.
*Citation: Outline 5.3*
