### Q001 | Module 4.0 | Easy
Maria pays her individual health insurance premium weekly. How many days is her grace period?
- A) 7 days
- B) 10 days
- C) 31 days
- D) 45 days
**Answer: A** — Grace periods are 7 days for weekly premium policies, 10 for monthly, 31 for all other modes.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(3)*

### Q002 | Module 4.0 | Easy
Under the Entire Contract provision, who has the authority to change a policy?
- A) Any appointed agent
- B) An executive officer of the insurer, with the change endorsed on or attached to the policy
- C) The producer who sold the policy, with the insured's consent
- D) The Commissioner of Insurance
**Answer: B** — No agent may change the policy or waive its provisions; only an executive officer can approve changes.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(1)*

### Q003 | Module 4.0 | Medium
Dan's lapsed policy required a reinstatement application, and he received a conditional receipt on April 1. The insurer never responds. When is the policy automatically reinstated?
- A) April 11
- B) April 16
- C) May 16
- D) Never, without written approval
**Answer: C** — Absent written disapproval, the policy reinstates automatically on the 45th day after the conditional receipt.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(4)*

### Q004 | Module 4.0 | Medium
Eight days after his policy was reinstated, Theo comes down with pneumonia. Six days later he breaks his arm in a fall. Which losses are covered?
- A) Both
- B) Neither
- C) The broken arm only
- D) The pneumonia only
**Answer: C** — A reinstated policy covers accidents immediately but sickness only if it begins more than 10 days after reinstatement. The pneumonia began on day 8.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(4)*

### Q005 | Module 4.0 | Easy
Written notice of claim must generally be given to the insurer within how many days of a covered loss?
- A) 10
- B) 20
- C) 31
- D) 90
**Answer: B** — 20 days, or as soon as reasonably possible.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(5)*

### Q006 | Module 4.0 | Medium
An insurer receives notice of claim but never sends claim forms. After how many days may the claimant satisfy proof-of-loss requirements with her own written statement of the occurrence, character, and extent of loss?
- A) 10 days
- B) 15 days
- C) 45 days
- D) 90 days
**Answer: B** — If forms aren't furnished within 15 days of notice, written proof in the claimant's own form satisfies the policy.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(6)*

### Q007 | Module 4.0 | Medium
Proof of loss is generally due within 90 days. If it wasn't reasonably possible to file on time, what is the absolute outer limit (absent legal incapacity)?
- A) 6 months
- B) 1 year
- C) 2 years
- D) 3 years
**Answer: B** — Late proof is excused when filing wasn't reasonably possible, but never later than 1 year unless the claimant lacks legal capacity.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(7)*

### Q008 | Module 4.0 | Hard
A disability income policy pays benefits monthly. The insured dies with two months of accrued, unpaid benefits and no effective beneficiary designation. The accrued benefits are payable to:
- A) The state unclaimed property fund
- B) The insured's estate
- C) The insured's nearest relative automatically
- D) No one; they lapse at death
**Answer: B** — With no effective beneficiary designation, indemnity for loss of life and accrued unpaid benefits are payable to the insured's estate (the insurer may optionally pay up to $1,000 to an equitably entitled relative under a facility-of-payment clause).
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(9)*

### Q009 | Module 4.0 | Easy
Who pays for physical examinations the insurer requires while a claim is pending?
- A) The insured
- B) The insurer
- C) Split equally
- D) The treating physician
**Answer: B** — The insurer may examine the insured as often as reasonably required during a claim, at its own expense.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(10)*

### Q010 | Module 4.0 | Medium
What is the earliest and latest a policyholder may bring a lawsuit on the policy?
- A) No earlier than 60 days after proof of loss; no later than 3 years after proof was required
- B) Immediately; within 1 year
- C) No earlier than 90 days; no later than 2 years
- D) No earlier than 30 days; no later than 6 years
**Answer: A** — Legal actions: 60-day minimum wait after furnishing proof of loss, 3-year outer limit from when proof was required.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(11)*

### Q011 | Module 4.0 | Medium
A Massachusetts insurer receives notice of a hospital expense claim on June 1 and neither pays nor explains the delay in writing. Interest begins accruing on the benefits at what point and rate?
- A) July 1, at the prime rate
- B) July 16, at 1% per month
- C) 45 days after notice, at 1.5% per month (max 18% per year)
- D) 90 days after notice, at 18% per month
**Answer: C** — MA's prompt-pay rule: pay or explain within 45 days of notice, or owe interest at 1.5%/month capped at 18%/year, accruing from day 45.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(4)(c)*

### Q012 | Module 4.0 | Medium
A family health policy must allow coverage of an incapacitated adult child beyond the limiting age if proof of incapacity is provided within how many days of when coverage would otherwise end?
- A) 10
- B) 26
- C) 31
- D) 60
**Answer: C** — 31 days. (Separately, dependent children are covered to age 26.)
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(2)(a)(3)*

### Q013 | Module 4.0 | Hard
An insurer denies a claim based on a pre-existing condition. Along with the denial, Massachusetts law requires the insurer to send the insured:
- A) A premium refund
- B) Documented evidence of actual treatment or observation of that condition
- C) An offer of arbitration
- D) Nothing additional; the denial letter suffices
**Answer: B** — Denials based on pre-existing conditions must include documented evidence of specific instances of actual treatment or observation, except confidential cases.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(2)(a)(2A)*

### Q014 | Module 4.0 | Medium
Rank these renewability provisions from MOST to LEAST favorable to the insured:
- A) Guaranteed renewable → noncancelable → conditionally renewable → insurer's option
- B) Noncancelable → guaranteed renewable → conditionally renewable → insurer's option
- C) Noncancelable → conditionally renewable → guaranteed renewable → insurer's option
- D) Insurer's option → conditionally renewable → guaranteed renewable → noncancelable
**Answer: B** — Noncancelable locks both renewal and premium; guaranteed renewable locks renewal but allows class-wide premium increases; conditionally renewable allows nonrenewal for stated reasons; optionally renewable lets the insurer decline for any reason at renewal.
*Citation: Series 16-52 outline § 4.3*

### Q015 | Module 4.0 | Easy
Under M.G.L. c. 175 § 187H, a policyowner who returns the policy within the free look period receives:
- A) A pro-rata premium refund
- B) A full premium refund, with the policy void from the beginning
- C) Credit toward a replacement policy
- D) 90% of premium paid
**Answer: B** — Cancel within 10 days of delivery for any reason; the policy is void from inception and all premium is refunded.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 187H*

### Q016 | Module 4.0 | Medium
The insured misstated her age on the application; the error is discovered at claim time. Under the optional Misstatement of Age provision, the insurer will:
- A) Void the policy
- B) Deny the claim
- C) Pay the benefits the premium would have purchased at her correct age
- D) Pay full benefits but raise future premiums
**Answer: C** — Benefits are adjusted to what the premium paid would have bought at the true age. The policy is not voided.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(b)(2)*

### Q017 | Module 4.0 | Hard
A roofer buys a disability policy, later becomes an accountant, then is injured. Under the optional Change of Occupation provision, the insurer:
- A) Reduces benefits to the more hazardous class rate
- B) Reduces the premium and refunds the pro-rata excess, since accounting is less hazardous
- C) Voids the policy for failure to notify
- D) Pays nothing until reclassification
**Answer: B** — Moving to a LESS hazardous occupation triggers a premium reduction and refund of excess; moving to a MORE hazardous one reduces benefits to what the premium buys at the riskier rate.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(b)(1)*

### Q018 | Module 1.0 | Easy
How many hours of pre-licensing education does Massachusetts require before sitting for the producer exam?
- A) 20
- B) 40
- C) 52
- D) None
**Answer: D** — MA eliminated pre-licensing coursework (the Division recommends, but does not require, a study course).
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162L*

### Q019 | Module 1.0 | Medium
A producer's license lapsed 7 months ago over an unpaid renewal fee. To reinstate, she must:
- A) Retake the exam and pay the fee
- B) Pay double the unpaid renewal fee; no exam required
- C) Complete 60 CE hours and reapply as new
- D) Wait until 12 months have passed
**Answer: B** — Within 12 months of the due date, reinstatement requires no exam but carries a penalty of double the unpaid renewal fee.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162M(c)*

### Q020 | Module 1.0 | Easy
A licensee moves from Worcester to Springfield. She must notify the Commissioner of her address change within:
- A) 10 days
- B) 15 days
- C) 30 days
- D) 60 days
**Answer: C** — 30 days.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162M(f)*

### Q021 | Module 1.0 | Medium
An insurer executes an agency contract with a producer on September 10; she submits her first application September 12. The insurer's notice of appointment is due by:
- A) September 17
- B) September 25
- C) October 10
- D) Before any policy is delivered
**Answer: B** — The notice is due within 15 days of the contract execution or first application — measured from the earlier event, September 10.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162S(b)*

### Q022 | Module 1.0 | Medium
The Commissioner refuses to renew a producer's license. To get a hearing, the producer must make written demand within:
- A) 10 days
- B) 30 days
- C) 60 days
- D) 90 days
**Answer: B** — Written demand within 30 days; the hearing is held under Chapter 30A.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162R(b)*

### Q023 | Module 1.0 | Hard
All of the following are grounds for license suspension or revocation under M.G.L. c. 175 § 162R EXCEPT:
- A) Using notes to complete the licensing examination
- B) Failing to comply with a child support order
- C) Having a license suspended in another state
- D) Declining to be appointed by a particular insurer
**Answer: D** — Appointment decisions are business choices. Exam cheating, child support noncompliance, and out-of-state license actions are all among the 14 statutory grounds.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162R(a)*

### Q024 | Module 1.0 | Medium
A producer deposits collected premiums into his personal account and uses them for rent, intending to repay them. Massachusetts law treats this as:
- A) A civil matter between producer and insurer
- B) Larceny — premiums are held in a fiduciary capacity
- C) Permissible if repaid within 30 days
- D) Rebating
**Answer: B** — Converting premiums held in a fiduciary capacity is larceny under 175:176, and misappropriation is also ground #4 for license revocation under 162R.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 176, 162R(a)(4)*

### Q025 | Module 1.0 | Hard
A loan officer was convicted of felony bank fraud in 2019. Before she can legally work in the business of insurance anywhere in the U.S., federal law requires:
- A) A 10-year waiting period
- B) Written consent from an insurance regulator (a 1033 waiver)
- C) Expungement of the conviction
- D) Only state licensing approval
**Answer: B** — 18 USC 1033 prohibits anyone convicted of a felony involving dishonesty or breach of trust from engaging in the business of insurance without a regulator's written consent.
*Citation: 18 USC §§ 1033(e), 1034*
