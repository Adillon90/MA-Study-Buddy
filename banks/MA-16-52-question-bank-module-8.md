### Q255 | 8.0 | Easy
Under a typical dental plan, diagnostic and preventive services (exams, X-rays, cleanings) are covered at approximately:
- A) 50% after a deductible
- B) 80% after a deductible
- C) 100%, often with no deductible
- D) 60% up to an annual maximum
**Answer: C** — Preventive/diagnostic services are at the top of the benefit ladder — ~100% with no deductible — because prevention is cheaper than repair.
*Citation: Outline 8.2*

### Q256 | 8.0 | Easy
A dental plan whose deductible is separate from, and independent of, the employee's major medical deductible is called a:
- A) Combination plan
- B) Integrated plan
- C) Stand-alone dental plan
- D) Nonscheduled plan
**Answer: C** — A stand-alone dental plan has its own deductible; an integrated plan folds dental into the major-medical deductible.
*Citation: Outline 8.3*

### Q257 | 8.0 | Medium
A scheduled dental plan differs from a nonscheduled (comprehensive) plan in that it:
- A) Covers only preventive services
- B) Pays fixed dollar amounts per procedure regardless of the provider's actual charge
- C) Uses usual/reasonable/customary reimbursement
- D) Applies an annual benefit maximum unique to dental
**Answer: B** — Scheduled plans pay a set fee-schedule amount per procedure; nonscheduled plans pay on a URC basis with coinsurance.
*Citation: Outline 8.2*

### Q258 | 8.0 | Hard
A group dental plan uses all of the following to minimize adverse selection EXCEPT:
- A) Waiting periods before major services are covered
- B) Penalizing late enrollees with reduced benefits or longer waits
- C) Annual benefit maximums and tiered coinsurance
- D) Applying an integrated deductible shared with the major medical plan
**Answer: D** — An integrated deductible is a plan design choice, not an adverse selection control; adverse selection is controlled through waiting periods, enrollment restrictions, annual maximums, and tiered cost-sharing.
*Citation: Outline 8.3*
