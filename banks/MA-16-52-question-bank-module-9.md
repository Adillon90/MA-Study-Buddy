### Q096 | 9.0 | Easy
Besides people age 65 and older, Medicare also covers younger people who:
- A) Have any chronic illness
- B) Receive Social Security disability benefits (after 24 months) or have ESRD or ALS
- C) Earn below the federal poverty line
- D) Have exhausted private coverage
**Answer: B** — Medicare reaches under-65 individuals on Social Security disability after 24 months and those with end-stage renal disease or ALS.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q097 | 9.0 | Easy
Medicare Part A is premium-free for a person with:
- A) 20 quarters of covered employment
- B) 40 quarters (10 years) of Medicare-covered employment
- C) Any current employment
- D) A Part B enrollment
**Answer: B** — 40 quarters (10 years) of covered work — one's own or a spouse's — earns premium-free Part A; others may buy in.
*Citation: CMS, Part A*

### Q098 | 9.0 | Easy
After the annual Part B deductible, Original Medicare pays 80% of the approved amount and the patient owes 20%, with:
- A) An annual out-of-pocket cap
- B) No out-of-pocket cap
- C) A lifetime maximum of $50,000
- D) A second deductible at midyear
**Answer: B** — Part B has no out-of-pocket cap under Original Medicare, which is the reason Medigap exists.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q099 | 9.0 | Easy
Massachusetts is one of only three states that design their own Medicare supplement plans rather than using the national lettered plans. The other two are:
- A) New York and California
- B) Minnesota and Wisconsin
- C) Vermont and Maine
- D) Connecticut and Rhode Island
**Answer: B** — Massachusetts, Minnesota, and Wisconsin hold federal waivers and design their own Medigap plans.
*Citation: Outline 9.2*

### Q100 | 9.0 | Easy
The Medicare Initial Enrollment Period lasts:
- A) 3 months
- B) 6 months
- C) 7 months
- D) 12 months
**Answer: C** — The IEP is 7 months: the 3 months before the 65th-birthday month, the birthday month, and the 3 months after.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q101 | 9.0 | Easy
Massachusetts Supplement 1A is identical to Supplement 1 except that 1A does not cover:
- A) The Part A deductible
- B) Skilled nursing facility coinsurance
- C) Foreign travel emergency
- D) The Part B deductible
**Answer: D** — Supplement 1A is Supplement 1 without Part B deductible coverage.
*Citation: 211 CMR 71.08*

### Q102 | 9.0 | Easy
Which of the following is one of the six Activities of Daily Living used in long-term care benefit triggers?
- A) Driving
- B) Managing money
- C) Bathing
- D) Grocery shopping
**Answer: C** — The six ADLs are bathing, dressing, toileting, transferring, continence, and eating.
*Citation: 211 CMR 65.05(1)*

### Q103 | 9.0 | Easy
Long-term care insurance is designed to cover the care Medicare largely does not, namely:
- A) Emergency surgery
- B) Extended custodial, intermediate, and skilled care
- C) Prescription drugs
- D) Physician office visits
**Answer: B** — LTC covers extended custodial, intermediate, and skilled care in facilities or alternatives — the long-term custodial care Medicare excludes.
*Citation: Outline 9.4*

### Q104 | 9.0 | Easy
Medicare Part D provides:
- A) Hospital insurance
- B) Optional private prescription drug coverage
- C) Long-term custodial care
- D) Foreign travel coverage
**Answer: B** — Part D is optional private prescription drug coverage, sold standalone or bundled inside Medicare Advantage.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q105 | 9.0 | Medium
Walter is hospitalized, discharged, and then readmitted 65 days after leaving the hospital. For the second stay, his Part A deductible:
- A) Is waived
- B) Applies again, because a new benefit period began
- C) Applies once per calendar year only
- D) Is cut in half
**Answer: B** — A benefit period ends after 60 consecutive days out of the hospital/SNF, so a readmission on day 65 begins a new benefit period and a new deductible.
*Citation: CMS, Part A*

### Q106 | 9.0 | Medium
Beatrice needs Medicare Part A to cover a skilled nursing facility stay. Coverage requires:
- A) A prior inpatient hospital stay of at least 3 days
- B) Any prior physician visit
- C) Enrollment in a Medigap plan
- D) Exhaustion of lifetime reserve days
**Answer: A** — SNF coverage requires a qualifying 3-day inpatient hospital stay; days 1–20 are then fully covered and 21–100 carry daily coinsurance.
*Citation: CMS, Part A*

### Q107 | 9.0 | Medium
A Part A patient who stays in the hospital beyond 90 days in a benefit period draws on:
- A) A new annual allotment of days
- B) 60 lifetime reserve days, a single pool that is never renewed
- C) Part B outpatient benefits
- D) Skilled nursing coverage
**Answer: B** — Beyond 90 days the patient uses 60 lifetime reserve days — one lifetime pool that never renews, each day carrying a higher coinsurance.
*Citation: CMS, Part A*

### Q108 | 9.0 | Medium
Theodore kept working past 65 with his employer's group plan and is now retiring. He may enroll in Part B penalty-free during a Special Enrollment Period lasting:
- A) 3 months after retirement
- B) 6 months after retirement
- C) 8 months after the employment or coverage ends
- D) Until the next General Enrollment Period
**Answer: C** — The SEP runs 8 months after the employment or group coverage ends, with no late penalty.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q109 | 9.0 | Medium
A person who missed their Initial Enrollment Period may sign up for Medicare during the General Enrollment Period, which runs each year:
- A) October 15 – December 7
- B) January 1 – March 31
- C) April 1 – June 30
- D) The person's birthday month only
**Answer: B** — The General Enrollment Period is January 1 – March 31 each year for those who missed the IEP.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q110 | 9.0 | Medium
A Medicare Advantage (Part C) plan must cover everything Parts A and B cover, and the enrollee:
- A) No longer pays the Part B premium
- B) Still pays the Part B premium
- C) Pays only a Part D premium
- D) Pays nothing further
**Answer: B** — Part C replaces Original Medicare's payment structure but the enrollee still pays the Part B premium.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*

### Q111 | 9.0 | Medium
Margaret became eligible for Medicare in 2018. In Massachusetts she may purchase:
- A) Any national lettered plan A through N
- B) Core, Supplement 1, or Supplement 1A
- C) Core or Supplement 1A only
- D) Supplement 1 only
**Answer: B** — Because she was eligible before January 1, 2020, MACRA does not bar Part B deductible coverage, so all three MA plans are open to her.
*Citation: c. 176K; 211 CMR 71.08; MACRA*

### Q112 | 9.0 | Medium
The Massachusetts Core Plan includes an MA-mandated inpatient mental health benefit of:
- A) 30 days per benefit period
- B) 60 days per calendar year
- C) 120 days per benefit period
- D) Unlimited days
**Answer: B** — Core's mandated benefit is 60 days per calendar year of inpatient mental health care.
*Citation: 211 CMR 71.08*

### Q113 | 9.0 | Medium
Massachusetts Supplement 1 adds all of the following to the Core Plan EXCEPT:
- A) The Part A deductible
- B) Skilled nursing facility coinsurance
- C) Foreign travel emergency
- D) Part B excess charge coverage
**Answer: D** — Massachusetts bars providers from charging Part B excess charges, so MA plans do not offer excess-charge coverage; Supplement 1 does add the Part A deductible, Part B deductible, SNF coinsurance, foreign travel emergency, and expanded inpatient mental health (120 days per benefit period).
*Citation: 211 CMR 71.08*

### Q114 | 9.0 | Medium
A Massachusetts Medigap issuer evaluating a 70-year-old applicant in poor health:
- A) May decline based on health status
- B) May charge a higher premium for the medical condition
- C) Must issue at the community-rated price regardless of health or claims history
- D) May require a genetic test
**Answer: C** — MA Medigap is guaranteed issue at all times and effectively community rated; no issuer may deny, condition, or price based on age, health, claims, or genetic information.
*Citation: 211 CMR 71.10*

### Q115 | 9.0 | Medium
A Massachusetts Medicare supplement policy may impose a pre-existing condition waiting period of:
- A) 6 months
- B) 3 months
- C) None — MA Medigap may contain no pre-existing limitations or waiting periods at all
- D) 12 months for unmanaged conditions
**Answer: C** — MA Medigap policies may not contain any pre-existing condition limitation or waiting period; the national 6-month waits do not apply here.
*Citation: 211 CMR 71.10*

### Q116 | 9.0 | Medium
For a dual-eligible Massachusetts resident needing long-term custodial care, MassHealth:
- A) Pays before Medicare in all cases
- B) Is the payer of last resort for custodial care Medicare excludes, after asset spend-down
- C) Covers nothing for seniors
- D) Replaces Medicare entirely
**Answer: B** — MassHealth pays Medicare premiums and cost-sharing for dual eligibles and is the payer of last resort for long-term custodial care after the person spends down assets.
*Citation: c. 118E*

### Q117 | 9.0 | Medium
In a long-term care policy, the number of waiting days an insured must satisfy before benefits begin is the:
- A) Benefit period
- B) Elimination period
- C) Probationary period
- D) Free-look period
**Answer: B** — The elimination period is the waiting days before benefits start; with the benefit period and daily benefit amount, it sets the price.
*Citation: Outline 9.4*

### Q118 | 9.0 | Medium
Harold buys a long-term care policy and reconsiders. Under the Massachusetts free-look right, he may return it within:
- A) 10 days of delivery
- B) 30 days of delivery, with premium refunded within 30 days
- C) 60 days of delivery
- D) The first premium period
**Answer: B** — The LTC free look is 30 days from delivery for any reason, with the premium refunded within 30 days, and the right must be prominently printed on the first page.
*Citation: c. 176U § 3(e)*

### Q119 | 9.0 | Hard
Estelle turns 65 and enrolls in Part B in May 2026. Which Massachusetts Medicare supplement plans may she buy?
- A) Core, Supplement 1, or Supplement 1A
- B) Core or Supplement 1A only
- C) Supplement 1 only
- D) Any national plan A through N
**Answer: B** — Because she became newly eligible after January 1, 2020, MACRA bars any plan covering the Part B deductible, leaving only Core or Supplement 1A.
*Citation: c. 176K; 211 CMR 71.08; MACRA*

### Q120 | 9.0 | Hard
Medicare Part A covers all of the following EXCEPT:
- A) Inpatient hospital care after the deductible
- B) Hospice for a terminally ill patient electing comfort care
- C) Long-term custodial-only nursing care
- D) Blood after the first 3 pints
**Answer: C** — Part A covers inpatient care, hospice, home health, and blood after the first 3 pints, but custodial-only care is not covered.
*Citation: CMS, Part A*

### Q121 | 9.0 | Hard
A Massachusetts beneficiary compares inpatient mental health coverage. Which correctly contrasts the Core Plan with Supplement 1?
- A) Both cover 60 days per calendar year
- B) Core covers 60 days per calendar year; Supplement 1 expands it to 120 days per benefit period
- C) Core covers 120 days; Supplement 1 covers 60 days
- D) Neither covers inpatient mental health
**Answer: B** — Core's mandated 60 days per calendar year is expanded under Supplement 1 to 120 days per benefit period.
*Citation: 211 CMR 71.08*

### Q122 | 9.0 | Hard
Under the Massachusetts unintentional-lapse protection, before a long-term care policy may lapse for nonpayment, the carrier must give at least 30 days' advance notice to:
- A) The insured only
- B) The insured and a designated third party
- C) MassHealth
- D) The producer of record
**Answer: B** — No LTC policy may lapse unless the carrier notifies both the insured and the designee at least 30 days before the lapse, and at issue the applicant must name a designee or sign a written waiver declining to.
*Citation: 211 CMR 65.10*

### Q123 | 9.0 | Hard
Massachusetts long-term care minimum standards prohibit all of the following EXCEPT:
- A) Conditioning benefits on a prior hospitalization
- B) An elimination period exceeding 365 days
- C) An elimination period of 180 days
- D) Post-claims underwriting
**Answer: C** — A 90-day elimination period is permitted (the cap is 365 days); conditioning benefits on prior hospitalization and post-claims underwriting are prohibited.
*Citation: 211 CMR 65.05*

### Q124 | 9.0 | Hard
A permanently institutionalized person's home is protected from MassHealth liens and recovery if, when purchased, their LTC policy met Massachusetts standards — in practice at least:
- A) 365 days of benefits at any daily amount
- B) 730 days (2 years) of nursing-facility benefits, at least $125 per day, elimination no more than 365 days
- C) 1,095 days of benefits with no elimination period
- D) Lifetime benefits at $200 per day
**Answer: B** — The home is protected if the policy provided at least 730 days of nursing-facility benefits, at least $125 per day, and an elimination period of no more than 365 days.
*Citation: c. 118E § 33; 130 CMR 515.014*

### Q125 | 9.0 | Hard
A producer explains Medicare late-enrollment penalties. Which correctly pairs the penalty with its part?
- A) Part B rises 1% per month; Part D rises 10% per 12 months
- B) Part B rises 10% for each full 12-month period; Part D adds roughly 1% per month
- C) Both rise 10% per year
- D) Neither carries a late penalty
**Answer: B** — The Part B penalty is 10% for each full 12-month period of delay (lifetime); Part D adds roughly 1% per month without creditable drug coverage.
*Citation: Outline 9.1*
