### Q026 | 6.0 | Easy
In an HMO, members are called subscribers rather than insureds because the plan:
- A) Is regulated federally
- B) Contracts to provide care rather than merely reimburse claims
- C) Has no premiums
- D) Issues no contracts
**Answer: B** — Service plans and HMOs deliver care through contracted providers; indemnity insurers reimburse insureds.
*Citation: Outline 6.1*

### Q027 | 6.0 | Easy
A plan that pays fixed dollar amounts per procedure regardless of the provider's actual charge uses:
- A) A benefit schedule
- B) Usual/reasonable/customary reimbursement
- C) Capitation
- D) Coinsurance
**Answer: A** — A schedule pays set amounts; URC pays prevailing area charges.
*Citation: Outline 6.1*

### Q028 | 6.0 | Easy
The defining cost-control feature of an HMO is:
- A) High deductibles
- B) The primary care physician acting as gatekeeper for referrals
- C) Retrospective review only
- D) Unlimited provider choice
**Answer: B** — The PCP coordinates care and controls specialist referrals.
*Citation: c. 176G; Outline 6.2*

### Q029 | 6.0 | Medium
An HMO member is vacationing in Maine and suffers a heart attack. The HMO:
- A) Pays nothing — out of network
- B) Must cover the emergency care without prior authorization
- C) Pays only after the gatekeeper approves retroactively
- D) Pays 50% as out-of-area care
**Answer: B** — Emergency services never require prior authorization, even out of network.
*Citation: PHS 2719A(b); Outline 6.2*

### Q030 | 6.0 | Easy
Blue Cross Blue Shield plans differ from commercial insurers in that BCBS:
- A) Is federally chartered
- B) Contracts with both subscribers and providers, paying providers directly
- C) Cannot offer HMO products
- D) Pays cash benefits to subscribers
**Answer: B** — Service plans contract on both sides and pay providers directly.
*Citation: c. 176A/176B; Outline 6.2*

### Q031 | 6.0 | Medium
A gatekeeper PPO differs from a non-gatekeeper PPO in that the gatekeeper version:
- A) Has no out-of-network coverage
- B) Requires PCP referrals to see specialists
- C) Is prepaid
- D) Has no deductibles
**Answer: B** — Gatekeeper PPOs add the referral requirement while keeping the open panel.
*Citation: 211 CMR 51.03; Outline 6.2*

### Q032 | 6.0 | Medium
Which plan type covers out-of-network care, but at higher member cost-sharing?
- A) Staff-model HMO
- B) PPO
- C) Basic hospital indemnity
- D) Closed-panel HMO
**Answer: B** — The PPO's open panel steers members financially without closing the network.
*Citation: 211 CMR 51.03*

### Q033 | 6.0 | Easy
Under HIPAA, prior creditable coverage matters because it:
- A) Reduces premiums automatically
- B) Counts toward any applicable waiting limitation under a new group plan
- C) Eliminates the need for enrollment
- D) Guarantees identical benefits
**Answer: B** — Creditable coverage offsets limitation periods, preserving portability.
*Citation: HIPAA; Outline 6.2*

### Q034 | 6.0 | Medium
Precertification of a scheduled surgery is an example of which utilization review?
- A) Concurrent
- B) Retrospective
- C) Prospective
- D) External
**Answer: C** — Review before treatment is prospective; during is concurrent; after is retrospective.
*Citation: Outline 6.3*

### Q035 | 6.0 | Medium
An insurance company nurse monitors a member's ongoing hospital stay to assess continued necessity. This is:
- A) Prospective review
- B) Concurrent review
- C) Retrospective review
- D) Case settlement
**Answer: B** — Review during the course of treatment is concurrent review.
*Citation: Outline 6.3*

### Q036 | 6.0 | Medium
Under 176O:23, Massachusetts carriers must give members, in real time:
- A) Provider disciplinary records
- B) Estimated or maximum allowed costs and the member's out-of-pocket share for proposed services
- C) Competitor pricing
- D) Their underwriting files
**Answer: B** — The cost-transparency mandate: toll-free line and website with real-time cost estimates.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O § 23; Bulletin 2013-10*

### Q037 | 6.0 | Medium
A carrier denies an MRI as not medically necessary. The written notice it must send the member is called:
- A) A declination
- B) An adverse determination
- C) A rescission
- D) A reservation of rights
**Answer: B** — Medical-necessity denials/reductions/terminations require a written adverse determination with clinical basis and appeal rights.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O § 12*

### Q038 | 6.0 | Hard
A member files an internal grievance June 1. The carrier never responds. Under Massachusetts law, on the deadline's expiration the grievance is:
- A) Dismissed
- B) Deemed resolved in favor of the insured
- C) Automatically appealed
- D) Referred to arbitration
**Answer: B** — A grievance not properly acted on within the time limits is deemed resolved for the insured and is immediately eligible for external review.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O § 13(c); 958 CMR 3.311*

### Q039 | 6.0 | Medium
The standard deadline for a carrier's written resolution of a non-expedited internal grievance is:
- A) 10 days
- B) 30 days from receipt
- C) 90 days
- D) No fixed deadline
**Answer: B** — 30 days, extendable only by mutual written agreement (max 30 more).
*Citation: 958 CMR 3.305*

### Q040 | 6.0 | Hard
A hospitalized member's physician certifies that a coverage denial poses an immediate risk of serious harm. The carrier must:
- A) Schedule a hearing within 30 days
- B) Automatically reverse the decision within 48 hours
- C) Refer the case to OPP
- D) Wait for discharge to act
**Answer: B** — Physician certification of immediate serious harm triggers automatic reversal within 48 hours.
*Citation: 958 CMR 3.309*

### Q041 | 6.0 | Medium
After exhausting the internal grievance process, a Massachusetts member's external review goes through:
- A) The carrier's board of directors
- B) The Office of Patient Protection
- C) Small claims court
- D) The federal HHS
**Answer: B** — External review panels are administered by the Office of Patient Protection, and their decisions bind the carrier.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O § 14*

### Q042 | 6.0 | Hard
The most a Massachusetts insured can be charged in external review fees in one plan year is:
- A) $25 per review with no cap
- B) $75, regardless of the number of reviews
- C) $250
- D) Nothing — reviews are always free
**Answer: B** — $25 per review (waivable for hardship, refunded on full reversal), capped at $75 per plan year; the carrier pays the rest.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O § 14(a)*

### Q043 | 6.0 | Medium
An expedited external review panel must issue its written disposition within:
- A) 24 hours
- B) 72 hours of receiving the request
- C) 10 business days
- D) 30 days
**Answer: B** — Expedited external reviews are decided within 72 hours; expedited internal and external requests may be filed simultaneously.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O §§ 13(d), 14*

### Q044 | 6.0 | Medium
The deadline for filing a request for external review after the carrier's final internal decision is:
- A) 30 days
- B) 60 days
- C) 4 months
- D) 1 year
**Answer: C** — The OPP filing window is 4 months from the carrier's most recent decision.
*Citation: 958 CMR 3.400; M.G.L. c. 176O § 14*

### Q045 | 6.0 | Easy
Dependent children must be covered under Massachusetts policies until age:
- A) 19

### Q046 | 6.0 | Medium
Coverage of a mentally incapacitated adult child continues beyond the limiting age if proof of incapacity is furnished within:
- A) 10 days

- C) 60 days

### Q047 | 6.0 | Easy
A newborn's coverage under her parents' MA health policy begins:
- A) Upon enrollment
- B) At the moment of birth

**Answer: B** — Immediately from the moment of birth, with no waiting period or underwriting.

### Q048 | 6.0 | Medium
Baby Sofia is born six weeks premature with a congenital heart defect requiring surgery. Her parents' policy:
- A) Covers her but excludes the congenital condition
- B) Covers the prematurity and congenital defect from birth
- C) Imposes a 12-month pre-existing wait
- D) Requires a separate rider
**Answer: B** — § 47C explicitly mandates coverage of congenital defects, birth abnormalities, and premature birth from the moment of birth.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 47C*

### Q049 | 6.0 | Hard
A policy requires a specific premium to add a newborn and demands "notification" of the birth. Under § 47C, the parents satisfy the notification requirement by:
- A) Certified mail only
- B) Submitting a claim

**Answer: B** — The statute states that "notification" may mean submission of a claim.

### Q050 | 6.0 | Hard
The Garcias receive a foster placement of 3-year-old Leo (with foster payments) in January, file an adoption petition in June, and finalize in December. Leo is covered as their dependent from:
- A) January
- B) June — the petition filing date
- C) December — finalization
- D) The next open enrollment
**Answer: B** — A foster child receiving foster-care payments is covered from the petition filing date; other adoptions run from agency placement.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 47C*

A child adopted through a licensed agency (no prior foster relationship) is covered beginning:
- A) At adoption finalization
- B) On the date of placement in the home

### Q052 | 6.0 | Hard
Section 47C requires MA policies to cover all of the following EXCEPT:

### Q058 | 6.0 | Medium
Under PHS 2705, a carrier may NOT base premiums or eligibility on:
- A) Geographic area

### Q060 | 6.0 | Medium
Guaranteed renewability under PHS 2703 means coverage renews:
- A) At the carrier's discretion
- B) At the policyholder's option, except for statutory causes

### Q061 | 6.0 | Hard
Which correctly states the relationship between federal MHPAEA and M.G.L. c. 175 § 47B?

### Q062 | 6.0 | Hard

### Q063 | 6.0 | Medium
Under MHPAEA, a plan covering mental health may impose visit limits on therapy:
- A) Freely
- B) Only if no more restrictive than limits on comparable medical/surgical benefits

- D) Up to 20 visits per year by federal rule
**Answer: B** — Treatment limitations and financial requirements must be at parity with med/surg benefits — including non-quantitative limits like prior authorization.
*Citation: MHPAEA*

### Q064 | 6.0 | Medium
While a member grieves the termination of ongoing treatment, the disputed coverage:

- B) Remains in effect through completion of the internal grievance process

*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176O § 14(b)*
