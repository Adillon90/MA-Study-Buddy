### Q148 | 1.0 | Easy
The minimum age to obtain a Massachusetts insurance producer license is:
- A) 16
- B) 18
- C) 21
- D) 25
**Answer: B** — Candidates must be at least 18, pass the Prometric exam for their line, and apply through NIPR.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162I*

### Q149 | 1.0 | Easy
Massachusetts requires how many hours of pre-licensing education before sitting for the producer exam?
- A) 20 hours
- B) 40 hours
- C) 52 hours
- D) None
**Answer: D** — Massachusetts eliminated mandatory pre-licensing coursework; no pre-licensing hours are required.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162L*

### Q150 | 1.0 | Easy
Before using a DBA (assumed business name), a Massachusetts producer must:
- A) File it with the Secretary of State only
- B) Report it to the Commissioner before use
- C) Obtain insurer approval
- D) Wait 30 days after notifying the Commissioner
**Answer: B** — Assumed names must be reported to the Commissioner before use.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162P*

### Q151 | 1.0 | Easy
CE requirements before the first license renewal total ___ hours, including ___ hours of MA-approved Ethics.
- A) 45 hours; 3 hours
- B) 60 hours; 3 hours
- C) 60 hours; 6 hours
- D) 45 hours; 6 hours
**Answer: B** — 60 CE hours (including 3 ethics hours) are required before the first renewal; subsequent periods require 45 hours per 36 months.
*Citation: Outline 1.1*

### Q152 | 1.0 | Easy
After the first renewal, a Massachusetts producer must complete continuing education every ___ months.
- A) 12
- B) 24
- C) 36
- D) 60
**Answer: C** — Each subsequent CE cycle is 45 hours per 36-month period, including 3 ethics hours.
*Citation: Outline 1.1*

### Q153 | 1.0 | Easy
Premiums a producer collects from an applicant are held in what legal capacity?
- A) Trust
- B) Fiduciary
- C) Agency
- D) Corporate
**Answer: B** — Premiums are held in a fiduciary capacity; converting them is larceny under 175:176.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 176*

### Q154 | 1.0 | Easy
The Commissioner of Insurance is appointed rather than elected and has authority to:
- A) Write insurance statutes
- B) Enforce insurance laws, issue regulations, approve forms, and license producers
- C) Set premium rates by executive order
- D) Compel reinsurance purchases
**Answer: B** — The Commissioner enforces insurance laws, issues regulations, approves forms, examines companies, and licenses producers.
*Citation: 175:3A; 176D:5*

### Q155 | 1.0 | Medium
An insurer terminates a producer's appointment for one of the 162R causes. The insurer must notify the Commissioner within ___ of the termination's effective date, and must mail a copy to the producer within ___ by certified mail, return receipt requested.
- A) 15 days; 30 days
- B) 30 days; 15 days
- C) 30 days; 30 days
- D) 15 days; 15 days
**Answer: B** — Termination notice to the Commissioner: 30 days. Copy to producer: within 15 days of that notice, by certified mail for cause terminations.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162T*

### Q156 | 1.0 | Medium
A producer is found to have violated insurance regulations in Florida. Under 162V, the producer must report this administrative action to the Massachusetts Commissioner within:
- A) 10 days of the violation
- B) 30 days of final disposition, including a copy of the order
- C) 30 days of the initial pretrial hearing
- D) 60 days of becoming aware of the action
**Answer: B** — Administrative actions in any jurisdiction must be reported within 30 days of final disposition, with supporting documents.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162V*

### Q157 | 1.0 | Medium
A producer in Lowell is indicted for fraud. Under 162V, the producer must report the criminal prosecution to the Massachusetts Commissioner within:
- A) 30 days of the indictment
- B) 30 days of the initial pretrial hearing date
- C) 30 days of final disposition
- D) 60 days of the indictment
**Answer: B** — Criminal prosecutions in any jurisdiction must be reported within 30 days of the initial pretrial hearing date, including a copy of the complaint and any resulting orders.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162V*

### Q158 | 1.0 | Medium
A senior producer in Worcester dies unexpectedly. The Commissioner may issue a temporary producer license to keep the business running for up to:
- A) 90 days, with examination
- B) 180 days, without examination
- C) 180 days, with examination
- D) One year, without examination
**Answer: B** — Temporary licenses under 162Q last up to 180 days without examination, for qualifying circumstances such as a producer's death or disability.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162Q*

### Q159 | 1.0 | Medium
A company must hold a ___ to transact insurance in Massachusetts; one that holds it is called ___.
- A) Producer appointment; admitted
- B) Certificate of authority; admitted/authorized
- C) Surplus lines permit; admitted
- D) Charter; domestic
**Answer: B** — A certificate of authority (COA) is required to transact insurance in MA; insurers with a COA are admitted/authorized.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 4, 32, 151*

### Q160 | 1.0 | Medium
An insurer fires a producer and later discovers additional reportable information about her conduct. Under 162T the insurer must:
- A) File a new termination notice starting a new clock
- B) Promptly supplement its prior notice to the Commissioner
- C) Wait for the producer to report it under 162V
- D) Do nothing; the original notice suffices
**Answer: B** — If the insurer later discovers reportable information, it must promptly supplement the prior termination notice.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162T*

### Q161 | 1.0 | Medium
Under the unfair claims settlement practices statute, which conduct constitutes a violation when done with such frequency as to indicate a business practice?
- A) Paying claims above policy limits as a goodwill gesture
- B) Refusing to pay claims without conducting a reasonable investigation
- C) Asking for written proof of loss
- D) Denying a claim after conducting a thorough investigation
**Answer: B** — Refusing to pay without a reasonable investigation is one of the enumerated unfair claims settlement practices under 176D:3(9) when done with such frequency as to indicate a business practice.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176D § 3(9)*

### Q162 | 1.0 | Medium
A Springfield producer offers a prospect season tickets to the Red Sox if she buys a health policy. The prospect accepts. Under Massachusetts law:
- A) Only the producer violated the rebating prohibition
- B) Both the producer and the prospect violated the rebating prohibition
- C) Only the prospect violated the law by accepting
- D) No violation occurred because sports tickets are not currency
**Answer: B** — Rebating under 175:182–184 and 176D:3(8) prohibits both giving and receiving anything of value not specified in the policy as an inducement to buy.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 182–184; c. 176D § 3(8)*

### Q163 | 1.0 | Medium
An insurer files a policy form and begins selling it before the Commissioner approves it. This violates:
- A) The CE requirements
- B) The requirement that forms be filed with and approved by the Commissioner before use
- C) The appointment notification requirement
- D) The Commissioner's right to examine insurer books and records
**Answer: B** — Policy forms, riders, and endorsements must be filed with and approved by the Commissioner before use.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 2B, 192*

### Q164 | 1.0 | Medium
A producer's license is under investigation when she tries to surrender it to avoid discipline. The Commissioner:
- A) Must accept the surrender and close the case
- B) Retains authority over anyone under investigation — surrendering does not escape enforcement
- C) May waive disciplinary action if surrender is voluntary
- D) Must hold a hearing before the surrender takes effect
**Answer: B** — Surrendering or letting a license lapse does not stop the Commissioner from pursuing enforcement.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162R*

### Q165 | 1.0 | Medium
Under c. 175I, if an insurer makes an adverse underwriting decision based on personal information, the applicant:
- A) Has no recourse
- B) Must receive specific reasons for the adverse decision
- C) May only appeal to the Commissioner
- D) Must be offered coverage at a higher rate
**Answer: B** — The Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act requires that applicants receive specific reasons for adverse underwriting decisions.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175I*

### Q166 | 1.0 | Hard
A Massachusetts carrier is found to have committed an unfair or deceptive act under M.G.L. c. 176D. The maximum civil penalty per act is:
- A) $100
- B) $500
- C) $1,000
- D) $10,000
**Answer: C** — Unfair or deceptive acts carry a fine of up to $1,000 for each and every act under 176D:7, plus a cease-and-desist order (and license suspension/revocation for repeated violations).
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 176D § 7*

### Q167 | 1.0 | Hard
The fine for violating insurance laws not separately enumerated (such as failing to report under 162V) is up to ___ under M.G.L. c. 175 § 194.
- A) $100 per violation
- B) $500 per violation
- C) $1,000 per violation
- D) $10,000 per violation
**Answer: B** — Violations of insurance laws not separately listed carry up to $500 per violation under 175:194.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 194*

### Q168 | 1.0 | Hard
A business entity's insurance license may be disciplined if a partner or officer:
- A) Simply employed a producer who later committed a violation
- B) Knew or should have known of an individual licensee's violation and neither reported it nor took corrective action
- C) Hired an unlicensed staff member for non-sales duties
- D) Filed a late appointment notice
**Answer: B** — Entity licenses are at risk when a managing person knew or should have known of the violation and failed to report or correct it.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 162R*

### Q169 | 1.0 | Hard
Paying a commission to an unlicensed person, or accepting business submitted by an unlicensed person, violates:
- A) M.G.L. c. 175 § 175 (impersonation)
- B) M.G.L. c. 175 § 177 (unlicensed compensation)
- C) M.G.L. c. 175 § 176 (larceny)
- D) M.G.L. c. 176D § 3(8) (rebating)
**Answer: B** — Section 177 prohibits paying commission to, or accepting business from, any unlicensed person.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 177*

### Q170 | 1.0 | Hard
Under the FCRA, if an insurer takes adverse action against an applicant based on a consumer report, the insurer must:
- A) Destroy the report immediately
- B) Tell the applicant and identify the consumer reporting agency
- C) File a copy with the Commissioner
- D) Offer coverage with a rider instead
**Answer: B** — When adverse action is taken based on a consumer report, the FCRA requires the insurer to notify the applicant and identify the reporting agency so the applicant can dispute inaccuracies.
*Citation: 15 USC 1681*
