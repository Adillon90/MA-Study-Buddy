### Q126 | 3.0 | Easy
A policy covering only accidental death pays the principal sum for death and the capital sum for lesser dismemberments. What fraction of the principal sum is the capital sum typically set at?
- A) The same as the principal sum — all AD&D losses pay equally
- B) 75%
- C) 50%
- D) Two-thirds
**Answer: C** — The capital sum (for lesser dismemberments) is often 50% of the principal sum; it is a fraction, not the full benefit.

*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### Q127 | 3.0 | Easy
A policy covering a sports team's changing roster without naming individuals is classified as:
- A) Group insurance
- B) Blanket insurance
- C) Franchise insurance
- D) Credit insurance
**Answer: B** — Blanket insurance covers a changing group (sports teams, bus passengers, students) without identifying individual insureds.
*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### Q128 | 3.0 | Easy
The prerequisite for opening a Health Savings Account (HSA) is enrollment in a:
- A) Medicare supplement plan
- B) Qualified high-deductible health plan (HDHP)
- C) Group HMO
- D) Preferred provider organization
**Answer: B** — An HSA requires the accountholder to be enrolled in a qualified HDHP and have no other disqualifying coverage.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### Q129 | 3.0 | Easy
Archer Medical Savings Accounts are limited to self-employed individuals and employees of employers with no more than:
- A) 25 employees
- B) 50 employees
- C) 100 employees
- D) 250 employees
**Answer: B** — Archer MSAs are limited to the self-employed and employees of small employers of 50 or fewer; they are largely closed to new participants.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### Q130 | 3.0 | Easy
The Massachusetts individual mandate requires residents who are at least ___ to maintain Minimum Creditable Coverage or face a state tax penalty.
- A) 16
- B) 18
- C) 21
- D) 26
**Answer: B** — Massachusetts adults 18 and older must maintain MCC; the penalty is assessed through the state income tax return.
*Citation: c. 111M*

### Q131 | 3.0 | Easy
The three risk classifications an underwriter assigns individual applicants are:
- A) Acceptable, marginal, and declined
- B) Preferred, standard, and substandard
- C) Class 1, Class 2, and Class 3
- D) Basic, enhanced, and premium
**Answer: B** — Preferred (better than average), standard (average), and substandard (higher risk; rated premium, exclusion rider, or modified benefits).
*Citation: Outline 3.7*

### Q132 | 3.0 | Easy
Comprehensive medical coverage is classified in Massachusetts terminology as:
- A) Accident/sickness insurance
- B) Health insurance
- C) A limited policy
- D) A blanket policy
**Answer: B** — The MA exam outline distinguishes limited-benefit products (classified as accident/sickness policies) from comprehensive coverage (classified as health insurance).
*Citation: Outlines 3.1–3.3*

### Q133 | 3.0 | Medium
A Framingham schoolteacher loses sight in one eye and use of one hand in an accident. Under an AD&D policy, these partial losses that fall short of the principal-sum threshold are paid as the:
- A) Principal sum
- B) Capital sum
- C) Lesser of actual expenses or the principal sum
- D) Nothing — AD&D pays only for accidental death
**Answer: B** — Losses short of death, loss of both hands/feet, or total sight are partial dismemberments paid as the capital sum (a fraction of the principal sum).
*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### Q134 | 3.0 | Medium
Rosa buys a credit disability policy when she takes out a car loan. If she becomes disabled, the policy pays:
- A) Rosa's monthly income
- B) Rosa's loan payments; the creditor is the beneficiary
- C) Rosa directly in a lump sum
- D) Medical bills arising from the disability
**Answer: B** — Credit disability pays the insured's loan payments during disability; the creditor is the beneficiary, not the insured.
*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### Q135 | 3.0 | Medium
HSA funds that are not used in a given year:
- A) Are forfeited under the use-it-or-lose-it rule
- B) Roll over year to year with no limit
- C) Are taxed as ordinary income
- D) Must be used within 90 days
**Answer: B** — HSA funds roll over indefinitely; there is no use-it-or-lose-it rule, and they are portable if the accountholder changes jobs.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### Q136 | 3.0 | Medium
The "triple tax advantage" of an HSA means contributions are ___, growth is ___, and qualified medical withdrawals are ___.
- A) Taxable; taxable; tax-free
- B) Deductible; tax-deferred; tax-free
- C) Deductible; taxable; tax-free
- D) Deductible; tax-deferred; taxable
**Answer: B** — HSA contributions are deductible, earnings grow tax-deferred, and withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are tax-free.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### Q137 | 3.0 | Medium
The Massachusetts individual mandate requires MCC to be reported on which form with the state income tax return?
- A) Schedule D
- B) Schedule HC
- C) Form 1095-MA
- D) Form W-2MA
**Answer: B** — The MA mandate is reported on Schedule HC with the Massachusetts income tax return.
*Citation: 956 CMR 5.00*

### Q138 | 3.0 | Medium
When a Massachusetts replacement sale occurs, the agent must provide the "Notice to Applicant Regarding Replacement":
- A) Only at policy delivery
- B) At the time of application or before the policy is issued, leaving a copy with the applicant
- C) Within 30 days after issue
- D) Only if the client requests it
**Answer: B** — The notice must be given at application or before issue; a copy stays with the applicant and a signed copy is retained in the carrier's records.
*Citation: 211 CMR 42.08*

### Q139 | 3.0 | Medium
A producer orders an investigative consumer report containing interviews with a Worcester applicant's neighbors about her lifestyle. Under the FCRA, the applicant:
- A) Has no rights regarding the report
- B) Must be notified and may request disclosure of its nature and scope
- C) Must consent before any insurance is issued
- D) May only dispute the report after a claim
**Answer: B** — FCRA requires that applicants be notified when an investigative consumer report is ordered and may request disclosure of the nature and scope.
*Citation: Outline 3.7*

### Q140 | 3.0 | Medium
The outline of coverage required under 211 CMR 42.09 must be delivered:
- A) Only at renewal
- B) To the applicant at application or with policy delivery
- C) Only if the policy is replacing another
- D) Annually
**Answer: B** — The outline of coverage, a plain-language summary of benefits, exclusions, and renewability, must be delivered at application or with policy delivery.
*Citation: 211 CMR 42.09*

### Q141 | 3.0 | Medium
The producer report completed at the time of application:
- A) Is provided to the applicant for review and signature
- B) Is shared with the MIB database automatically
- C) Contains the agent's observations and is not seen by the applicant
- D) Replaces the attending physician statement
**Answer: C** — The producer report conveys the agent's risk observations to the insurer; it is not shown to the applicant.
*Citation: Outline 3.7*

### Q142 | 3.0 | Medium
No premium was collected at the time Henry submitted his Springfield health insurance application. At delivery, for coverage to take effect, the producer must collect the premium and obtain:
- A) A new application
- B) A statement of continued good health
- C) An MIB authorization
- D) A signed waiver of pre-existing conditions
**Answer: B** — Without a premium at application, delivery requires both payment and a statement of continued good health.
*Citation: Outline 3.6*

### Q143 | 3.0 | Medium
An individual accident/sickness policy in Massachusetts that offers only limited coverage must carry a prominent:
- A) Certificate of authority number
- B) Notice to the insured that the coverage is limited
- C) Schedule of benefits on the declarations page
- D) Producer disclosure statement
**Answer: B** — Massachusetts requires limited-benefit policies to carry a prominent notice to the insured that the coverage is limited.
*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### Q144 | 3.0 | Hard
"Accidental means" wording differs from "accidental bodily injury" wording in that accidental means:
- A) Is more favorable to the insured — any unexpected result qualifies
- B) Is stricter — the cause of the injury itself must be accidental
- C) Requires the insured to prove gross negligence
- D) Covers sickness as well as injury
**Answer: B** — Accidental means requires the cause to be accidental (stricter); accidental bodily injury/results requires only that the result be accidental (broader, more favorable to the insured).
*Citation: Outlines 3.1–3.3*

### Q145 | 3.0 | Hard
The replacement notice under 211 CMR 42.11 warns the applicant in substance that:
- A) The new policy's premiums may be higher
- B) Existing health conditions may not be covered under the new policy
- C) The new policy must provide identical benefits to the old policy
- D) The agent is not required to give the notice if the client initiates the replacement unprompted
**Answer: B** — The prescribed replacement notice warns that existing health conditions may not be covered under the new policy and urges comparison before terminating old coverage.
*Citation: 211 CMR 42.11*

### Q146 | 3.0 | Medium
A Massachusetts insurer may NOT do which of the following in individual A&H underwriting?
- A) Order an HIV test with written informed consent
- B) Use genetic information without the applicant's consent
- C) Classify risk as substandard and offer a rated policy
- D) Request an attending physician statement
**Answer: B** — Massachusetts bars insurers from requiring genetic tests or using genetic information in A&S underwriting without consent under 175:108H and 108I.
*Citation: 175:108H; 175:108I*

### Q147 | 3.0 | Hard
All of the following satisfy the Massachusetts Minimum Creditable Coverage requirement EXCEPT:
- A) An employer-sponsored major medical plan
- B) A MassHealth plan
- C) A Medicare Advantage plan
- D) A hospital indemnity policy paying $200 per day
**Answer: D** — Limited accident/sickness policies such as hospital indemnity do not constitute MCC; the mandate requires comprehensive coverage meeting state standards.
*Citation: c. 111M; 956 CMR 5.00*
