### Q243 | 10.0 | Easy
The core rule of thumb for health insurance taxation is: if premiums were paid with pre-tax (deducted) dollars, benefits are:
- A) Tax-free
- B) Taxable
- C) Taxed at capital gains rates
- D) Taxed at 50%
**Answer: B** — Somebody gets the tax break, but not both ends: pre-tax premiums → taxable benefits; after-tax premiums → tax-free benefits.
*Citation: Outline 10.1*

### Q244 | 10.0 | Easy
Employer-paid premiums for a group medical and dental plan are deductible to the employer and:
- A) Included in employees' taxable income
- B) Not taxable income to employees; benefits are also tax-free
- C) Taxable to employees above $5,000
- D) Deductible by employees as well
**Answer: B** — The most tax-favored arrangement: employer deducts the premium, employees exclude it from income, and benefits are tax-free.
*Citation: Outline 10.2*

### Q245 | 10.0 | Easy
FICA taxes apply to employer-paid disability benefits for the first ___ after the employee last worked.
- A) 3 calendar months
- B) 6 calendar months
- C) 12 months
- D) Until the employee qualifies for Social Security disability
**Answer: B** — FICA (Social Security and Medicare tax) applies to employer-paid disability benefits during the first 6 calendar months after the employee last worked; after that, FICA stops.
*Citation: Outline 10.2*

### Q246 | 10.0 | Easy
Group long-term care benefits paid by the employer are deductible to the employer, excluded from employee income, and qualified benefits are tax-free — but group LTC may NOT be offered through a:
- A) A group medical expense plan offered by the employer
- B) A group AD&D program
- C) Cafeteria plan or FSA
- D) A self-insured (self-funded) health arrangement
**Answer: C** — Group LTC cannot be offered through a cafeteria plan or flexible spending account; it is the notable exception in the group benefits tax rules.
*Citation: Outline 10.2*

### Q247 | 10.0 | Medium
Diana personally owns a medical expense policy and pays all premiums herself. The premiums are potentially deductible as an itemized deduction only to the extent total unreimbursed medical expenses exceed:
- A) 5% of AGI
- B) 7.5% of AGI
- C) 10% of AGI
- D) $2,000 per year
**Answer: B** — Personal medical expense premiums count toward the itemized deduction, which requires total unreimbursed medical expenses to exceed 7.5% of AGI.
*Citation: Outline 10.1*

### Q248 | 10.0 | Medium
A self-employed architect deducts 100% of her family's health insurance premiums above the line. The deduction is limited to her:
- A) Total adjusted gross income
- B) Earned income from the business
- C) Investment income
- D) Combined household income
**Answer: B** — The 100% above-the-line self-employed deduction is capped at the owner's earned income from the self-employment activity.
*Citation: Outline 10.3*

### Q249 | 10.0 | Medium
The self-employed health insurance deduction covers premiums for all of the following EXCEPT:
- A) Medical coverage for the owner
- B) Dental coverage for the owner's spouse
- C) Tax-qualified LTC coverage for the owner
- D) Disability income premiums
**Answer: D** — The 100% above-the-line deduction covers medical, dental, and qualified LTC; disability income premiums are not included.
*Citation: Outline 10.3*

### Q250 | 10.0 | Medium
Kevin's employer pays 70% of his LTD premium; Kevin pays the remaining 30% with after-tax dollars. When Kevin collects LTD benefits, they are:
- A) Fully taxable
- B) Fully tax-free
- C) 70% taxable, proportional to the employer-paid share
- D) Taxable only after 6 months
**Answer: C** — Benefits are taxable in proportion to the employer-paid share; 70% employer-paid = 70% of benefits taxable to Kevin.
*Citation: Outline 10.2*

### Q251 | 10.0 | Medium
AD&D benefits received under an employer-paid group plan are generally:
- A) Fully taxable as ordinary income
- B) Received tax-free
- C) Taxable only above the employee's cost basis
- D) Subject to estate tax only
**Answer: B** — AD&D is treated like medical/health coverage: employer premiums are deductible, and benefits are generally received tax-free.
*Citation: Outline 10.2*

### Q252 | 10.0 | Hard
Key person disability income and disability buy-sell both share which tax treatment?
- A) Premiums deductible; benefits taxable
- B) Premiums not deductible; benefits not taxable
- C) Premiums deductible; benefits tax-free
- D) Premiums not deductible; benefits taxable
**Answer: B** — Both key person DI and disability buy-sell carry non-deductible premiums and tax-free benefit proceeds — the inverse of BOE.
*Citation: Outline 10.4*

### Q253 | 10.0 | Hard
A Lowell dentist pays BOE premiums for her practice. Which statement correctly describes the tax treatment?
- A) Premiums are not deductible; benefits are tax-free
- B) Premiums are deductible; benefits are taxable — but the benefits pay deductible overhead, washing out the tax cost
- C) Premiums are deductible; benefits are tax-free
- D) Premiums and benefits are both tax-free
**Answer: B** — BOE inverts the usual pattern: deductible premiums and taxable benefits — but the benefits are spent on rent, staff, and other deductible overhead, so the net tax impact washes out.
*Citation: Outline 10.4*

### Q254 | 10.0 | Hard
All of the following correctly apply the rule of thumb EXCEPT:
- A) Personally owned DI: non-deductible premiums → tax-free benefits
- B) Employer-paid group medical: deductible to employer, excluded from employee income → tax-free benefits
- C) Employee-paid LTD with after-tax dollars: → tax-free benefits
- D) Employer-paid group LTD: → tax-free benefits to the employee
**Answer: D** — Employer-paid group LTD premiums are deductible to the employer and excluded from employee income, which makes the benefits taxable to the employee — the rule of thumb in action.
*Citation: Outline 10.2*
