### L021 | 4.0 | Easy
The cheapest form of pure life insurance, with no cash value and protection only during a set period, is:
- A) Whole life
- B) Universal life
- C) Term life
- D) Variable life
**Answer: C** — Term is pure death protection with no cash value, so it is always cheaper than whole life for the same face.
*Citation: Outline 4.1*

### L022 | 4.0 | Easy
A term policy whose face amount declines on a schedule while the premium stays level is typically used for:
- A) Estate accumulation
- B) Mortgage protection
- C) Retirement income
- D) Charitable giving
**Answer: B** — Decreasing term matches a falling debt like a mortgage; the premium stays level as the face drops.
*Citation: Outline 4.1*

### L023 | 4.0 | Medium
When a renewable term policy is renewed, the premium increases because the insurer uses the insured's:
- A) Original issue age
- B) Attained (current) age
- C) Average age over the term
- D) Spouse's age
**Answer: B** — Renewal and conversion both reprice at attained age, the number-one driver of life premium cost.
*Citation: Outline 4.1*

### L024 | 4.0 | Medium
The convertibility feature of a term policy allows the owner to:
- A) Lower the face amount at will
- B) Change to permanent coverage without evidence of insurability
- C) Skip premiums during disability
- D) Add a spouse automatically
**Answer: B** — Conversion changes term to permanent without proving insurability; the premium is based on attained age.
*Citation: Outline 4.1*

### L025 | 4.0 | Easy
A whole life policy matures (endows) at age:
- A) 65
- B) 90
- C) 100
- D) 120
**Answer: C** — Whole life provides permanent protection, level premiums, and cash value, maturing at age 100.
*Citation: Outline 4.2*

### L026 | 4.0 | Medium
Compared with straight whole life, a 20-pay life policy has:
- A) A lower premium and slower cash value growth
- B) A higher premium and faster cash value growth
- C) The same premium but a higher face
- D) No cash value
**Answer: B** — A shorter premium-paying period means higher premiums and faster cash value accumulation.
*Citation: Outline 4.2*

### L027 | 4.0 | Hard
Which whole life variation creates an immediate cash (nonforfeiture) value at issue?
- A) Modified whole life
- B) Graded premium whole life
- C) Single-premium whole life
- D) Straight whole life
**Answer: C** — Paying the entire premium in one lump sum creates an immediate cash value; the other types build value over time.
*Citation: Outline 4.2*

### L028 | 4.0 | Medium
A universal life policy is characterized by:
- A) Fixed premiums and a fixed face amount
- B) Flexible premiums and an adjustable death benefit
- C) No cash value
- D) Mandatory securities licensing to own
**Answer: B** — UL offers flexible premiums and benefits with a minimum guaranteed interest rate; surrender charges must be disclosed.
*Citation: Outline 4.3*

### L029 | 4.0 | Hard
To sell a variable life policy, a producer must hold:
- A) Only a Massachusetts life license
- B) Only a FINRA securities registration
- C) Both a life license and a FINRA registration
- D) A property-casualty license
**Answer: C** — Variable products are securities (separate account, owner bears investment risk), requiring both credentials.
*Citation: 211 CMR 95; Outline 4.3*

### L030 | 4.0 | Medium
In a family plan policy, coverage on the spouse and children is usually provided as:
- A) Separate whole life policies
- B) Level term riders on the primary insured's policy
- C) Universal life
- D) Annuities
**Answer: B** — The head is covered by permanent insurance and the family rides on as level term riders, at one premium regardless of the number of children.
*Citation: Outline 4.4*

### L031 | 4.0 | Hard
A survivorship (second-to-die) policy pays the death benefit when:
- A) The first insured dies
- B) The last surviving insured dies
- C) Either insured becomes disabled
- D) The policy matures
**Answer: B** — Survivorship life pays on the second death and is common in estate planning; its premium is lower than two separate policies.
*Citation: Outline 4.4*

### L032 | 4.0 | Medium
A juvenile policy's payor provision waives premiums if the:
- A) Child becomes disabled
- B) Adult premium payor dies or becomes disabled
- C) Policy reaches maturity
- D) Insurer becomes insolvent
**Answer: B** — The payor benefit protects the policy by waiving premiums until the child reaches a set age if the payor dies or is disabled.
*Citation: Outline 4.4*

### L033 | 4.0 | Easy
Credit life insurance is generally written:
- A) For twice the loan balance
- B) For no more than the outstanding loan balance
- C) As a whole life policy
- D) Without a beneficiary
**Answer: B** — Credit life covers a debtor and is capped at the loan balance, usually as decreasing term matched to the loan.
*Citation: Outline 4.6*

### L034 | 4.0 | Easy
A policy that fails the 7-pay test is classified as a:
- A) Modified endowment contract
- B) Variable contract
- C) Group policy
- D) Participating policy
**Answer: A** — Overfunding beyond the 7-pay limit makes the policy a MEC, which loses favorable living-benefit tax treatment.
*Citation: Outline 4.3; 7.2*

### L035 | 4.0 | Hard
Distributions from a MEC taken before age 59½ are:
- A) Tax-free
- B) Taxed FIFO with no penalty
- C) Taxed LIFO and subject to a 10% penalty
- D) Subject only to a 6% excise tax
**Answer: C** — MEC distributions are taxed LIFO (earnings first) with a 10% penalty before 59½; the death benefit stays tax-free.
*Citation: Outline 4.3; 7.2*

### L036 | 4.0 | Medium
An equity-indexed universal life policy differs from a variable policy because it:
- A) Has no cash value
- B) Guarantees a minimum interest floor while linking gains to an index
- C) Invests in a separate account chosen by the owner
- D) Requires no life license
**Answer: B** — Indexed UL ties growth to a market index but keeps a guaranteed minimum floor, unlike a variable policy with no floor.
*Citation: Outline 4.3*
