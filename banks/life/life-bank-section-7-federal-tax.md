### L079 | 7.0 | Easy
A life insurance death benefit paid as a lump sum to a named beneficiary is generally:
- A) Fully taxable as ordinary income
- B) Income-tax-free
- C) Taxed as a capital gain
- D) Subject to a 10% penalty
**Answer: B** — Death benefits are received income-tax-free; only interest on proceeds left with the insurer is taxable.
*Citation: Outline 7.1*

### L080 | 7.0 | Medium
Policy dividends on a participating life policy are not taxable because they are treated as:
- A) Capital gains
- B) A return of overpaid premium
- C) Tax-deferred earnings
- D) Ordinary income
**Answer: B** — Dividends are a return of premium and not taxable, though interest earned on accumulated dividends is taxable.
*Citation: Outline 7.1*

### L081 | 7.0 | Medium
A policy loan taken against an in-force (non-MEC) life policy is:
- A) Taxable as ordinary income
- B) Not taxable while the policy stays in force
- C) Subject to a 10% penalty
- D) Taxed LIFO
**Answer: B** — Loans against an in-force policy are not taxable; unpaid loans reduce the death benefit.
*Citation: Outline 7.1*

### L082 | 7.0 | Hard
Distributions from a modified endowment contract are taxed:
- A) FIFO with no penalty
- B) LIFO, with a 10% penalty before age 59½
- C) As a tax-free return of basis
- D) Only at death
**Answer: B** — MEC distributions are LIFO (earnings first) and carry a 10% penalty before 59½; the death benefit remains tax-free.
*Citation: Outline 7.2*

### L083 | 7.0 | Medium
Contributions to a traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, and the distributions are:
- A) Tax-free
- B) Taxed as ordinary income
- C) Taxed as capital gains
- D) Never taxed
**Answer: B** — A traditional IRA is deductible going in and taxed coming out as ordinary income.
*Citation: Outline 7.4*

### L084 | 7.0 | Hard
A qualified, tax-free withdrawal of earnings from a Roth IRA requires the funds be held at least five years AND one of the following EXCEPT:
- A) Reaching age 59½
- B) Death of the owner
- C) Permanent disability
- D) Reaching age 50
**Answer: D** — A qualified Roth withdrawal needs the 5-year hold plus 59½, death, disability, or a first-home purchase; age 50 is not a trigger.
*Citation: Outline 7.4*

### L085 | 7.0 | Easy
A Section 1035 exchange allows a policyowner to exchange one contract for another without:
- A) The insurer's consent
- B) Recognizing a taxable gain
- C) Underwriting
- D) Paying premiums
**Answer: B** — A 1035 exchange moves cash value between qualifying contracts without recognizing gain.
*Citation: Outline 7.6*

### L086 | 7.0 | Medium
The taxable portion of a non-qualified annuity payment is the part representing:
- A) Return of principal
- B) Earnings above the investment in the contract
- C) The death benefit
- D) The premium load
**Answer: B** — The exclusion ratio shields the principal; the earnings portion of each payment is taxable.
*Citation: Outline 7.3*
