### L057 | 6.0 | Easy
An annuity primarily protects against the risk of:
- A) Dying too soon
- B) Outliving your money
- C) Disability
- D) Property loss
**Answer: B** — An annuity is the opposite of life insurance: it protects against outliving your income.
*Citation: Outline 6.1*

### L058 | 6.0 | Medium
The person whose lifespan determines the annuity payout is the:
- A) Owner
- B) Annuitant
- C) Beneficiary
- D) Insurer
**Answer: B** — The annuitant is the measuring life; the owner controls the contract and the beneficiary receives death proceeds.
*Citation: Outline 6.1*

### L059 | 6.0 | Easy
An immediate annuity begins paying income within about one month and can be purchased only with:
- A) Flexible premiums
- B) A single premium
- C) Annual premiums
- D) Employer contributions
**Answer: B** — Immediate annuities (SPIAs) require a single premium and pay within roughly a month.
*Citation: Outline 6.2*

### L060 | 6.0 | Medium
In a fixed annuity, the investment risk is borne by the:
- A) Annuitant
- B) Owner
- C) Insurer
- D) Beneficiary
**Answer: C** — A fixed annuity guarantees a rate and keeps the investment risk on the insurer, in its general account.
*Citation: Outline 6.4*

### L061 | 6.0 | Hard
A producer selling a variable annuity must hold:
- A) Only a life license
- B) Only a FINRA registration
- C) Both a life license and a FINRA registration
- D) A health license
**Answer: C** — A variable annuity is a security (separate account, owner bears the risk), requiring both credentials.
*Citation: Outline 6.4*

### L062 | 6.0 | Hard
An equity-indexed annuity is best described as:
- A) A variable annuity with no guarantees
- B) A fixed annuity with index-linked excess interest and a guaranteed floor
- C) A whole life policy
- D) A market value adjusted annuity surrendered early
**Answer: B** — An equity-indexed annuity is a fixed annuity that adds index-linked upside while keeping a minimum guaranteed floor.
*Citation: Outline 6.4*

### L063 | 6.0 | Medium
A market value adjustment on a deferred annuity applies only when the contract is:
- A) Annuitized
- B) Surrendered before the end of the contract period
- C) Held to maturity
- D) Converted to life insurance
**Answer: B** — The MVA feature applies only on early surrender; otherwise the annuity behaves like a fixed annuity.
*Citation: Outline 6.4*

### L064 | 6.0 | Medium
The payout option that provides the highest monthly income but leaves nothing to a beneficiary is:
- A) Life with period certain
- B) Joint and survivor
- C) Straight life
- D) Cash refund
**Answer: C** — Straight (pure) life pays the most precisely because it guarantees nothing beyond the annuitant's lifetime.
*Citation: Outline 6.3*

### L065 | 6.0 | Hard
Under a life with period certain option, if the annuitant dies during the guaranteed period:
- A) Payments stop immediately
- B) The beneficiary receives payments for the remainder of the period
- C) The insurer keeps the balance
- D) The estate must repay prior payments
**Answer: B** — The beneficiary collects the remaining guaranteed payments if death occurs within the certain period.
*Citation: Outline 6.3*

### L066 | 6.0 | Medium
A joint and one-half survivor option pays the surviving annuitant:
- A) The full original benefit
- B) Two-thirds of the original benefit
- C) One-half of the original benefit
- D) Nothing
**Answer: C** — The one-half survivor option reduces the survivor's payment to half the original joint benefit.
*Citation: Outline 6.3*

### L067 | 6.0 | Easy
During the accumulation period of an annuity, contributions are credited as:
- A) Annuity units
- B) Accumulation units
- C) Dividends
- D) Paid-up additions
**Answer: B** — Contributions buy accumulation units, which convert to annuity units when payout begins.
*Citation: Outline 6.1*

### L068 | 6.0 | Medium
The portion of each annuity payment representing a tax-free return of principal is determined by the:
- A) Exclusion ratio
- B) Loading factor
- C) Expense ratio
- D) Mortality charge
**Answer: A** — The exclusion ratio (investment in the contract divided by expected return) sets the tax-free share of each payment.
*Citation: Outline 6.5; 7.3*

### L069 | 6.0 | Hard
Which 1035 exchange is NOT permitted tax-free?
- A) Life insurance to an annuity
- B) Annuity to an annuity
- C) Annuity to a life insurance policy
- D) Endowment to an annuity
**Answer: C** — You may move life into an annuity, but never an annuity back into a life insurance policy.
*Citation: Outline 7.6*

### L070 | 6.0 | Easy
A deferred annuity differs from an immediate annuity in that income payments begin:
- A) Within one month
- B) At least one year after purchase
- C) Only at death
- D) Never
**Answer: B** — Deferred annuities postpone income for at least a year and may be funded with single or flexible premiums.
*Citation: Outline 6.2*
