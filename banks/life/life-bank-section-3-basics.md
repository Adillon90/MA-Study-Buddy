### L037 | 3.0 | Easy
For a life insurance policy, insurable interest must exist:
- A) At the time of the insured's death
- B) At the time of application
- C) Continuously throughout the policy
- D) Only if there is a loan
**Answer: B** — Life insurable interest is tested at application (inception), unlike property insurance, which needs it at the time of loss.
*Citation: Outline 3.1*

### L038 | 3.0 | Medium
A method of determining life insurance need that totals a family's obligations (final expenses, income, education) and subtracts existing resources is the:
- A) Human life value approach
- B) Needs approach
- C) Capitalization approach
- D) Estate tax approach
**Answer: B** — The needs approach is best for answering how much coverage a client should apply for.
*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### L039 | 3.0 | Easy
The human life value approach measures:
- A) The cash value of a policy
- B) The dollar value of the insured's future earnings
- C) The estate tax due at death
- D) The premium needed for whole life
**Answer: B** — Human life value estimates lost future earnings to set the face amount.
*Citation: Outline 3.4*

### L040 | 3.0 | Medium
In a partnership cross-purchase buy-sell plan:
- A) The partnership owns a policy on each partner
- B) Each partner owns a policy on each other partner
- C) A bank owns the policies
- D) The employees own the policies
**Answer: B** — Cross-purchase means each partner insures the others; the entity plan is where the partnership itself owns the policies.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### L041 | 3.0 | Hard
A close corporation buy-sell plan in which the corporation owns, pays for, and is the beneficiary of policies on each stockholder is called a:
- A) Cross-purchase plan
- B) Stock redemption plan
- C) Split-dollar plan
- D) Salary continuation plan
**Answer: B** — The corporate version of the entity plan is a stock redemption plan; the corporation redeems the deceased's shares.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### L042 | 3.0 | Medium
In a key person life insurance arrangement, the policy is owned by and payable to:
- A) The key employee
- B) The key employee's family
- C) The business
- D) A trust for the employee
**Answer: C** — The business owns, pays for, and is the beneficiary; premiums are not deductible and the death proceeds are not taxable.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### L043 | 3.0 | Hard
Which statement about key person insurance is correct?
- A) Premiums are tax-deductible and proceeds are taxable
- B) Premiums are not deductible and proceeds are not taxable
- C) Premiums are deductible and proceeds are tax-free
- D) Both premiums and proceeds are tax-deductible
**Answer: B** — Key person premiums are paid with after-tax dollars (not deductible), so the death proceeds to the business are received tax-free.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### L044 | 3.0 | Medium
An arrangement where an employer and employee share the cost of a life policy on the employee is a:
- A) Deferred compensation plan
- B) Salary continuation plan
- C) Split-dollar plan
- D) Cross-purchase plan
**Answer: C** — Split-dollar is a method of sharing the cost of buying insurance, not a policy type.
*Citation: Outline 3.5*

### L045 | 3.0 | Easy
The three primary factors used to determine a life insurance premium are mortality, interest, and:
- A) Inflation
- B) Expense
- C) Lapse rate
- D) Dividends
**Answer: B** — Mortality, interest, and expense (the loading) are the three primary premium factors.
*Citation: Outline 3.7*

### L046 | 3.0 | Medium
Choosing a monthly premium mode instead of an annual one generally results in:
- A) A lower total annual cost
- B) A higher total annual cost
- C) The same total cost
- D) A waiver of interest
**Answer: B** — More frequent payment modes cost more in total because of lost interest and added billing expense.
*Citation: Outline 3.7*

### L047 | 3.0 | Medium
When an applicant makes an error on a life application, the producer may correct it only if:
- A) The producer initials the change alone
- B) The applicant initials the change
- C) The underwriter approves by phone
- D) A new policy is issued first
**Answer: B** — The applicant must initial any correction; a producer can never alter the application alone.
*Citation: Outline 3.8*

### L048 | 3.0 | Easy
At policy delivery the producer must provide a general description of policy types in the:
- A) Policy summary
- B) Buyer's guide
- C) Agent's report
- D) Outline of coverage
**Answer: B** — The buyer's guide gives general descriptions; the policy summary gives the specifics of the policy purchased.
*Citation: Outline 3.8*

### L049 | 3.0 | Hard
A Massachusetts life application may be backdated to obtain a lower premium for up to:
- A) 3 months
- B) 6 months
- C) 12 months
- D) 18 months
**Answer: B** — Backdating ("saving age") is permitted up to six months; the owner pays the back premiums.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 130; Outline 3.8*

### L050 | 3.0 | Medium
Under a conditional receipt, if the applicant proves insurable as applied for, coverage takes effect on the:
- A) Date the policy is delivered
- B) Date of application or the medical exam, whichever is later
- C) Date the first claim is filed
- D) Date the agent is paid
**Answer: B** — A conditional receipt makes coverage effective from the application or exam date, whichever is later, contingent on insurability.
*Citation: Outline 3.8*

### L051 | 3.0 | Hard
A binding receipt differs from a conditional receipt because it:
- A) Requires no premium
- B) Provides coverage regardless of insurability until the insurer rejects the application
- C) Is the standard receipt in life insurance
- D) Only applies after the policy is delivered
**Answer: B** — A binding receipt covers the applicant regardless of insurability until formal rejection; it is rare in life insurance.
*Citation: Outline 3.8*

### L052 | 3.0 | Easy
The primary source of underwriting information is the:
- A) Attending physician statement
- B) Application
- C) MIB report
- D) Inspection report
**Answer: B** — The application is the starting point and primary source; Part III is the agent's report, not shown to the applicant.
*Citation: Outline 3.9*

### L053 | 3.0 | Medium
Which statement about the Medical Information Bureau is correct?
- A) An insurer may decline an applicant solely on MIB data
- B) The MIB is notified when an application is declined
- C) An insurer may not decline solely on MIB data, and the MIB is not notified of declinations
- D) MIB data is shared publicly
**Answer: C** — MIB is an information exchange, not a decision-maker; insurers cannot decline on it alone and do not report declinations to it.
*Citation: Outline 3.9*

### L054 | 3.0 | Medium
Before an insurer may screen a blood sample for HIV, Massachusetts requires the producer to obtain:
- A) A court order
- B) A signed informed-consent form from the applicant
- C) Approval from the MIB
- D) A physician's referral
**Answer: B** — HIV testing requires the applicant's signed consent even when blood testing is routine; sexual orientation cannot be used in classification.
*Citation: 211 CMR 36.03; Outline 3.9*

### L055 | 3.0 | Easy
An applicant with chronic health conditions who is issued coverage at a higher-than-normal premium is classified as:
- A) Preferred
- B) Standard
- C) Substandard
- D) Declined
**Answer: C** — A substandard (rated) risk is issued with a rated-up premium, exclusion rider, or modified benefits.
*Citation: Outline 3.9*

### L056 | 3.0 | Hard
Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, when an insurer uses an investigative consumer report, the applicant:
- A) Has no right to know
- B) Must be notified in writing and may request a copy
- C) Must pay for the report
- D) Waives all privacy rights
**Answer: B** — FCRA requires written notice and gives the applicant the right to a copy of the report.
*Citation: Outline 3.9*
