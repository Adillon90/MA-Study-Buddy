### L087 | 2.0 | Easy
A peril is best defined as the:
- A) Condition that increases the chance of loss
- B) Cause of a loss
- C) Uncertainty of loss
- D) Financial value of a loss
**Answer: B** — A peril is the cause of loss; a hazard is a condition that increases the chance of loss.
*Citation: Outline 2.1*

### L088 | 2.0 | Medium
A mutual insurance company is owned by its policyholders and issues policies that are:
- A) Nonparticipating
- B) Participating, eligible for policy dividends
- C) Assessable only
- D) Variable only
**Answer: B** — Mutual companies issue participating policies that may pay policy dividends (a nontaxable return of premium).
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### L089 | 2.0 | Medium
An insurer incorporated in another U.S. state but doing business in Massachusetts is, in MA, a(n):
- A) Domestic insurer
- B) Foreign insurer
- C) Alien insurer
- D) Admitted insurer
**Answer: B** — Foreign = another state; alien = another country; domestic = this state.
*Citation: Outline 2.2*

### L090 | 2.0 | Hard
A consumer reasonably believes an agent has authority because the insurer left rate books and signage in place. This is:
- A) Express authority
- B) Implied authority
- C) Apparent authority
- D) Fiduciary authority
**Answer: C** — Apparent authority arises from the appearance the insurer allows; the insurer can be bound by it.
*Citation: Outline 2.3*

### L091 | 2.0 | Easy
Life insurance is classified as which type of contract because it pays a stated sum rather than reimbursing an actual loss?
- A) Indemnity
- B) Valued
- C) Conditional
- D) Unilateral
**Answer: B** — Life insurance is a valued contract, paying a stated face amount, unlike indemnity (health/property) contracts.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*

### L092 | 2.0 | Medium
A statement on an application that the applicant believes is true to the best of their knowledge is a:
- A) Warranty
- B) Representation
- C) Concealment
- D) Waiver
**Answer: B** — Application statements are representations, which void coverage only if a misstatement is material; warranties are guaranteed true.
*Citation: Outline 2.4*
