### L093 | 8.0 | Easy
A qualified retirement plan receives favorable tax treatment because it:
- A) Is offered only to executives
- B) Meets IRS guidelines
- C) Is funded with after-tax dollars
- D) Has no contribution limits
**Answer: B** — A qualified plan meets IRS rules, so contributions are deductible and growth is tax-deferred.
*Citation: Outline 8.1*

### L094 | 8.0 | Medium
In a qualified plan, distributions taken in retirement are:
- A) Tax-free
- B) Taxed as ordinary income
- C) Taxed as capital gains
- D) Subject to a 6% excise tax
**Answer: B** — The tax break is front-loaded: deductible going in, so distributions are taxed as ordinary income.
*Citation: Outline 8.2*

### L095 | 8.0 | Medium
A retirement plan in which a benefit formula sets the guaranteed retirement benefit is a:
- A) Defined contribution plan
- B) Defined benefit plan
- C) SEP
- D) SIMPLE plan
**Answer: B** — A defined benefit plan fixes the payout by formula; the employer bears the funding risk.
*Citation: Outline 8.1*

### L096 | 8.0 | Hard
A 403(b) tax-sheltered annuity is available specifically to:
- A) Self-employed individuals
- B) Employees of public schools, certain nonprofits, and some ministers
- C) Owners of small corporations
- D) Federal employees only
**Answer: B** — The 403(b)/TSA is for public-school, qualifying tax-exempt, and minister employees.
*Citation: Outline 8.3*

### L097 | 8.0 | Medium
A retirement plan designed to fund the retirement of self-employed individuals is a:
- A) 401(k)
- B) Keogh (HR-10) plan
- C) 403(b)
- D) SEP
**Answer: B** — Keogh (HR-10) plans fund retirement for self-employed individuals and unincorporated businesses.
*Citation: Outline 8.3*

### L098 | 8.0 | Easy
The federal law that sets minimum participation, vesting, and fiduciary standards for private pension plans is:
- A) HIPAA
- B) ERISA
- C) COBRA
- D) FINRA
**Answer: B** — ERISA (1974) sets the minimum standards for most private-industry retirement plans.
*Citation: Outline 8.1*
