### L001 | 5.0 | Easy
The grace period for a Massachusetts life insurance policy is:
- A) 7 days
- B) 10 days
- C) 30 days
- D) 31 days
**Answer: C** — MA sets a 30-day grace period for any premium after the first year; coverage stays in force during it.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(1)*

### L002 | 5.0 | Easy
A Massachusetts life policy becomes incontestable after it has been in force during the insured's lifetime for:
- A) 6 months
- B) 1 year
- C) 2 years
- D) 3 years
**Answer: C** — After two years the insurer can no longer contest the policy except for nonpayment of premium.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(2)*

### L003 | 5.0 | Easy
Under the entire contract provision, the documents that make up the contract are the policy and:
- A) The agent's report
- B) The application
- C) The buyer's guide
- D) The MIB report
**Answer: B** — The policy plus the attached application form the entire contract; nothing outside them applies.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(3)*

### L004 | 5.0 | Medium
A policyowner replaces an existing life policy with a new one. Under MA law, the right to return the new policy for an unconditional refund must be exercisable within:
- A) 10 days
- B) 14 days
- C) 20 days
- D) 30 days
**Answer: C** — A replacement carries a 20-day free look, longer than the 10-day window for small individual policies.
*Citation: 211 CMR 34.06*

### L005 | 5.0 | Medium
The 10-day free look under M.G.L. c. 175 § 187H attaches specifically to individual life insurance with a face amount:
- A) Of any size
- B) Under $25,000
- C) Over $100,000
- D) Issued to seniors only
**Answer: B** — The statutory 10-day right to examine applies to individual life under $25,000 of face amount.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 187H*

### L006 | 5.0 | Medium
An insured understated her age on the application. At death the insurer discovers the error. The insurer will:
- A) Deny the claim entirely
- B) Pay the full face amount anyway
- C) Pay what the premium would have purchased at her correct age
- D) Void the policy and refund premiums
**Answer: C** — Misstatement of age adjusts the benefit to what the premium paid would have bought at the true age; it never voids.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(4)*

### L007 | 5.0 | Medium
A Massachusetts life policy may be reinstated after lapse within how long, with evidence of insurability and back premiums at interest not exceeding 6%?
- A) 1 year
- B) 2 years
- C) 3 years
- D) 5 years
**Answer: C** — Reinstatement is allowed within three years of default on those conditions.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(11)*

### L008 | 5.0 | Hard
All of the following are required to reinstate a lapsed MA life policy EXCEPT:
- A) Evidence of insurability
- B) Payment of overdue premiums
- C) Interest not exceeding 6% per annum on overdue premiums
- D) A new two-year incontestability period waiver
**Answer: D** — Reinstatement requires insurability plus back premiums with interest up to 6%; no waiver of the contestability clock is required (a fresh contestable period may actually apply).
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(11)*

### L009 | 5.0 | Easy
The nonforfeiture option that uses the cash value to buy term coverage equal to the net death benefit, and which is typically automatic, is:
- A) Reduced paid-up
- B) Extended term
- C) Cash surrender
- D) Automatic premium loan
**Answer: B** — Extended term insurance is the standard automatic nonforfeiture option.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 144; Outline 5.4*

### L010 | 5.0 | Medium
Nonforfeiture values on a Massachusetts life policy become available once premiums have been paid for at least:
- A) 1 full year
- B) 2 full years
- C) 3 full years
- D) 5 full years
**Answer: C** — Both nonforfeiture options and policy loans require three full years of premiums.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 144; § 142*

### L011 | 5.0 | Medium
A policyowner assigns her life policy to a bank only as security for a loan, keeping the rest of her rights. This is:
- A) An absolute assignment
- B) A collateral assignment
- C) A beneficiary change
- D) A spendthrift clause
**Answer: B** — A collateral assignment is partial and temporary; on death the creditor is reimbursed and the beneficiary keeps the excess.
*Citation: Outline 5.1*

### L012 | 5.0 | Hard
Under a collateral assignment, if the insured dies with $200,000 of coverage and a $30,000 loan balance owed to the assignee, the beneficiary receives:
- A) $0
- B) $30,000
- C) $170,000
- D) $200,000
**Answer: C** — The creditor is reimbursed only the $30,000 owed; the beneficiary takes the remaining $170,000.
*Citation: Outline 5.1*

### L013 | 5.0 | Medium
A policy loan on a Massachusetts life policy may carry a fixed interest rate of no more than:
- A) 6%
- B) 8%
- C) 10%
- D) 12%
**Answer: B** — The statute caps a fixed policy-loan rate at 8% per annum (an adjustable rate is also permitted).
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 142*

### L014 | 5.0 | Hard
Which statement about dividend options is correct?
- A) Dividends are taxable as ordinary income when received
- B) Choosing a dividend option surrenders the policy
- C) Dividends are a return of premium, but interest earned on accumulated dividends is taxable
- D) Only nonparticipating policies pay dividends
**Answer: C** — Dividends are a nontaxable return of overpaid premium; only the interest they earn is taxable, and the policy stays in force.
*Citation: Outline 5.6*

### L015 | 5.0 | Easy
The waiver of premium rider pays benefits in the event the insured becomes:
- A) Unemployed
- B) Totally and permanently disabled
- C) Hospitalized for any reason
- D) Terminally ill
**Answer: B** — Waiver of premium covers total and permanent disability of the primary insured; it pays no cash to the owner.
*Citation: Outline 5.7*

### L016 | 5.0 | Medium
The spendthrift clause is distinctive because the settlement option is selected by:
- A) The beneficiary at the insured's death
- B) The owner at the time of application
- C) The insurer at claim time
- D) A court
**Answer: B** — The owner locks the settlement option at application, shielding proceeds from the beneficiary's creditors.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 119A; Outline 5.2*

### L017 | 5.0 | Medium
An accidental death and dismemberment rider pays the capital sum for:
- A) Loss of both hands
- B) Accidental death
- C) Loss of one hand
- D) Loss of sight in both eyes
**Answer: C** — The capital sum (half the face) is paid for a single loss; two losses pay the principal sum.
*Citation: Outline 5.10*

### L018 | 5.0 | Hard
Under 211 CMR 55, a Massachusetts life policy with an accelerated benefit provision may NOT:
- A) Reduce the death benefit by the amount accelerated
- B) Contain a pre-existing condition exclusion
- C) Require terminal illness as a trigger
- D) Be attached to a whole life policy
**Answer: B** — Accelerated benefit provisions cannot include pre-existing condition exclusions, and the carrier cannot restrict or recoup benefits except for fraud or material misrepresentation.
*Citation: 211 CMR 55*

### L019 | 5.0 | Easy
The Massachusetts Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association covers life insurance death benefits up to:
- A) $100,000
- B) $250,000
- C) $300,000
- D) $500,000
**Answer: C** — The death benefit limit is $300,000; net cash surrender value is covered to $100,000 and annuities to $250,000.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 146B*

### L020 | 5.0 | Medium
A whole life policy lapses for nonpayment. The owner does nothing. Coverage most likely continues automatically under:
- A) Reduced paid-up insurance
- B) Extended term insurance
- C) A policy loan she must request
- D) The grace period indefinitely
**Answer: B** — When no election is made, the extended term option is the automatic nonforfeiture default.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 144; Outline 5.4*
