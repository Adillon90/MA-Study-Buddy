### Q171 | 4.0 | Easy
Under M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(3), the grace period for a policy with an all-modes-other-than-weekly-or-monthly premium frequency is:
- A) 7 days
- B) 10 days
- C) 31 days
- D) 45 days
**Answer: C** — All premium modes other than weekly (7 days) or monthly (10 days) carry a 31-day grace period.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(3)*

### Q172 | 4.0 | Easy
A reinstated individual A&H policy covers which of the following immediately upon reinstatement?
- A) Sickness, but only after 10 days
- B) Accidents
- C) Both accidents and sickness
- D) Neither — a new waiting period applies to all claims
**Answer: B** — Reinstated policies cover accidents immediately; sickness is covered only if it begins more than 10 days after reinstatement.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(4)*

### Q173 | 4.0 | Easy
Under the Time of Payment of Claims provision, non-periodic benefits must be paid:
- A) Within 30 days of proof of loss
- B) Immediately upon receipt of written proof of loss
- C) Within 45 days to avoid the prompt-pay penalty
- D) At the next monthly cycle
**Answer: B** — Non-periodic claims are paid immediately upon receipt of written proof; periodic benefits (such as disability income) must be paid at least monthly.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(8)*

### Q174 | 4.0 | Easy
The insured may change the beneficiary at any time unless the designation is:
- A) Filed with the Commissioner
- B) Irrevocable
- C) Older than two years
- D) Named on the declarations page
**Answer: B** — Beneficiary consent is not required to change beneficiaries unless the designation is irrevocable.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(12)*

### Q175 | 4.0 | Easy
An accident-only policy in Massachusetts must carry what on its first page?
- A) The Commissioner's approval seal
- B) A bold notice that the policy covers accident only and pays nothing for sickness
- C) A list of all exclusions
- D) The producer's license number
**Answer: B** — Accident-only policies must carry a bold first-page notice warning that the policy covers accident only.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(9)*

### Q176 | 4.0 | Easy
The insuring clause in a health policy states:
- A) The exchange of consideration between the parties
- B) The insurer's basic promise: who is covered, against what perils, and for what period
- C) The renewability terms
- D) The list of required provisions
**Answer: B** — The insuring clause is the insurer's fundamental coverage promise — who, what perils, what period.
*Citation: Outline 4.3*

### Q177 | 4.0 | Medium
James lapsed his policy and had his conditional receipt date stamped April 3. The insurer sends a written disapproval on May 15. Is his policy reinstated?
- A) Yes — only a Commissioner order can prevent an automatic reinstatement
- B) No — the insurer disapproved in writing before the 45th day
- C) Yes — written disapproval is insufficient; the insurer must file with the Commissioner
- D) No — reinstatement requires new underwriting approval in all cases
**Answer: B** — The policy reinstates automatically on day 45 only if the insurer has NOT disapproved it in writing by then; a written disapproval before day 45 prevents automatic reinstatement.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(4)*

### Q178 | 4.0 | Medium
Premiums accepted at reinstatement may be applied to cover a lapse period no further back than:
- A) 30 days
- B) 60 days
- C) 90 days
- D) The date of the lapse
**Answer: B** — Premiums accepted at reinstatement can be applied no further back than 60 days before reinstatement.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(4)*

### Q179 | 4.0 | Medium
A disability income policy pays benefits monthly. How often may the insurer require proof of continuing disability?
- A) Every month
- B) Every 3 months
- C) Every 6 months
- D) Annually only
**Answer: C** — For continuing disability income benefits payable for at least 2 years, the insurer may require proof of continuing disability no more often than every 6 months.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(5)*

### Q180 | 4.0 | Medium
The insured's statements on an application are not binding on the insured unless:
- A) The policy has been in force for 2 years
- B) A copy of the application is attached to the policy
- C) The producer signed the application as a witness
- D) The insurer conducted an APS
**Answer: B** — Under § 108(5), the insured is not bound by application statements unless a copy of the application is attached to the policy.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(5)*

### Q181 | 4.0 | Medium
The optional Illegal Occupation provision means the insurer owes no liability if:
- A) The insured was injured at work
- B) A contributing cause of the loss was the insured committing or attempting a felony or engaged in an illegal occupation
- C) The insured failed to notify the insurer of a job change
- D) The insured was uninsured at the time of the loss
**Answer: B** — The Illegal Occupation provision excludes losses where the contributing cause was the insured's commission or attempted commission of a felony or work in an illegal trade.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(b)*

### Q182 | 4.0 | Medium
The optional Conformity with State Statutes provision means:
- A) The policy automatically adjusts to any future statute changes
- B) Policy provisions that conflict with state law are automatically amended to meet state minimums
- C) The policy is void if it conflicts with any regulation
- D) The insurer must re-file the policy form annually
**Answer: B** — Conflicting provisions are automatically amended to conform to state minimums — a self-healing clause.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(b)*

### Q183 | 4.0 | Medium
Under M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(5), a false statement on a health insurance application bars recovery only if it:
- A) Was made during the first two years of the policy
- B) Materially affected acceptance of the risk or the hazard assumed
- C) Involved the insured's age or occupation
- D) Was identified by the insurer within 30 days of the claim
**Answer: B** — Application misstatements bar recovery only when material to the risk accepted; the 2-year time-limit-on-defenses provision separately governs non-fraudulent errors.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(5)*

### Q184 | 4.0 | Medium
The optional Unpaid Premium provision allows the insurer to:
- A) Increase the premium for frequent claimants
- B) Deduct any premium due from a claim payment
- C) Cancel the policy for nonpayment outside the grace period
- D) Apply a lien against future benefits
**Answer: B** — The Unpaid Premium provision allows any premium due to be deducted from the claim payment at time of loss.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(b)*

### Q185 | 4.0 | Hard
A policyholder is on active military duty. Under the military suspense provision:
- A) The policy is permanently cancelled
- B) Coverage is suspended with a premium refund, and may be restored after service
- C) Benefits are reduced to 50% during active duty
- D) The beneficiary must elect COBRA
**Answer: B** — The military suspense provision suspends coverage during active duty with premium refunded, and allows restoration after service ends.
*Citation: Outline 4.3*

### Q186 | 4.0 | Hard
The optional Other Insurance in This Insurer provision applies when:
- A) The insured has coverage with a competing carrier
- B) The insured holds multiple A&H policies from the same insurer that together exceed the loss
- C) The insured fails to disclose other coverage
- D) The COB rules apply
**Answer: B** — This provision limits benefit-stacking when the insured holds multiple policies with the same insurer; excess coverage is void and excess premium is refunded.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(b)*

### Q187 | 4.0 | Hard
Three years after a health policy was issued, the insurer discovers the insured intentionally stated a false medical history on the application. Under the Time Limit on Certain Defenses provision:
- A) The policy cannot be voided — the 2-year period has passed
- B) The policy may still be voided because fraudulent misstatements are exempt from the 2-year bar
- C) The insurer may only reduce benefits, not void the policy
- D) The insurer must obtain Commissioner approval before voiding
**Answer: B** — After 2 years, only fraudulent misstatements may be used to void the policy or deny a claim; unintentional errors are barred.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(2)*

### Q188 | 4.0 | Hard
A guaranteed renewable policy may differ from a standard policy in which way regarding the Time Limit on Certain Defenses?
- A) The period is extended to 3 years — matching the legal action outer limit
- B) It may use an "Incontestable" version that drops even the fraud exception
- C) Fraud is never a basis for voiding a guaranteed renewable policy
- D) The provision does not apply to guaranteed renewable policies
**Answer: B** — A guaranteed renewable policy may use an Incontestable version of the provision that removes even the fraud exception after the period runs.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(2)*

### Q189 | 4.0 | Hard
Under the MA nonrenewal notice rule, if the insurer reserves the right to refuse renewal, it must mail nonrenewal notice at least ___ before the premium due date.
- A) 10 days
- B) 20 days
- C) 30 days
- D) 60 days
**Answer: C** — If the insurer has a right to refuse renewal, it must mail nonrenewal notice at least 30 days before the premium due date.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(3)*

### Q190 | 4.0 | Hard
The facility-of-payment clause allows the insurer to pay up to ___ to an equitably entitled blood relative when the named payee is an estate, a minor, or incompetent.
- A) $500
- B) $1,000
- C) $2,500
- D) $5,000
**Answer: B** — The facility-of-payment clause lets the insurer optionally pay up to $1,000 to a blood relative or relative by marriage who is equitably entitled, avoiding estate administration delay.
*Citation: M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(3)(a)(9)*
