## Part 1: Required standard provisions (M.G.L. c. 175 § 132)

Section 132 lists the provisions every MA life policy must contain. The exam lives on these numbers.

- **Grace period — 30 days.** A **30-day** grace period applies to any premium after the first year; coverage stays in force during it. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(1)]]
- **Incontestability — 2 years.** The policy becomes incontestable after it has been in force during the **insured's lifetime** for **two years** (except for nonpayment of premium). [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(2)]]
- **Entire contract.** The policy **and the application** together constitute the entire contract between the parties. Nothing outside those documents can be used against the insured. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(3)]]
- **Misstatement of age.** If the insured's age was stated wrong, the benefit is adjusted to **what the premium paid would have purchased at the correct age** (not a denial). [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(4)]]
- **Reinstatement — within 3 years.** A lapsed policy may be reinstated **within three years** of default by showing **evidence of insurability** and paying **all overdue premiums with interest not exceeding 6% per annum**. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 132(11)]]

## Part 2: Free look / right to examine

Massachusetts has **two** free-look windows, and the exam can test either:
- **Standard free look — 10 days.** Examine and return the policy for a **full premium refund**, after which the policy is void from the beginning; attaches to **individual life insurance with a face amount under $25,000**. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 187H]]
- **Replacement free look — 20 days.** When the policy **replaces** existing life insurance/annuity coverage, the applicant has the right to an **unconditional refund of all premiums** exercisable within **20 days** from the date of delivery. [[211 CMR 34.06]]

## Part 3: Nonforfeiture options (M.G.L. c. 175 § 144)

When a policy with cash value lapses, the owner does not forfeit that value. MA codifies the three standard nonforfeiture options.

- Available once premiums have been paid for at least **three full years**.
- The owner elects within **60 days** after the due date of the defaulted premium (and before the insured's death). [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 144]]
- **The three options:**
  - **Cash surrender value** — take the value in cash and end the policy.
  - **Extended term insurance** — use the cash value as a single premium to buy term coverage for the **same face amount** for as long as it will last (the **default/automatic** option in most policies).
  - **Reduced paid-up insurance** — use the cash value as a single premium to buy a **smaller, fully paid-up** permanent policy.
- **Children's term rider** is exempt from the minimum nonforfeiture rules when it **expires before the child reaches age 26**. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 144(7)(iv)]]
- **Accidental death benefit** amounts are **disregarded** when computing adjusted premiums and cash surrender values. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 144(7)(i)]]

## Part 4: Policy loan and withdrawal options (M.G.L. c. 175 § 142)

- A **policy loan** is available after premiums have been paid for at least **three full years**.
- **Minimum** loan amount: **$25**. The loan plus interest cannot exceed the cash surrender value at the end of the policy year; the insurer may deduct existing indebtedness and unpaid premiums.
- **Interest:** a fixed rate of **not more than 8% per annum**, or an adjustable rate tied to the published corporate-bond average (reviewed at least annually, changed only when the rate moves more than ½%). [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 142]]
- **Automatic premium loan** — an optional provision that automatically pays an overdue premium out of the cash value to prevent lapse.

## Part 5: Guaranty Association (M.G.L. c. 175 § 146B)

The **Massachusetts Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association** pays benefits and continues coverage when a member insurer becomes insolvent, up to verified statutory limits:
- **Life insurance death benefit:** up to **$300,000**.
- **Life insurance net cash surrender / withdrawal value:** up to **$100,000**.
- **Annuity present value (incl. cash surrender):** up to **$250,000**.
- **Aggregate cap:** **$300,000** per individual across most coverage types ($500,000 for basic hospital/medical/major medical).
- **Gag rule:** no insurer, agent, or affiliate may use the Association's **existence as a sales inducement** in any advertisement or solicitation. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 146B]]

## Part 6: Replacement of life insurance (211 CMR 34)

When a sale replaces existing coverage, MA imposes duties to protect the buyer (so the **20-day** free look in Part 2 has teeth):
- The regulation sets **minimum standards of conduct** in replacement transactions: the agent must identify the replacement, the replacing insurer must give the buyer the information needed to decide in their own interest, and misrepresentation/incomplete disclosure is curtailed. [[211 CMR 34]]
- Structured in sections **34.01–34.09** (purpose, definitions, exemptions, agent/broker duties, insurer duties, direct-response duties, penalties).

## Part 7: Accelerated / living benefits (211 CMR 55)

- A policy with an accelerated benefit provision **may not contain pre-existing condition exclusions**.
- A carrier **may not restrict** the use of the accelerated benefit, nor **recoup** benefits paid, **except** in contestable cases of **material misrepresentation, fraud, or criminal misconduct**.
- For qualifying events **not** due to accidental injury, the provision must be effective **no more than 30 days** after the policy/rider effective date (unless the insured chose an elimination period for chronic-illness-only coverage). [[211 CMR 55]]

## Part 8: Other verified MA provisions

- **Group life assignment** — an insured under a **group** life policy may **assign any or all incidents of ownership** (right to name a beneficiary, to have an individual policy issued, to pay premiums), subject to the policy's terms. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 134C]]
- **Protection of retained proceeds** — proceeds left with the insurer (and interest on them) are **shielded from the beneficiary's creditors**, debts, and contracts; this is a creditor-protection rule, not an interest-rate mandate. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 119A]]

## Drill card (verified MA values)
- Grace **30 days**; Incontestable after **2 years**; Reinstatement within **3 years** (evidence of insurability + overdue premiums at interest **≤6%**).
- Misstatement of age = benefit the premium **would have bought at the correct age**.
- Free look: **10 days** (individual life under **$25,000** face, 175:187H) vs. **20 days** on a **replacement** (211 CMR 34.06).
- Nonforfeiture available after **3 full years**, elect within **60 days**: cash surrender / extended term (automatic) / reduced paid-up.
- Policy loan after **3 full years**, minimum **$25**, fixed interest **≤8%**.

## Exam traps
- *Grace is **30 days**, reinstatement is **3 years**, incontestability is **2 years** — three different clocks the exam loves to swap.*
- *Misstatement of age **adjusts** the benefit, it does not void the policy.* Same for the incontestability clause after 2 years.
- *Extended term is the **automatic** nonforfeiture option; reduced paid-up must usually be elected.*
- *Two free looks: the 175:187H **10-day** window (small <$25k individual life) vs. the **20-day** window on any **replacement** (211 CMR 34.06). The exam picks whichever fact pattern mentions replacing existing coverage.*
- *Nonforfeiture and policy loans both require **3 full years** of premiums — easy to misremember as 1 or 2.*
