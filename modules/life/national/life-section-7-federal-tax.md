# Life Producer Exam — National Core
## Module L7: Federal Tax Considerations for Life Insurance & Annuities
**Scope: national/federal (applies to all states) | Maps to outline Section 7.0 — 7% (7 of 100 items) | Source: verified study notes (RN/KW Policies, Provisions, Annuities, Retirement Accounts — green/must-know). Cross-references §4.0, §5.0, §6.0**

> **How to study this module:** Life insurance gets favorable tax treatment as long as it stays "life insurance." The exam tests the boundaries: when money comes out tax-free (death benefit, return of premium/dividends), when it's tax-deferred (cash value growth), and when the favorable treatment is **lost** (MECs) or **partly taxed** (annuity earnings via the exclusion ratio). Anchor on "is this a return of my own money, or is it earnings?"

---

## Part 1: Taxation of personal life insurance

- **Death benefit** — generally **income-tax-free** to the beneficiary when paid as a lump sum. (If left with the insurer to earn interest, the **interest portion** of settlement payments is taxable.)
- **Cash value growth** — accumulates **tax-deferred** while the policy is in force.
- **Dividends** — a **return of overpaid premium**, so **not taxable**; but **interest earned** on dividends left to accumulate **is** taxable.
- **Policy loans** — **not taxable** while the policy stays in force (you're borrowing your own cash value).
- **Surrender** — taxable only on the **gain** (cash value received above total premiums paid).

## Part 2: Modified Endowment Contracts (MECs)

- A life policy **overfunded** beyond IRS limits becomes a **MEC**. A policy that **fails the 7-pay test** (more paid in during the first **7 years** than the limit) is a MEC and **loses** favorable living-benefit tax treatment. Once a MEC, always a MEC.
- MEC distributions are taxed **LIFO** — earnings come out first and are taxed as ordinary income.
- Distributions (including **loans**) before age **59½** carry a **10% penalty**.
- The **death benefit** of a MEC remains income-tax-free; only **lifetime** distributions are affected.

## Part 3: Taxation of non-qualified annuities

- Funded with **after-tax** dollars; growth is **tax-deferred** during accumulation.
- **Exclusion ratio** — each annuity payment is split into a **tax-free return of principal** and a **taxable earnings** portion: investment in the contract ÷ expected return.
- Premature distributions (before **59½**) face a **10% penalty** on the taxable portion; like MECs, non-qualified annuity withdrawals are generally **LIFO** (earnings first).

## Part 4: IRAs

- **Traditional IRA** — contributions may be **tax-deductible**; growth is **tax-deferred**; **distributions are taxed** as ordinary income.
- **Roth IRA** — funded with **after-tax** income; **earnings and qualified withdrawals are tax-free**. A **qualified** Roth withdrawal requires the funds be held **at least 5 years** AND one of: age **59½**, death, permanent disability, or first-home purchase (**$10,000** lifetime max).
- A withdrawal that exceeds total contributions without meeting the criteria is **nonqualified** — the earnings become taxable.

## Part 5: Rollovers, transfers, and 1035 exchanges

- **Rollover** — moving funds from one IRA or qualified plan into another after terminating the first; done correctly it preserves tax deferral.
- **Section 1035 exchange** — exchange **without recognizing gain**: **life → life**, **life → annuity**, **endowment → annuity**, **annuity → annuity**. You **cannot** exchange an **annuity into a life** policy. Lets a policyowner move cash value between contracts tax-free.

---

## Drill card (the testable facts)
- Death benefit = **income-tax-free**; cash value growth = **tax-deferred**; dividends = **not taxable** (but accumulated **interest is**); policy loans = **not taxable** in force.
- MEC = fails **7-pay test**, taxed **LIFO**, **10%** penalty before **59½**; death benefit still tax-free.
- Non-qualified annuity = **exclusion ratio** (investment ÷ expected return) splits tax-free principal from taxable earnings.
- Traditional IRA = deductible in, **taxed out**. Roth = after-tax in, **tax-free out** (5-year hold + 59½/death/disability/first home $10k).
- **1035**: life→life, life→annuity, annuity→annuity OK; **annuity→life NOT** OK.

## Exam traps
- *MEC and non-qualified annuity distributions are both **LIFO** (earnings taxed first) with a **10%** pre-59½ penalty — life insurance that **isn't** a MEC is FIFO-friendly (basis first).*
- *Dividends themselves aren't taxed (return of premium), but the **interest** they earn is.*
- *1035 is one-way into annuities: you can roll life into an annuity, never an annuity back into life.*
- *Roth qualified withdrawal needs **both** the 5-year clock **and** a qualifying event — meeting only age 59½ without 5 years still isn't fully qualified.*
