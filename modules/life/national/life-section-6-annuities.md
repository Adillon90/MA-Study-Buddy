# Life Producer Exam — National Core
## Module L6: Annuities
**Scope: national (applies to all states) | Maps to outline Section 6.0 — 14% (14 of 100 items) | Source: verified study notes (KW Annuities, green/must-know) | MA touchpoints: deferred/fixed general-account 175:144A½, variable annuities 175:132FGH, 211 CMR 95 — see MA overlay**

> **How to study this module:** An annuity is the **opposite** of life insurance: life insurance protects against dying too soon; an annuity protects against living too long (outliving your money). Two axes define every annuity: **when it pays** (immediate vs. deferred) and **how the money is invested** (fixed vs. variable). Then there's a menu of **payout options** that trade lifetime income against guarantees to beneficiaries. Master those three layers and the scenarios resolve.

---

## Part 1: The two periods and the parties

- **Accumulation period** — premiums paid in are credited as **accumulation units**; the money grows tax-deferred.
- **Annuity (payout) period** — accumulation units convert to **annuity units** and income is paid out.
- **Parties:** the **owner** (pays premiums, controls the contract), the **annuitant** (the measuring life whose lifespan governs payout), and the **beneficiary** (receives any death-related proceeds).

## Part 2: When it pays — immediate vs. deferred

- **Immediate annuity** — income begins within about **one month** of purchase; can only be bought with a **single premium** (SPIA).
- **Deferred annuity** — payout postponed until a later date or age; income does not begin for **at least one year**. Bought with **single premium** or **flexible (periodic) premiums**.

## Part 3: How it's invested — fixed vs. variable

- **Fixed annuity** — **guaranteed** minimum interest rate, declared in advance; income is a level dollar amount. The **investment risk is on the insurer**; money sits in the **general account**.
- **Variable annuity** — payments **fluctuate** with the performance of the **separate account** (common stocks). The **investment risk shifts to the owner**; **no guaranteed payment amount**. Because it is a **security**, the producer must hold a **FINRA** registration **and** a state life license.
- **Equity-indexed annuity** — a **fixed** deferred annuity with a guaranteed minimum rate **plus** excess interest tied to an external **equities index**. (Indexed, not variable — keeps the floor.)
- **Market value adjusted (MVA) annuity** — fixed guarantees plus an interest adjustment that applies **only if surrendered early**, before the contract period ends; otherwise behaves like a fixed annuity.

## Part 4: Payout (benefit) options

Ordered roughly from highest income to most beneficiary protection:
- **Straight life (pure life)** — income for the annuitant's lifetime, then **stops**. **Highest** monthly income, **nothing** to a beneficiary.
- **Life with period certain** — lifetime income, but guarantees payments for a **minimum period** (e.g., 10 years); if the annuitant dies within it, the **beneficiary** collects the remainder of the period.
- **Cash refund / installment refund** — if the annuitant dies before payments equal the purchase price, the **excess** is paid to the beneficiary (cash refund = lump sum).
- **Period certain** — payments for a **set number of years** only (not tied to lifespan).
- **Joint and survivor** — covers **two lives**; income continues to the survivor for life. **Full** survivor = same amount; **two-thirds** or **one-half** survivor options reduce the survivor's payment.

## Part 5: Taxation snapshot (national)

- **Exclusion ratio** — the fraction of each annuity payment that is **tax-free return of principal**: investment in the contract ÷ expected return. The earnings portion is taxed as ordinary income.
- **1035 exchange** — you can exchange life → annuity, endowment → annuity, or annuity → annuity **without** recognizing gain. You **cannot** exchange an **annuity into a life** policy. (Full tax detail in the Federal Tax module.)

## Drill card (the testable facts)
- Annuity = protection against **outliving your money** (opposite of life insurance).
- Accumulation units → annuity units at payout. Owner / annuitant / beneficiary.
- Immediate = single premium, pays within ~**1 month**. Deferred = single or flexible, pays after ≥**1 year**.
- Fixed = insurer bears risk, general account. Variable = owner bears risk, separate account, **FINRA + license**.
- Equity-indexed = fixed annuity with index-linked excess interest **and a floor**. MVA = adjustment **only on early surrender**.
- Straight life = most income, nothing to beneficiary. Life with period certain / cash refund / J&S add beneficiary protection.
- Exclusion ratio = investment ÷ expected return (the tax-free slice). 1035: life→annuity OK, annuity→life NOT OK.

## Exam traps
- *Variable annuity shifts investment risk to the **owner** and requires a **securities** registration; the fixed annuity keeps risk on the insurer.*
- *Equity-indexed is a **fixed** annuity (has a guaranteed floor), not a variable one* — a classic trap.
- *Straight life pays the **most** per month precisely because it guarantees **nothing** to a beneficiary.*
- *1035 exchanges are one-directional into annuities; you can roll a life policy into an annuity but never an annuity back into life insurance.*
- *The **annuitant**, not the owner, is the measuring life that determines payout.*
