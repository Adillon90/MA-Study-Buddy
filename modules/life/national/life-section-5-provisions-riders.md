# Life Producer Exam — National Core
## Module L5: Policy Provisions, Options & Riders
**Scope: national (applies to all states) | Maps to outline Section 5.0 — 24%, the largest section (24 of 100 items) | Source: verified study notes (RN/KW Provisions, Options and Riders, green/must-know) | MA statute values are in the MA overlay (`life-MA-section-5-provisions.md`)**

> **How to study this module:** This is the biggest section of the exam. It splits into four buckets: provisions tied to the **contract** (entire contract, incontestable, misstatement), provisions tied to **cash value** (nonforfeiture, loans), provisions tied to **proceeds** (beneficiaries, settlement, dividends), and **riders**. Learn the universal concept here; the exact MA day-counts (grace, reinstatement, free look) live in the MA overlay. Watch the three look-alike pairs the exam loves: nonforfeiture vs. dividend options, absolute vs. collateral assignment, waiver of premium vs. accelerated benefit.

## Part 1: Standard contract provisions

- **Entire contract** — the policy **plus the application** are the whole contract; nothing outside them counts. Changes require an **executive officer's** endorsement; an agent cannot alter it.
- **Insuring clause** — the insurer's basic **promise to pay** the stated benefit on a covered loss. All insurer promises live here.
- **Consideration clause** — the owner's consideration is **the completed application + the initial premium**; it states the amount and frequency of payments.
- **Incontestable clause** — after the policy has been in force a set period (typically **2 years**), the insurer can no longer contest it for misstatements (except nonpayment of premium). Protects beneficiaries from delayed denials.
- **Misstatement of age or sex** — the insurer **adjusts the benefit** to what the premium would have purchased at the **correct** age; it does **not** void the policy. Older-than-stated → benefit reduced; younger → benefit increased.
- **Owner's rights** — the owner names/changes beneficiaries, selects options, and receives living benefits.
- **Assignment** — the right to transfer policy rights:
  - **Absolute assignment** — complete, **irrevocable** transfer of all ownership to the assignee.
  - **Collateral assignment** — **partial, temporary** transfer to a creditor as security for a debt. On death, the creditor is reimbursed for the amount owed; the **beneficiary gets the excess**.
- **Free look** — a set number of days after **delivery** to return the policy for a **full refund** (MA value in overlay).
- **Grace period** — time after the due date during which coverage continues; if the insured dies in it, the benefit is paid **minus the premium owed** (MA value in overlay).
- **Reinstatement** — restores a lapsed policy on **proof of insurability** + payment of back premiums, outstanding loans, and interest. If the insurer takes no action on a reinstatement application for **45 days**, it is reinstated automatically. (MA time limit in overlay.)
- **Physical exam and autopsy** — the insurer may, at its expense, examine the insured during a claim and order an autopsy **unless forbidden by state law**.

## Part 2: Cash-value provisions and options

### Nonforfeiture options (you don't lose the cash value)
- **Cash surrender** — take the cash value; coverage ends. The insurer may **defer payment up to 6 months** (delayed payment provision).
- **Extended term** — use the cash value as a single premium to buy term coverage **equal to the net death benefit** for as long as it lasts. Usually the **automatic** option.
- **Reduced paid-up** — use the cash value to buy a **smaller, fully paid-up** permanent policy; no more premiums.

### Loans
- **Policy loan** — available after the policy builds cash value (in most states after **3 years**). Loans plus interest cannot exceed the guaranteed cash value. **Policy loans are not taxable.** Unpaid loans + interest are **deducted from the death benefit**.
- **Automatic premium loan (APL)** — automatically borrows from cash value to pay an overdue premium by the end of the grace period, preventing lapse. No fee, just interest. Continues until cash value is exhausted.

## Part 3: Proceeds — beneficiaries, settlement, dividends

- **Beneficiary designation / change** — the owner names who receives proceeds and may change it any time **unless the beneficiary is irrevocable**.
- **Settlement options** — how proceeds are paid out; usually the **beneficiary** chooses at the insured's death. The **spendthrift clause** lets the **owner** lock the settlement option at application (protects proceeds from the beneficiary's creditors).
- **Dividend options** (participating policies only; dividends are a **return of overcharged premium → not taxable**, but **accumulated interest is taxable**):
  - **Cash** — take it.
  - **Reduced premium** — apply to next premium.
  - **Accumulate at interest** — leave it to earn interest (interest taxable).
  - **Paid-up additions** — buy small amounts of single-premium whole life.
  - **One-year term** — buy one-year term protection.
  - *Key contrast:* with a **dividend** option the policy **stays active**; with a **nonforfeiture** option you are surrendering/lapsing.

## Part 4: Riders

- **Waiver of premium** — waives premiums during **total and permanent disability** (after a waiting period, e.g., **90 days/6 months**). Covers the **primary insured only**, pays **no cash**, voids after a set age (60/65) unless already in use.
- **Accidental death benefit (ADB / double indemnity)** — pays a **multiple** of the face (double/triple) if death is **accidental**. Cheap way to add coverage.
- **Accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D)** — pays the **principal sum** (full face) for loss of two limbs/both eyes; **capital sum (½ face)** for one. One hand + one foot = **100%**.
- **Guaranteed insurability option (GIO)** — buy more permanent coverage at set dates or life events (marriage, birth) **without evidence of insurability**, usually every **3 years** up to the original face.
- **Cost of living (COLA)** — adjusts the face for inflation via the **CPI**.
- **Payor provision** — on a **juvenile** policy, waives premiums until the child reaches a set age (18/21/25) if the **payor** dies or is disabled.
- **Return of premium** — pays face **plus all premiums paid** if death occurs within a set period (it's increasing term under the hood).
- **Term riders / other-insured riders** — add level term coverage on spouse or children. A **child term rider** is **one flat premium regardless of the number of children** and pays the **parent** (beneficiary), not the child.

## Part 5: Living benefits

- **Accelerated benefits rider** — pays part of the death benefit early on a **terminal illness** (expected death within ~1–2 years); whatever is taken **reduces the death benefit**.
- **Long-term care rider** — a type of accelerated rider paying out for confinement/inability to perform **2+ activities of daily living** (eating, dressing, bathing, toileting/continence, transferring, ambulation).

## Part 6: Exclusions

Common life exclusions: **war/military service**, **aviation** (high-risk: stunt/student/military pilot), **hazardous occupation or hobby**, **criminal activity/felony**, **intoxicants/narcotics**.
- **Suicide clause** — no death benefit if suicide occurs within a set period (typically **2 years**) of issue; premiums are usually refunded. Protects the insurer against purchase in contemplation of suicide.

## Drill card (the testable facts)
- Entire contract = policy + application; only an **executive officer** can change it.
- Incontestable after **~2 years**; misstatement of age **adjusts** (never voids).
- Absolute assignment = full + irrevocable; collateral = partial, to a creditor, beneficiary gets the excess.
- Nonforfeiture: cash surrender / **extended term (automatic)** / reduced paid-up. Cash payout deferrable **6 months**.
- Policy loans not taxable; unpaid loans reduce the death benefit. **APL** prevents lapse from cash value.
- Spendthrift clause = **owner** picks the settlement option at application.
- Dividends = return of premium, **not taxable**; only **accumulated interest** is taxable. Policy stays active.
- Waiver of premium = total & permanent disability, primary insured only, no cash. GIO = more coverage, no evidence, every **3 years**.
- AD&D: principal sum (full) for two losses, capital sum (½) for one.
- Suicide clause typically **2 years**; accelerated benefits = terminal illness, reduces death benefit.

## Exam traps
- *Dividend options vs. nonforfeiture options:* dividends keep the policy **in force**; nonforfeiture means you are **leaving**.
- *Misstatement of age adjusts the benefit; the incontestable clause bars contest after 2 years — neither one voids a valid policy.*
- *Waiver of premium pays nothing to the owner and covers only the primary insured* — it is not disability income and not the payor rider.
- *Extended term is the **automatic** nonforfeiture choice; reduced paid-up is **elected**.*
- *Collateral assignee is reimbursed only up to the debt; the beneficiary keeps the rest.*
