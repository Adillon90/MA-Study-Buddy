# Life Producer Exam — National Core
## Module L8: Qualified Plans
**Scope: national/federal (applies to all states) | Maps to outline Section 8.0 — 6% (6 of 100 items) | Source: verified study notes (KW Retirement Accounts, green/must-know)**

> **How to study this module:** A "qualified" plan is one that meets **IRS** rules and therefore gets **favorable tax treatment** (deductible contributions, tax-deferred growth). The exam tests the trade-off baked into that tax break: money goes in pre-tax and grows untaxed, so **distributions are taxed as ordinary income**, and early withdrawals are penalized. Know the plan-type roster and who each one is for.

---

## Part 1: What makes a plan "qualified"

- A **qualified plan** is an employer-established retirement plan meeting **IRS guidelines**, which earns **favorable tax treatment**: employer contributions are **deductible**, and earnings grow **tax-deferred**.
- **ERISA (1974)** sets minimum standards (participation, vesting, funding, fiduciary duties) for most private-industry pension and welfare plans.
- The tax bargain: contributions and growth are untaxed going in, so **distributions are taxed as ordinary income** coming out, and early withdrawals generally carry a penalty.

## Part 2: Two structural types

- **Defined benefit plan** — a **benefit formula** sets the guaranteed retirement benefit (e.g., based on salary and years of service); the employer bears the funding risk.
- **Defined contribution plan** — a **contribution formula** sets what goes in; the eventual benefit **varies** with contributions and investment results (the employee bears the risk).

## Part 3: Plan types and who they're for

- **SEP (Simplified Employee Pension)** — the **employer** contributes to an IRA the **employee** owns; simple plan for small businesses.
- **Keogh (HR-10)** — funds retirement for **self-employed** individuals and unincorporated businesses.
- **Profit-sharing plan** — the employer sets aside a share of **profits** for distribution to qualifying employees.
- **401(k)** — employer-sponsored; employees defer **pre-tax** salary, taxed only at withdrawal (often with an employer match).
- **403(b) / TSA (tax-sheltered annuity)** — for employees of **public schools, certain tax-exempt organizations, and some ministers**.
- **SIMPLE** (Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees) — a tax-favored plan letting **small employers** set up employee retirement savings with matching.

## Drill card (the testable facts)
- Qualified = meets **IRS** rules → contributions **deductible**, growth **tax-deferred**, **distributions taxed** as ordinary income.
- **ERISA** sets participation/vesting/funding/fiduciary standards.
- **Defined benefit** = formula sets the benefit (employer risk); **defined contribution** = formula sets the input (employee risk).
- SEP = employer funds employee's IRA. Keogh/HR-10 = self-employed. 401(k) = pre-tax salary deferral. 403(b)/TSA = schools/nonprofits/ministers. SIMPLE = small employers.

## Exam traps
- *Defined **benefit** fixes the payout; defined **contribution** fixes only the input and lets the payout float.*
- *403(b)/TSA is specifically for public-school/nonprofit/minister employees* — a common "who qualifies" trap.
- *The qualified-plan tax break is front-loaded: deductible in, taxed out. Distributions are ordinary income, not tax-free.*
