# California Overlay — Life-Only Agent + Accident & Health Agent
**Scope: California-specific statute layer | Exams: TWO separate PSI exams — Life-Only Agent (75 scored questions) and Accident & Health or Sickness Agent (75 scored questions); a combined Life + A&H exam (150 questions) is also offered. Testing vendor: PSI Services, LLC for the California Department of Insurance (CDI). Source: California Insurance Code (Cal. Ins. Code / "CIC") + 10 CCR, verified via leginfo.legislature.ca.gov, the CDI prelicensing objectives, and CLHIGA (califega.org). Pairs with the national Life and A&H cores.**

> **How this layer works:** California's exams map onto the national Life and A&H cores we already built. This overlay carries only the California-specific values that differ by state. Every number below was verified against the CIC text, the CDI exam content outline, or CLHIGA, not a study guide. Where CA matches the NAIC standard, that is noted. CA has several genuinely distinctive rules (an **elected** commissioner, an **80%** guaranty-association haircut, automatic beneficiary revocation on divorce) flagged as traps.

## Section 1: Insurance Regulation (California)
- **Elected Insurance Commissioner.** California is one of the states that **elects** its Insurance Commissioner (a statewide elected office since 1991, created by Proposition 103); 4-year term (Cal. Ins. Code § 12900). The Commissioner heads the CDI and enforces the Code. *Distinctive: most states appoint; CA elects.*
- **Continuing education: 24 hours per 2-year license term**, including **3 hours of ethics** (Cal. Ins. Code §§ 1749.3, 1749.33). General excess hours carry over (up to 24), but excess ethics hours carry as general credit only.
- **Ethics CE detail (CDI, verified):** the **3 ethics hours are PART OF the 24, not on top of them**, and are required **every** 2-year renewal, **including for newly licensed agents**. **Prelicensing** code-and-ethics training does **not** count toward the CE ethics requirement, and surplus ethics hours do **not** carry to the next period. (CA's prelicensing itself is heavier on ethics: an A&H or Life candidate completes **20 hours** of line prelicensing **plus 12 hours** of ethics/Code = 32 hours.)
  - **Annuity training:** an agent selling annuities to individuals must complete **8 hours** of initial training before soliciting, then **4 hours** every 2 years before renewal (§ 1749.8). Does not increase the 24-hour total.
  - **LTC training:** **8 hours** before soliciting LTC, 8 hours each of the first 4 years, then 8 hours per 2-year term from year 5 (§ 10234.93 area).
- **Unfair practices:** the Unfair Practices Act, Cal. Ins. Code §§ 790–790.15 (the NAIC UTPA, CA-numbered). **Only the Commissioner** may enforce it. Rebating (giving or receiving) is prohibited.
- **Privacy:** the Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act, §§ 791–791.26, plus federal GLBA and the California Financial Information Privacy Act (Fin. Code §§ 4050–4060).
- **Producer terms:** life-only agent defined at § 1626(a)(1); accident & health agent at § 1626(a)(2). Appointment/notice of appointment §§ 1704–1705; change of address § 1729; fiduciary duties §§ 1733–1735 (premiums held in a fiduciary capacity). Acting without a license / prohibited acts §§ 1631, 1633.
- **Federal:** 18 U.S.C. §§ 1033–1034 (felony-dishonesty bar + the 1033 written-consent waiver) is tested in CA as elsewhere.

## Section: Underwriting limits (California) — both lines
- **HIV/AIDS:** insurers may **not make unfair distinctions** between individuals in underwriting for HIV/AIDS risk (Cal. Ins. Code § 799 et seq.).
- **Genetic characteristics:** insurers may **not unfairly discriminate** based on tests of a person's genetic characteristics (§ 10146); "genetic characteristics" defined at § 10147(b).

## Section 5: Life Policy Provisions (California)
- **Free look / right to return — general:** **not less than 10 days nor more than 30 days** to return an individual life policy or annuity for a full refund; the period is set by the insurer within that range and stated on the policy (Cal. Ins. Code § 10127.9; applies to policies issued in CA on/after 1/1/1990).
- **Free look — seniors (age 60+):** every individual life policy and individual annuity issued to a **senior citizen (60 or older)** must give **at least 30 days** to return it (Cal. Ins. Code § 10127.10). *Trap: the senior cutoff for the 30-day free look is age 60, not 65.*
  - For an annuity bought by a senior where funds are placed in a variable account, special disclosure applies; CA also requires return-of-premium handling under § 10127.10.
- **Nonforfeiture options (Standard Nonforfeiture Law, Cal. Ins. Code § 10159.1 et seq.):** the three options are **cash surrender, reduced paid-up, and extended term** (extended term is the typical automatic/default option). Verified mechanics: a **cash** value becomes available on or after the **3rd policy anniversary** (ordinary) or **5th** (industrial); on surrender, the insurer pays the cash value if surrendered **within 30 days after a policy anniversary**, and may **defer payment up to 6 months** after demand.
- **Insurable interest** must exist at the **inception** of the policy (at application), not necessarily at death (Cal. Ins. Code § 10110 area).
- **Automatic beneficiary revocation on divorce (CA-distinctive):** a **divorced spouse is automatically revoked** as the beneficiary of a life policy issued in California; the owner must re-name them if still intended. *Strong CA trap; most states do not auto-revoke.*
- **Group life standard provisions (CA cites):** incontestability § 10350.2; misstatement of age § 10369.3; conversion privilege § 10209; war/military/aviation exclusion § 10206.5.

## Section: Replacement of Life Insurance & Annuities (California) — Cal. Ins. Code §§ 10509–10509.09
- When a replacement is involved, the agent must present a **"Notice Regarding Replacement of Life Insurance,"** **signed by both the applicant and the agent**, not later than at the time of application, leaving a copy with the applicant.
- A replacing insurer must give a **30-day unconditional-refund** right (on the front of the policy or as a separate delivered notice).
- The **existing insurer** that undertakes a conservation has **20 days** from receiving the required materials to furnish the policyowner a policy summary / ledger statement on the existing policy.

## Guaranty Association (California) — CLHIGA, Cal. Ins. Code §§ 1067–1067.18
The **California Life and Health Insurance Guarantee Association** is unusual: it pays only **80% of the insurer's contractual obligation**, subject to per-life caps:
- **Life death benefit: 80%, up to $300,000.**
- **Life net cash surrender / withdrawal value: 80%, up to $100,000.**
- **Annuity present value (incl. cash value): 80%, up to $250,000.**
- **Aggregate cap for any one life across all life + annuity coverage: $300,000.**
- **Health insurance: up to $200,000** of contractual obligation, **indexed to healthcare-cost inflation** (e.g., ~$661,862 as of March 31, 2024); long-term care is treated as health coverage. The aggregate life/annuity cap does not apply to health.
- **Gag rule:** an agent may **not** use CLHIGA's existence to sell insurance (§ 1067.02 area). *Traps: the 80% haircut is pure CA; and the per-life life+annuity aggregate is $300,000.*

## Section 10: Individual Health (A&S) Policy Provisions (California) — Cal. Ins. Code § 10350 et seq.
California's compulsory standard provisions for individual disability (accident & health) policies — the NAIC uniform provisions, CA-numbered:
- **Grace period (§ 10350.3):** at least **7 days** (weekly-premium), **10 days** (monthly-premium), **31 days** (all other).
- **Time limit on certain defenses / incontestability (§ 10350.2):** after **2 years** from issue, no misstatement (except fraud) voids the policy; pre-existing conditions not excluded by name cannot be used after 2 years.
- **Reinstatement (§ 10350.4):** later acceptance of premium reinstates; with an application + conditional receipt, reinstated on approval or on the **45th day** after the conditional receipt unless disapproved. Covers accidental injury **immediately** and sickness beginning **more than 10 days** after reinstatement; premium applied no more than **60 days** before reinstatement.
- **Notice of claim (§ 10350.5):** within **20 days** of loss, or as soon as reasonably possible.
- **Claim forms (§ 10350.6):** insurer furnishes forms within **15 days**, else proof-of-loss met by written proof.
- **Proof of loss (§ 10350.7):** within **90 days** (or as soon as reasonably possible, outer limit ~**1 year**).

## Section 13: Group Health Continuation (California) — Cal-COBRA, Cal. Ins. Code §§ 10128.50–10128.59
- **Cal-COBRA** extends continuation to small groups of **2–19 eligible employees** (below the federal COBRA 20-employee threshold).
- Continuation runs up to **36 months** (since 1/1/2003; previously 18). Premium up to **110%** of the group rate.
- For groups already under federal COBRA (20+), Cal-COBRA can extend the total to 36 months in some cases.

## Exam content weights (CDI prelicensing objectives, verified)
- **Life-Only Agent (75 questions):** General Insurance **30%** (Basic Concepts 9, Contract Law 6, Marketplace 7 = 22q), Life Insurance **65%** (49q), Life Policy Riders **5%** (4q).
- **Accident & Health Agent (75 questions):** General Insurance **~25%** (18q), General Concepts of Medical & Disability Insurance **~5%** (4q), **Medical Expense Insurance ~60% (45q)** — which **includes 15 Senior Health Products questions** — and Disability Income **~5%** (4q), plus Long-Term Care. *Takeaway: the A&H exam is dominated by medical-expense and senior products; disability income is lightly weighted (the reverse of how heavily DI/large-group sit on some other states' A&H exams).*

## California mandated health benefits (high-frequency; verified topics)
California heavily mandates coverage. For most state-regulated plans the mandates live in the **Health & Safety Code (Knox-Keene/DMHC)** with **Insurance Code** parallels for indemnity health insurers. The exam-relevant ones:
- **Essential Health Benefits** for individual/small-group: ambulatory, emergency, hospitalization, **maternity and newborn care**, **mental health and substance-use (incl. behavioral health treatment)**, prescription drugs, rehabilitative/habilitative, labs, preventive/wellness, and **pediatric services including oral and vision** (Cal. Ins. Code § 10112.27 references the EHB package).
- **Mental health parity:** California mandates coverage and parity for mental health and substance-use disorders (broader than the federal parity-only rule).
- **Autism / Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA):** behavioral health treatment for autism spectrum disorder must be covered.
- **Maternity hospital stay** (H&S § 1367.62) and **mammography screening** (H&S § 1367.65) are specific long-standing mandates.
- *Note:* the full California mandate list (CHBRP catalogs dozens) is large and is a maintenance item; teach the categories above, not an exhaustive list.

## CA vs. MA vs. CT — the differences a test-taker must keep straight
| Item | Massachusetts | Connecticut | California |
|------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| Exam structure | Two exams: Life 16-51, A&H 16-52 (Prometric) | One combined Life+Health 18-03 (Pearson VUE) | **Two exams: Life-Only 75q + A&H 75q (PSI)** (combined 150q also offered) |
| Commissioner | Appointed | Appointed | **Elected** (since 1991, Prop 103) |
| Life free look | 10 days (<$25k); 20 on replacement | 10 days (38a-436) | **10 days general (10127.9); 30 days seniors 60+ (10127.10)** |
| Health grace | (MA-specific) | 7 / 10 / 31 (38a-483) | **7 / 10 / 31 (§ 10350.3)** |
| Guaranty limits | $300k death / $100k cash / $250k annuity | $500k flat | **80% haircut: $300k death / $100k cash / $250k annuity; $300k aggregate/life** |
| CE | 45 hrs / 36 mo, 3 ethics | 24 hrs / 2 yrs, 3 ethics | **24 hrs / 2 yrs, 3 ethics** (annuity 8+4; LTC 8) |
| Small-group continuation | mini-COBRA 176J:9 | until eligible for other group (38a-512a) | **Cal-COBRA 2–19 emp, 36 mo, 110% (§ 10128.50)** |
