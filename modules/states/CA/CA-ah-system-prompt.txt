EXAM STRUCTURE (California Accident & Health or Sickness Agent, PSI, 75 scored questions): general insurance and California regulation, accident and health basics, individual health policy provisions, disability income, medical expense plans, group health and continuation, dental, seniors and special needs, and federal tax of health coverage. Content weights: General Insurance about 25%, Medical Expense Insurance about 60% (including 15 Senior Health Products questions), Disability Income about 5%, and General Concepts of Medical and Disability Insurance about 5%; medical-expense and senior products dominate this exam while disability income is lightly weighted. California statutes are in the California Insurance Code (CIC).

SECTION 1: CALIFORNIA REGULATION
California ELECTS its Insurance Commissioner (a statewide elected office since 1991 under Proposition 103); the Commissioner heads the California Department of Insurance and enforces the Code [[CIC 12900]]. Distinctive: most states appoint. Unfair Practices Act, CIC 790 through 790.15; only the Commissioner enforces it. Rebating (giving OR receiving) is prohibited. Privacy: Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act (CIC 791-791.26) plus federal GLBA. Continuing education: 24 hours per 2-year license term including 3 hours of ethics; the ethics hours are PART OF the 24 (not extra), required every renewal even for newly licensed agents, and prelicensing ethics does not count toward them (CIC 1749.3, 1749.33); long-term care sales require 8 hours of LTC training. Federal 18 USC 1033/1034: a felony involving dishonesty bars insurance work without written regulator consent (the 1033 waiver). Underwriting limits: no unfair distinctions for HIV/AIDS risk (CIC 799); no unfair discrimination on genetic characteristics (CIC 10146). Producer premiums are held in a fiduciary capacity (CIC 1733-1735).

SECTION 2: GENERAL INSURANCE
Risk = uncertainty of loss; pure risk insurable, speculative not. Peril = cause of loss; hazard = condition increasing loss (physical, moral, morale). Risk-handling: avoidance, retention, sharing, reduction, transfer. Law of large numbers; adverse selection; reinsurance. Stock (nonparticipating) vs mutual (participating) vs fraternal. Domestic/foreign/alien = this state/another state/another country. Admitted = certificate of authority. Agent authority: express, implied, apparent. Contract elements: offer/acceptance, consideration, competent parties, legal purpose. Characteristics: adhesion (ambiguities against the insurer), aleatory, unilateral, conditional. Health insurance is a reimbursement (indemnity) contract. Representations (void only if material) vs warranties. Waiver vs estoppel; utmost good faith.

SECTION 3: ACCIDENT & HEALTH BASICS
Two perils: accidental injury (accidental means = the cause is accidental, stricter; accidental results/bodily injury = broader, more favorable) and sickness (first manifests after the effective date). Four benefit types: disability income, medical expense, dental/vision, long-term care. Policy classes: individual vs group; private vs government; limited vs comprehensive. Limited policies: AD&D (principal sum for death/two losses, capital sum for one), specified/dread disease, hospital indemnity (fixed dollars per day regardless of expense), credit disability, blanket. HSAs require a qualified high-deductible health plan, no other disqualifying coverage, and not being enrolled in Medicare; triple tax advantage, funds roll over and are portable. Archer MSAs are the older small-employer (50 or fewer) vehicle. Underwriting sources: application (representations, primary), producer report, attending physician statement, MIB (cannot decline on MIB alone), investigative consumer report (FCRA). Risk classes: preferred, standard, substandard.

SECTION 4: INDIVIDUAL HEALTH POLICY PROVISIONS (CALIFORNIA) - CIC 10350 et seq.
These are California's compulsory standard (uniform) provisions for individual disability policies:
Grace period: at least 7 days (weekly-premium), 10 days (monthly-premium), 31 days (all other) [[CIC 10350.3]]. Time limit on certain defenses (incontestability): after 2 years from issue, no non-fraudulent misstatement voids the policy, and pre-existing conditions not excluded by name cannot be used (CIC 10350.2). Reinstatement: a later premium acceptance reinstates; with an application and conditional receipt, reinstated on approval or on the 45th day after the conditional receipt unless disapproved; covers accidental injury immediately and sickness beginning more than 10 days after, premium applied no more than 60 days before reinstatement (CIC 10350.4). Notice of claim within 20 days (CIC 10350.5). Claim forms furnished within 15 days, else written proof suffices (CIC 10350.6). Proof of loss within 90 days, outer limit about 1 year (CIC 10350.7). Other uniform provisions: entire contract, time of payment of claims, payment of claims, physical exam, legal actions, change of beneficiary.

SECTION 5: DISABILITY INCOME
Own-occupation (more favorable: pays if you cannot do YOUR occupation) vs any-occupation (pays only if you cannot do any suitable occupation) vs pure income replacement. Presumptive disability (loss of two limbs, sight, hearing, speech) is presumed total. Elimination period (waiting period, no benefits, a time deductible) vs benefit period. Benefits paid in arrears; insurers cap benefits around 60-70% of income to preserve the return-to-work incentive. Riders: additional monthly benefit, Social Insurance Supplement, COLA, future increase option, residual (proportional to lost income), partial. Business DI: key person, business overhead expense (BOE, pays business expenses not owner income), disability buy-sell. Social Security disability: any substantial gainful activity, expected to last 12 months or result in death, 5-month elimination period (benefits begin month 6).

SECTION 6: MEDICAL EXPENSE PLANS
HMO (closed network, prepaid, gatekeeper PCP controls referrals, emergency care needs no prior authorization) vs PPO (open panel, in/out-of-network tiers, gatekeeper optional) vs indemnity (any provider, usual/reasonable/customary reimbursement). Service plans (Blue Cross Blue Shield) contract with providers; subscribers receive care. HIPAA: creditable coverage, guaranteed issue/renewability for groups. Utilization review: prospective, concurrent, retrospective. ACA: guaranteed issue and renewability, $0 in-network preventive care, no lifetime/annual dollar limits on essential health benefits, out-of-pocket maximums, emergency care without prior authorization. Mental health parity (federal MHPAEA): parity if mental health is covered. In California many plans are regulated by the Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) under the Knox-Keene Act, while indemnity health insurers are regulated by the CDI. California mandated benefits: California mandates essential health benefits (including maternity and newborn care, mental health and substance-use treatment, prescription drugs, and pediatric oral and vision), mental health parity, behavioral health treatment for autism (ABA), a maternity hospital stay (H&S 1367.62), and mammography screening (H&S 1367.65).

SECTION 7: GROUP HEALTH AND CONTINUATION (CALIFORNIA)
Group coverage uses a master contract; the group is underwritten as a whole, generally with no individual evidence of insurability; employees get certificates. Coordination of benefits prevents over-payment (the birthday rule decides primary for a dependent child: the parent whose birthday falls earlier in the calendar year). Federal COBRA applies to employers with 20 or more employees (18/29/36 months, up to 102% of premium). CALIFORNIA Cal-COBRA extends continuation to small employers of 2 to 19 eligible employees for up to 36 months at up to 110% of the group rate [[CIC 10128.50]]. ERISA governs private employer plans; self-funded ERISA plans are exempt from state mandates.

SECTION 8: DENTAL
Typical coinsurance tiers: 100% preventive / 80% basic / 50% major. Predetermination (pre-treatment estimate) for large work. Watch adverse selection. Scheduled vs comprehensive (integrated deductible) plans.

SECTION 9: SENIORS AND SPECIAL NEEDS
Medicare: Part A (hospital, mostly premium-free with 40 quarters, benefit periods end after 60 days out, SNF needs a 3-day prior stay), Part B (medical, monthly premium, 80/20 after the deductible, no out-of-pocket cap), Part C (Medicare Advantage), Part D (drugs). Medicare Supplement (Medigap): California uses the standard NAIC lettered plans (A through N) with the 6-month open enrollment tied to turning 65 and enrolling in Part B. MACRA bars new (2020+) eligibles from any plan covering the Part B deductible. Long-term care covers custodial care, triggered by inability to perform 2 of 6 activities of daily living for 90+ days or severe cognitive impairment; LTC is treated as health coverage under the guaranty association. Medi-Cal is California's Medicaid (needs-based, payer of last resort for custodial care).

SECTION 10: FEDERAL TAX (HEALTH)
Personally-owned disability income: premiums NOT deductible, benefits TAX-FREE. Employer-paid group disability: premiums deductible to the employer, benefits TAXABLE to the employee. Employer-paid group medical: deductible to the employer, not income to employees, benefits tax-free (the most tax-favored arrangement). Self-employed health insurance: above-the-line deduction. Key person and disability buy-sell: premiums not deductible, benefits not taxable. Business overhead expense (BOE): premiums ARE deductible, benefits ARE taxable (the inversion).

GUARANTY ASSOCIATION (CALIFORNIA): The California Life and Health Insurance Guarantee Association (CLHIGA) covers health insurance up to $200,000 of the insurer's contractual obligation, indexed to healthcare-cost inflation, and pays only 80% of the obligation; long-term care is treated as health coverage (CIC 1067.02). An agent may NOT use CLHIGA's existence to sell insurance (the gag rule). The 80% haircut is distinctive to California.
