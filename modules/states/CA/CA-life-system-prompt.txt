EXAM STRUCTURE (California Life-Only Agent, PSI, 75 scored questions): I. General Insurance (30%): basic insurance concepts and principles, contract law, the insurance marketplace. II. Life Insurance (65%): life basics, types of life policies, annuities, policy replacement and cancellation, the individual life insurance contract, taxation of life insurance and annuities, group life, the Social Security disability program, individual underwriting/pricing/claims. III. Life Policy Riders (5%). California statutes are in the California Insurance Code (CIC).

SECTION 1: GENERAL INSURANCE
Risk = uncertainty of loss; pure risk (loss or no loss) is insurable, speculative (chance of gain, gambling) is not. Peril = cause of loss; hazard = condition that increases loss (physical, moral, morale). Risk-handling: avoidance, retention, sharing, reduction, transfer (insurance is transfer). Law of large numbers; adverse selection (underwriting fights it); reinsurance (ceding insurer transfers risk). Ideally insurable risk: due to chance, definite/measurable, predictable, not catastrophic, large homogeneous group, affordable. Contract elements: offer/acceptance, consideration, competent parties, legal purpose. Insurance contract characteristics: adhesion (ambiguities construed against the insurer), aleatory (unequal exchange dependent on chance), unilateral (only the insurer promises), conditional, personal. Representations (believed true, void only if material) vs warranties (guaranteed true). Concealment, misrepresentation, materiality (judged by reasonable influence on the insurer, CIC 334). Utmost good faith. Insurers: stock (nonparticipating) vs mutual (participating, policy dividends are a non-taxable return of premium) vs fraternal. Domestic/foreign/alien = this state/another state/another country. Admitted = holds a certificate of authority. Agent authority: express, implied, apparent. Producer holds premiums in a fiduciary capacity (CIC 1733-1735).

SECTION 2: CALIFORNIA REGULATION
California ELECTS its Insurance Commissioner (a statewide elected office since 1991 under Proposition 103); the Commissioner heads the California Department of Insurance and enforces the Code [[CIC 12900]]. This is distinctive: most states appoint. Unfair Practices Act, CIC 790 through 790.15; only the Commissioner enforces it. Rebating (giving OR receiving anything of value not in the policy) is prohibited. Privacy: Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act (CIC 791-791.26), plus federal GLBA and the California Financial Information Privacy Act. Continuing education: 24 hours per 2-year license term including 3 hours of ethics; the ethics hours are PART OF the 24 (not extra), required every renewal even for newly licensed agents, and prelicensing ethics does not count toward them (CIC 1749.3, 1749.33). An agent selling annuities to individuals must complete 8 hours of annuity training before soliciting plus 4 hours every 2 years (CIC 1749.8). Federal 18 USC 1033/1034: a felony involving dishonesty bars working in insurance without written regulator consent (the 1033 waiver). Underwriting limits: insurers may not make unfair distinctions for HIV/AIDS risk (CIC 799) and may not unfairly discriminate on genetic characteristics (CIC 10146).

SECTION 3: LIFE INSURANCE BASICS
Insurable interest must exist at the time of APPLICATION (inception), not at death [[CIC 10110]]. Human life value approach = replace future earnings; needs approach = total needs minus existing resources (best for how much coverage to apply for). Business uses: key person (the business owns, pays, and is beneficiary; premiums NOT deductible, death proceeds NOT taxable), buy-sell agreements (cross-purchase vs entity/stock-redemption), split dollar, deferred compensation. Premium factors: mortality, interest, expense; more frequent payment mode = higher total annual cost. Life insurance "creates an immediate estate." Funeral/burial policies in CA have an initial face amount of $20,000 or less.

SECTION 4: TYPES OF LIFE POLICIES
Term = cheapest, no cash value, pays only during the term; level/decreasing (mortgage)/increasing/annually renewable; renewable and convertible reprice at attained age. Whole life = permanent, level premium, guaranteed cash value, matures at 100/121; variants differ in how paid (straight/continuous, limited-pay, single-premium, modified, graded). Universal life = flexible premium and adjustable death benefit, interest-sensitive, minimum guaranteed rate, options A (level) and B (increasing). Variable life and variable universal life are securities (separate account, owner bears investment risk) and require FINRA registration plus a life license. Indexed universal life credits interest tied to an index with a floor. Special: family policy, juvenile (payor rider), joint life (first-to-die) vs survivorship (second-to-die), credit life (decreasing term, capped at the loan). MEC: fails the 7-pay test, taxed LIFO with a 10% penalty before 59 1/2; death benefit stays tax-free.

SECTION 5: ANNUITIES
An annuity protects against outliving your money. Parties: owner, annuitant (the measuring life), beneficiary. Immediate (single premium, first payment within 12 months) vs deferred (single or flexible premium, accumulation then payout). Fixed (guaranteed rate, insurer bears risk, general account) vs variable (separate account, owner bears risk, requires FINRA plus a license). Equity-indexed/fixed-indexed = index-linked excess interest with a floor. Payout options: straight life (most income, nothing to a beneficiary), life with period certain, life with refund (cash/installment), joint and survivor. Exclusion ratio = investment in the contract divided by expected return (the tax-free portion). Annuity suitability information must be gathered before a recommendation (CIC 10509.913). California senior rules (60+) apply to annuity sales.

SECTION 6: POLICY REPLACEMENT AND CANCELLATION (CALIFORNIA)
Free look (right to return) on individual life and annuities: not less than 10 days nor more than 30 days, set by the insurer and stated on the policy [[CIC 10127.9]]. For a senior citizen age 60 or older, the free look is at least 30 days (CIC 10127.10). The senior cutoff is 60, not 65. Replacement (CIC 10509 through 10509.09): when replacing existing coverage, the agent must present a "Notice Regarding Replacement of Life Insurance," signed by both the applicant and the agent, no later than at application, leaving a copy with the applicant; the replacing insurer gives a 30-day unconditional refund right; the existing insurer has 20 days to furnish a policy summary on the existing policy. Twisting (misrepresentation to induce a replacement) and churning are prohibited.

SECTION 7: THE INDIVIDUAL LIFE CONTRACT (CALIFORNIA PROVISIONS)
Common provisions: insuring clause, consideration clause, free look, owner's rights (assignment, change beneficiary, change mode, cash values, dividends). Conditional receipt vs binding receipt (binders are prohibited in life and disability). Beneficiaries: primary vs contingent, revocable vs irrevocable, per capita vs per stirpes, common disaster (Uniform Simultaneous Death Act), spendthrift clause. CALIFORNIA TRAP: a divorced spouse is AUTOMATICALLY revoked as the beneficiary of a California life policy and must be re-named if still intended. Standard provisions: grace period, automatic premium loan, reinstatement, policy loan, incontestability (2 years), suicide (refund of premium during the contestable period), misstatement of age or sex (adjusts the benefit to what the premium would have bought, never voids). Nonforfeiture options: cash surrender, reduced paid-up, extended term (extended term is the usual automatic option); a cash value is available on or after the 3rd policy anniversary (5th for industrial), paid on surrender within 30 days after a policy anniversary, and the insurer may defer payment up to 6 months (CIC 10159.1). Dividend options (participating only, dividends are a non-taxable return of premium): cash, reduced premium, accumulate at interest, paid-up additions, one-year term. Settlement options: lump sum, interest only, fixed period, fixed amount, life income.

SECTION 8: TAXATION OF LIFE INSURANCE AND ANNUITIES
Death benefit is income-tax-free to the beneficiary. Cash value grows tax-deferred. Dividends are a non-taxable return of premium (interest earned on them is taxable). Policy loans are not taxable while the policy is in force. Surrender: gain above basis is taxable. Annuities: the exclusion ratio splits tax-free principal from taxable earnings; pre-59 1/2 distributions face a 10% penalty and are taxed LIFO; unpaid annuity benefits after the annuitant's death are taxable to the beneficiary. MEC: fails the 7-pay test, taxed LIFO, 10% penalty before 59 1/2. 1035 exchange: life-to-life, life-to-annuity, and annuity-to-annuity are tax-free; annuity-to-life is NOT allowed. Group term life: employer-paid coverage over $50,000 creates imputed income to the employee.

SECTION 9: GROUP LIFE, SOCIAL SECURITY, UNDERWRITING
Group life uses a master contract to the employer/association (not formed just to buy insurance) with certificates to insureds; mostly group term; conversion to individual on leaving (CIC 10209); group incontestability (CIC 10350.2); misstatement of age (CIC 10369.3). Social Security disability: fully insured = 40 credits; strict "any substantial gainful activity" definition; 5-month elimination period; blackout period for a surviving spouse. Underwriting sources: application (primary), MIB (cannot decline on MIB alone), attending physician statement, inspection/consumer report (FCRA notice and right to a copy), DMV report. Risk classes: preferred, standard, substandard (rated), declined. The agent is the field underwriter. Rate-making components: mortality, expenses, investment earnings.

SECTION 10: LIFE POLICY RIDERS
Waiver of premium (premiums waived on total disability). Accidental death (double indemnity). Guaranteed insurability (buy more coverage later without proving insurability). Cost-of-living. Payor (on juvenile policies). Return of premium. Accelerated death benefit / living needs (terminal or chronic illness advances part of the death benefit; reduces the death benefit). Long-term care rider (LTC training required to sell, 8 hours). Guaranteed minimum income/withdrawal riders on annuities.

GUARANTY ASSOCIATION (CALIFORNIA): The California Life and Health Insurance Guarantee Association (CLHIGA) is distinctive because it pays only 80% of the insurer's obligation, subject to caps: life death benefit 80% up to $300,000; net cash surrender value 80% up to $100,000; annuity present value 80% up to $250,000; the aggregate for any one life across all life and annuity coverage is $300,000 [[CIC 1067.02]]. An agent may NOT use CLHIGA's existence to sell insurance (the gag rule). Two traps: the 80% haircut is pure California, and the per-life life-plus-annuity aggregate is $300,000.
