## Section 1: Insurance Regulation (CT)
- **Continuing education: 24 hours per 2-year license term**, including a minimum of **3 hours** of Connecticut insurance law/regulation or ethics, and a minimum of **6 hours per line of authority**. (R.C.S.A. § 38a-782a; CID.)
- Producer licensing, appointments, and unfair-practice rules sit in Title 38a, Chapter 701a (38a-702 series) and the unfair insurance practices act (38a-815 et seq) — the same NAIC framework as other states, CT-numbered.

## Section 5: Life Policy Provisions (CT)
- **Free look / right to return: 10 days.** Every individual life policy must carry a notice that the applicant may return it within **ten days** of receipt, and on return the policy is **void ab initio**. [[C.G.S. 38a-436]]
- **Standard Nonforfeiture Law (C.G.S. 38a-438/439):** the standard options are cash surrender, extended term, and reduced paid-up. Verified mechanics: the owner elects (or surrenders for cash) by request made **no later than 60 days after** the due date of the defaulted premium; a **cash** surrender value is available once premiums have been paid for at least **3 full years** (ordinary) or **5 full years** (industrial); the insurer may **defer paying** the cash value up to **6 months** after demand.
- **Policy loans / automatic premium loan (C.G.S. 38a-444):** policies provide cash/policy loans; an automatic premium loan pays a delinquent premium from cash value at the end of the grace period.
- **Replacement of life insurance and annuities:** governed by regulation **R.C.S.A. 38a-480-1 to 480-5** (agent/insurer duties, notice to the applicant, comparison disclosures).
- *Note:* CT does not fix an individual-life **grace-period** day-count in statute the way it does for health (38a-483); the grace period appears as a policy standard provision.

## Section 10: Individual Health Policy Provisions (CT) — C.G.S. 38a-483
These are CT's required ("standard") provisions for individual health policies:
- **Grace period:** at least **7 days** (weekly-premium policies), **10 days** (monthly-premium), or **31 days** (all other policies).
- **Time limit on certain defenses (incontestability):** the policy is incontestable, except for nonpayment of premium, after **2 years** from its date of issue.
- **Reinstatement:** a later acceptance of premium reinstates the policy; if the insurer requires an application and issues a conditional receipt, the policy reinstates on approval or on the **45th day** after the conditional receipt unless disapproved. A reinstated policy covers accidental injury immediately and **sickness beginning more than 10 days** after reinstatement; premium may be applied no more than **60 days** before the reinstatement date. [[C.G.S. 38a-483]]

## Guaranty Association (CT) — C.G.S. 38a-858 et seq.
The **Connecticut Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association** covers, per insured life:
- **Life death benefit: $500,000**
- **Life cash surrender/withdrawal value: $500,000**
- **Health insurance claims: $500,000**
- **Annuity present value: $500,000** (per contract owner)
- **Aggregate cap: $500,000** for any one insured life across all policies from an insolvent insurer.
- Gag rule applies: the association's existence may not be used to sell insurance. (Source: ctlifega.org; C.G.S. 38a-858 to 38a-875.)

## Section 13: Group Health Continuation (CT)
- **Continuation of coverage (C.G.S. 38a-512a / 38a-538):** a group health plan must offer the option to continue coverage on qualifying events **until the individual becomes eligible for other group insurance** (with limited exceptions). This is broader than federal COBRA's fixed 18/36-month windows.
- **Managed-care grace period (C.G.S. 38a-478g):** a managed care contract's grace period for payment must be **not less than 10 days**.
- **Handicapped dependent children (C.G.S. 38a-515):** coverage continues past the limiting age for a child incapable of self-support and chiefly dependent on the member.

## CT vs. MA — the differences a test-taker must keep straight
| Item | Massachusetts | Connecticut |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Exam structure | Two exams: Life 16-51, A&H 16-52 | **One combined** Life+Health, 18-03 |
| Testing vendor | Prometric | **Pearson VUE** |
| Life free look | 10 days (<$25k); 20 on replacement | **10 days**, all individual life (38a-436) |
| Health grace | (MA-specific) | **7 / 10 / 31 days** by premium mode (38a-483) |
| Guaranty limits | $300k death / $100k cash / $250k annuity | **$500k flat** across the board |
| CE | 45 hrs / 36 months, 3 ethics | **24 hrs / 2 yrs**, 3 ethics, 6 per line |
