## Part 1: Producer responsibilities (MA cites) — Outline 3.6
- **Marketing (211 CMR 40.00) and advertising (175:110E):** advertisements must be truthful and not misleading; the Commissioner enforces form, content, and disclosure standards.
- **Guaranty Association gag rule (175:146B(19)):** the Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association protects policyholders if an insurer becomes insolvent, but producers are **prohibited from using its existence as a sales tool** ("don't worry, the state guarantees it") — even truthfully. Classic exam question.
- **Outline of coverage (211 CMR 42.09):** a plain-language summary of benefits, exclusions, and renewability, delivered at application (or with the policy).
- **Disclosure of information about individuals (175:108E; 211 CMR 42.09),** within the c. 175I privacy framework (see the §1 overlay).
- **Limited-policy notice:** MA requires a prominent notice to the insured that limited coverage is limited (notice wording unpulled — low-priority maintenance item).

## Part 2: MA underwriting limits — Outline 3.7
- **HIV testing (211 CMR 36.05):** requires the applicant's **written informed consent** on an approved form, with confidentiality protections; underwriting may **not** use **sexual orientation** in risk classification.
- **Genetic information (175:108H, 108I):** MA bars insurers from requiring **genetic tests** or using genetic information in accident & sickness underwriting without consent — a strong MA-specific protection.

## Part 3: Replacement — 211 CMR 42.08, 42.11 (verified)
- Every **application form must contain a question** asking whether the new policy will replace existing accident and sickness coverage (42.08(1)).
- When replacement is involved, the agent or carrier must furnish the prescribed **"Notice to Applicant Regarding Replacement"** — **at the time of application or before the policy is issued** — leaving a copy with the applicant and keeping a **signed copy** in the carrier's records (42.08(2)).
- The prescribed notice (42.11) warns that **existing health conditions may not be covered** under the new policy and urges comparison before terminating the old coverage. A bad replacement is a textbook E&O scenario.

## Part 4: The Massachusetts individual mandate — c. 111M; 956 CMR 5.00
- Since **2006** (pre-dating the ACA, and surviving the federal penalty's repeal), MA adults **18+** must maintain **Minimum Creditable Coverage (MCC)** or face a **state tax penalty**, reported on **Schedule HC** with the MA income tax return. Penalties scale with income; exemptions exist for affordability hardship and religious belief.
- **Exam-critical nuance:** owning only a limited accident/sickness policy (hospital indemnity, dread disease) does **NOT** satisfy the mandate — MCC requires comprehensive coverage meeting state standards.

## Part 5: MA child health safety net
- **Children's Medical Security Plan (CMSP):** primary/preventive coverage for uninsured MA children — a state safety-net program, not private insurance.

## Exam traps (MA-specific)
- *A limited policy (hospital indemnity, dread disease) does NOT satisfy the MA mandate* — MCC requires comprehensive coverage.
- *Producers may never use the Guaranty Association as a sales inducement, even if the statement is true* (175:146B(19)).
- *MA bars genetic-test requirements and sexual-orientation classification;* HIV testing needs written informed consent.
