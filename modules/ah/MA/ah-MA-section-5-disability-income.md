# MA A&H Overlay — Series 16-52
## Section 5: Disability Income (Massachusetts statute layer)
**Scope: Massachusetts-specific | Pairs with the national core (`ah/national/ah-section-5-disability-income.md`) | Source law: 211 CMR 42.05(1)(g); M.G.L. c. 152 (workers' compensation)**

> **How this layer works:** Disability income is ~95% universal — the definitions, elimination/benefit periods, residual math, business DI products, and the Social Security disability test are all in the national core (and SSDI is federal). Massachusetts adds only a thin layer: minimum definition-of-disability standards by regulation and the in-state workers' comp cite. This is the lightest A&H overlay.

---

## Part 1: MA minimum benefit / definition standards — 211 CMR 42.05(1)(g)
- Massachusetts sets **minimum definition-of-disability standards** for individual A&S policies by regulation. The "any occupation" definition and minimum benefit standards a MA-delivered policy must meet are governed here. (Field-level wording is a low-priority pull; the core teaches the universal own-occ / any-occ / income-replacement hierarchy.)

## Part 2: Workers' compensation — M.G.L. c. 152
- MA workers' compensation covers **occupational** injury and disease on a **no-fault** basis — medical care, disability income (a fraction of average weekly wage), rehabilitation, and death benefits — as the employee's exclusive remedy against the employer.
- *Scope note:* workers' comp benefit percentages and maximums are tested in depth on the **casualty/P&C** exam; the A&H exam keeps this summary-level.
