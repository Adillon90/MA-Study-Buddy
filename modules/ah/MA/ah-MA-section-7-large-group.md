## Part 1: The MA continuation cluster — three statutes

These MA statutes apply to group policies issued **within or without the commonwealth** that cover Massachusetts residents.

| Statute | Trigger | What continues, and for how long |
|---|---|---|
| **175:110D** | Insured **leaves the group** (any reason) | Coverage continues **automatically for 31 days** after leaving, unless entitled to similar benefits elsewhere. No election required. |
| **175:110G** | **Involuntary layoff** or **death** of the member | Coverage for the member (or surviving spouse) and dependents continues **39 weeks** from ineligibility — or until eligible under another group plan, whichever is first; never longer than the most recent period of coverage. The employer must notify them of the right. |
| **175:110I** | **Divorce or separate support judgment** | The former spouse **remains eligible under the member's plan without additional premium or examination**, as if the judgment had not entered (unless the judgment says otherwise) — until the **remarriage of either** party, or as the judgment provides. If the *member* remarries, the judgment may give the former spouse the right to continue via a rider or individual plan at fair additional premium. |

- *Exam traps:* 110G's clock runs in **weeks (39)**, not months. 110I has **no fixed clock** — it runs to remarriage or the judgment's terms, and costs the former spouse nothing extra while it lasts. 110D's **31 days** happens automatically with no election.

## Part 2: Mini-COBRA — small-group continuation, c. 176J § 9 (verified)
MA extends COBRA-parallel continuation rights to small-group plans below the federal 20-employee threshold. Verified mechanics:
- Coverage must be **identical** to what similarly situated active members receive.
- May **not** be conditioned on **evidence of insurability**.
- Disability extension follows the federal pattern: notice to the carrier within **60 days** of the disability determination and before the 18-month period ends, with the disabled individual chargeable **150%** of premium after month 18.
- When continuation expires, the carrier must offer enrollment in a **conversion nongroup plan** during the **180-day period** ending on the expiration date.
- *Pure-MA trap:* Mini-COBRA does **NOT** apply to dental or vision plans.

## Part 3: Merged market & small-group rating (Outline 7.5)
- **Small employer (176J:1; 211 CMR 66.04, verified):** MA merged its individual and small-group markets into one risk pool. "Eligible small business" = **1 to 50 eligible employees** (carriers may require groups of **5 or fewer** to enroll through an intermediary or the Connector).
- **Guaranteed availability + modified community rating (211 CMR 66.07, verified):** carriers must offer plans to all eligible small groups; premiums are based on the **collective experience of the merged pool**; only the **enumerated rating adjustment factors** apply — notably an **age factor capped at 2:1** (highest adult to lowest, tighter than the federal 3:1) and an **area factor between 0.8 and 1.2** — all other adjustments **prohibited**. Health status and claims experience can **never** be rating factors. Annual rate filings with actuarial certification reviewed by the Division (66.08).
- **Guaranteed renewability (211 CMR 66.06):** small-group plans renew at the employer's option except for statutory causes (nonpayment, fraud, market withdrawal).

## Part 4: MA COB & nondiscrimination cites
- **Coordination of benefits:** the birthday rule and payment-order mechanics (in the national core) are codified for MA at **211 CMR 38.01–.08**.
- **Group nondiscrimination:** MA bars discriminatory group practices under **175:108C** and parallels.

## Exam traps (MA-specific)
- *110G runs in **weeks (39)**, not months;* 110D is **31 days automatic, no election;** 110I has **no fixed clock** (to remarriage) and **no extra premium**.
- *Mini-COBRA excludes dental and vision* and requires **no evidence of insurability**.
- *MA caps the small-group age factor at **2:1**,* tighter than the federal 3:1; health status and claims experience are never rating factors.
