## How MA adopted the model
Massachusetts enacted the NAIC Producer Licensing Model Act as **M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 162G–162X**, so the licensing mechanics follow the national pattern with MA citations. Unfair practices live in **c. 176D**; privacy in **c. 175I**.

## Part 1: Licensing values (Outline 1.1)

### Getting licensed — 175:162I, 162L
- License required to **sell, solicit, or negotiate** in MA (175:162I).
- **No pre-licensing coursework** required (175:162L). Candidate must be at least **18**, pass the Prometric exam for the line, and apply through NIPR.
- Exams required for the major lines (Life, A&H, Property, Casualty, Personal Lines); **Variable** requires the Life line plus FINRA registration (no separate state exam); **Travel** requires no exam.
- Lines of authority enumerated at **175:162M(a)**.

### Keeping the license — 175:162M(b)–(f), 162P
- License **remains in effect indefinitely** while the renewal fee is paid and CE is met by the due date. (Initial license runs **3 years or less**, pegged to the licensee's birthday.)
- **Lapsed license:** reinstatable within **12 months** of the renewal due date **without re-exam**, penalty = **double the unpaid renewal fee** (162M(c)).
- Window to apply for the license after passing the exam: **12 months**.
- **Address change:** report to the Commissioner within **30 days**.
- **Assumed (DBA) names:** report before use (162P).
- **Reporting actions (162V):** report any **administrative action** in another jurisdiction (or by another MA agency) within **30 days of final disposition** (with order/consent docs); report any **criminal prosecution** in any jurisdiction within **30 days of the initial pretrial hearing date** (with complaint and resulting orders).
- **Temporary license (162Q):** up to **180 days, no exam**, to keep an insurance business running — surviving spouse/personal representative of a producer who dies or is disabled, designee of a business entity's key individual, designee of a producer entering active military service, or other public-interest cases. The Commissioner may require a **sponsor** (a licensed producer or insurer); the temporary license ends when the business is sold.

### Continuing education (MA values)
- **60 hours** before the first license renewal, including **3 hours of MA-approved Ethics**.
- **45 hours** each subsequent **36-month** period, also including **3 hours of Ethics**.

### Discipline — 175:162R
MA enumerates the **14 grounds** (listed in the national core) and adopts them under 162R; civil penalties run through c. 176D § 7.
- Denial/nonrenewal: applicant may make **written demand within 30 days** for a hearing (held under Chapter 30A).
- **Fines (verified):**
  - Unfair/deceptive acts under c. 176D: up to **$1,000 per act or practice** plus cease-and-desist, with suspension — or revocation for **repeated** violations (176D:7).
  - Violating a final cease-and-desist order: up to **$10,000 per violation** (176D:10).
  - Insurance-law violations not separately listed (e.g., failing to report under 162V): up to **$500 per violation** (175:194); violations of laws/regulations/orders under 162R(a)(2): up to **$1,000 each**.

## Part 2: State regulation values (Outline 1.2)

### Commissioner & company regulation — 175:3A, 4, 32, 151, 2B, 192; 176D:5
- Commissioner heads the **Division of Insurance** and **enforces** the laws (175:3A; 176D:5).
- **Certificate of authority:** 175:4, 32, 151. **Policy forms** filed/approved: 175:2B, 192. **Exam of records:** 175:4.

### Producer appointments — 175:162S
- Insurer files the appointment notice within **15 days** of the agency contract being executed **or** the first application being submitted, whichever comes first.
- Commissioner verifies eligibility within **30 days**; notifies the insurer within **5 days** if the producer is ineligible. The insurer pays the appointment fee.

### Termination of appointment — 175:162T
- Insurer notifies the Commissioner within **30 days of the termination's effective date** (same clock for-cause or not; for-cause requires the prescribed format and supporting records on request).
- Within **15 days** after notifying the Commissioner, the insurer mails a copy to the producer at the last known address — by **certified mail, return receipt requested**, when the termination was for a 162R cause. Supplement if new reportable info surfaces.

### Producer conduct crimes
- **Impersonation** (175:175); **larceny / fiduciary capacity** for collected premiums (175:176); **unlicensed compensation** prohibited (175:177).

### Unfair & deceptive practices — c. 176D § 3
MA codifies the NAIC Unfair Trade Practices Act at 176D:3 (misrepresentation/false advertising, defamation, boycott/coercion/intimidation, false financial statements, failure to keep a complaint record, unfair discrimination, **rebating** at 175:182–184 and 176D:3(8), and **unfair claims settlement practices** at 176D:3(9)). MA **retains** the rebate ban for both giver and receiver.

### Privacy — c. 175I
The Insurance Information and Privacy Protection Act: **notice of information practices**, access/correction rights, specific reasons for adverse underwriting decisions, investigative-consumer-report disclosure, and the applicant's right to request an interview.

## Exam traps (MA-specific)
- *MA requires **no** pre-licensing education* — a national prep book that assumes classroom hours is wrong for MA.
- *162V uses two different clocks*: administrative actions run from **final disposition**; criminal prosecutions run from the **initial pretrial hearing**.
- *162T's two-step clock*: **30 days** to notify the Commissioner, then **15 days** to mail the producer (certified, return receipt, when for-cause).
- *The fines ladder*: $1,000 / $10,000 / $500 map to unfair practices / cease-and-desist violation / unlisted violations.
