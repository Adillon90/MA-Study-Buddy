# MA A&H Overlay — Series 16-52
## Section 4: Individual A&S Policy Provisions (Massachusetts statute layer)
**Scope: Massachusetts-specific | Pairs with the national core (`ah/national/ah-section-4-policy-provisions.md`) | Source law verified against malegislature.gov: M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 108, 187H — current through the 194th General Court**

> **How this layer works:** The national core carries the NAIC Uniform Individual Accident and Sickness Policy Provision Law — the 12 required provisions and the optional provisions, whose values are national. Massachusetts codifies that law at **M.G.L. c. 175 § 108** and layers a handful of MA-specific rules on top. This overlay carries those MA additions: the free look, prompt-pay, and the § 108 quirks the exam loves.

---

## How MA adopted the law
Massachusetts enacted the NAIC Uniform Law at **M.G.L. c. 175 § 108**; the free look lives separately at **§ 187H**. The 12 required provisions and the optional provisions in the national core apply in MA with the national values.

## Part 1: Free look (Right to Examine) — § 187H
- The owner may cancel within **10 days of delivery** for any reason; surrendering the policy to the insurer's home office, branch office, or selling agent voids it **from the beginning** with a **full premium refund**.
- *Exam focus:* 10 days, full refund, **void from inception** (not from the cancellation date).

## Part 2: MA grace-period nonrenewal note
- If the insurer reserves the right to refuse renewal, it must mail nonrenewal notice at least **30 days** before the premium due date.

## Part 3: Prompt-pay rule — § 108(4)(c)
- For hospital/medical/surgical/dental expense policies, the insurer must furnish claim forms within **15 days** of notice.
- If the claim isn't paid within **45 days**, the insurer must explain why in writing — otherwise it owes **interest at 1.5% per month (max 18% per year)** on the benefits, accruing from day 45.
- Does **not** apply to suspected-fraud investigations.

## Part 4: Other § 108 MA rules to memorize
- **Dependent coverage:** family policies must cover children to **age 26**, and incapacitated children beyond that if proof of incapacity is given within **31 days** of when coverage would end. (Primary home for the dependent-coverage mandate is the §6 overlay, `ah-MA-section-6-health-plans.md`.)
- **Pre-existing condition denials (§ 108(2)(a)(2A)):** if an insurer denies a claim based on a pre-existing condition, it must send the insured **documented evidence of actual treatment or observation** of that condition with the denial.
- **Application rules (§ 108(5)):** the insured isn't bound by application statements unless a copy is attached to the policy; a false statement bars recovery only if it **materially affected** acceptance of the risk or the hazard assumed.
- **Accident-only policies (§ 108(9)):** must carry a **bold first-page notice** that the policy covers accident only and pays nothing for sickness.

## Exam traps (MA-specific)
- *MA free look voids the policy **from inception**, not from the cancellation date* — full premium refund.
- *Prompt-pay interest is **1.5%/month (18%/yr)** from **day 45***, unless suspected fraud is under investigation.
- *A pre-existing-condition denial must come with documented evidence of actual treatment/observation* (§ 108(2)(a)(2A)).
