# MA A&H Overlay — Series 16-52
## Section 6: Health Insurance Plans (Massachusetts statute layer)
**Scope: Massachusetts-specific | Pairs with the national core (`ah/national/ah-section-6-health-plans.md`) | Source law verified against malegislature.gov: M.G.L. c. 175 §§ 47B, 47C, 108, 110; c. 176A/B/G/J/O; DOI Bulletins**

> **How this layer works:** The national core covers how plans work (HMO/PPO/indemnity, HIPAA, ACA, federal parity). This overlay carries the Massachusetts pieces the exam loads with day-counts: who must be covered (newborns, adopted children, dependents), the managed-care appeal mechanics through the Office of Patient Protection, cost transparency, and the MA mental-health coverage mandate.

---

## Part 1: Mandated eligibility (Outline 6.4)

### Dependent children — to age 26
Dependent coverage must extend to children to **age 26** regardless of student, marital, or financial status. **Incapacitated adult children** stay covered past the limiting age if proof of incapacity is furnished within **31 days** of when coverage would otherwise end. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 108(2)(a)(3)]] (parallels: 175:110(P); 176A:8BB; 176B:4BB; 176G:4T)

### Newborn coverage — M.G.L. c. 175 § 47C
- Newborns (of the policyholder and of a covered dependent) are insured **immediately from the moment of birth** — no waiting period, no underwriting.
- Coverage explicitly includes **congenital defects, birth abnormalities, and premature birth**.
- If a **specific premium** is required to add the child, the insurer may require **notification of birth plus the premium** — and the statute says "notification" **may mean simply submitting a claim**.
- Also mandates: **metabolic formulas** (PKU and related), **lead poisoning screening**, **newborn hearing screening before hospital discharge**. [[M.G.L. c. 175 § 47C]]

### Adopted children — M.G.L. c. 175 § 47C
- Foster child for whom the policyholder receives foster payments: covered **from the date the adoption petition is filed**.
- All other cases: covered **from the date of placement** by a licensed agency.
- *Exam trap:* coverage does NOT wait for the adoption to be **finalized**.

### Children's preventive/primary care — § 47C
Well-child visits: **six in the first year, three in the second, then annually to age six**, plus immunizations and screenings. **Early intervention services** for children **birth to age 3** with **no copay, coinsurance, or deductible** (narrow tax-qualified-plan exception).

### Open enrollment — M.G.L. c. 176J § 4
Individual and small-group ("merged market") coverage is sold **guaranteed-issue** during annual open enrollment, with special enrollment for qualifying events.

## Part 2: Managed-care appeals — M.G.L. c. 176O §§ 12–14

A carrier denial/reduction/termination based on medical necessity triggers a written **adverse determination notice** and a two-tier appeal:
1. **Internal grievance** — one level, reviewed by uninvolved clinicians. Written resolution due within **30 days** (clock pauses until the insured signs a records release; extendable only by **mutual written agreement**, max 30 more days). Grievance records kept **7 years**. A grievance not acted on in time is **deemed resolved in favor of the insured** and becomes immediately eligible for external review (§ 13(c)). On a treating physician's certification of **immediate risk of serious harm**, the carrier must **automatically reverse within 48 hours** (958 CMR 3.309).
2. **External review** — through the **Office of Patient Protection (OPP)** within **4 months** of the final internal decision. The insured pays only the first **$25** (waived for hardship, refunded if fully reversed, capped at **$75 per plan year**); the carrier pays the rest. The panel's decision **binds the carrier**.
- **Expedited track:** internal and external expedited reviews may be requested **simultaneously**; the external panel issues its written disposition within **72 hours** (§§ 13(d), 14).

## Part 3: Cost transparency — M.G.L. c. 176O § 23; Bulletin 2013-10
Carriers must give members **real-time cost estimates** via a toll-free number and website: the estimated/maximum allowed amount and the member's out-of-pocket cost for proposed services. (Distinctly Massachusetts; national prep skips it.)

## Part 4: Mental health — M.G.L. c. 175 § 47B
MA goes beyond federal parity by **mandating** coverage: policies must cover **biologically-based mental disorders** (schizophrenia, bipolar, major depression) on a **nondiscriminatory basis**, plus mental disorders of **rape victims** and non-biologically-based disorders of **children/adolescents under 19**. (Parallels 176A:8A; 176B:4A; 176G:4M; Bulletin 2013-02.)
- *Exam framing:* federal MHPAEA = parity IF covered; **Massachusetts = coverage mandated** for the listed categories, at parity.

## Drill card (verified MA values)
- Dependents to **age 26**; incapacitated child proof within **31 days**.
- Newborn covered **from the moment of birth** (incl. congenital/premature); "notification" can be **a claim**.
- Adopted: **petition-filing date** (foster child) or **placement date** (all others) — never waits for finalization.
- Well-child **6 / 3 / annual** to age 6; early intervention birth-to-**3** at **$0** cost-sharing.
- Appeals: internal grievance **30 days**, one level; **deemed resolved** for the insured if untimely; **48-hour** auto-reversal on harm certification; external review via **OPP** within **4 months**, **$25/$75** fees, **72-hour** expedited decision.
- MA mandates mental-health coverage (47B), unlike federal parity-only.

## Exam traps
- *Adopted-child coverage starts at petition filing (foster) or placement, not at finalization.*
- *The untimely-grievance rule deems the dispute **resolved in the insured's favor** and fast-tracks external review.*
- *Massachusetts **mandates** mental-health coverage; the federal law only requires parity if it's offered.*
