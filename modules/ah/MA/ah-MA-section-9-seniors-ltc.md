# MA A&H Overlay — Series 16-52
## Section 9: Seniors / LTC (Massachusetts statute layer)
**Scope: Massachusetts-specific | Pairs with the national core (`ah/national/ah-section-9-seniors-ltc.md`) | Source law verified against malegislature.gov & the Division of Insurance: M.G.L. c. 176K; 211 CMR 71.00 (Medicare supplement); 211 CMR 65.00 (long-term care); c. 176U § 3(e); c. 118E (MassHealth); 130 CMR 515.014; MACRA § 401 | MA Medigap plan structure verified against the Division's official "Medicare Supplement Plans Offered in Massachusetts 2026" carrier chart**

> **How this layer works:** Medicare itself is federal (national core). This overlay carries the two places Massachusetts breaks from the rest of the country: its **waiver-state Medigap designs** (Core, Supplement 1, Supplement 1A — not the lettered A–N plans) and its **211 CMR 65 long-term care** regulation, plus MassHealth and the LTC free look. National prep books routinely teach the lettered plans for MA — that's the wrong system here.

---

## Part 1: Massachusetts Medigap — the waiver plans (c. 176K; 211 CMR 71.08)
MA is one of only **three waiver states** (with MN and WI) and designs its own Medicare supplements instead of the lettered A–N plans:
- **Core Plan** — basic package: Part A hospital coinsurance (plus extension days after Medicare benefits end), Part B 20% coinsurance, hospice cost-sharing, first 3 pints of blood, and MA-mandated benefits including **60 days per calendar year of inpatient mental health** care.
- **Supplement 1** — Core plus: **Part A deductible, skilled nursing facility coinsurance, Part B deductible, foreign travel emergency**, and expanded inpatient mental health (**120 days per benefit period**). Roughly comparable to the old national Plan F.
- **Supplement 1A** (added 2020) — identical to Supplement 1 **except it does not cover the Part B deductible**.
- **MACRA applied to MA plans:** newly eligible **before 2020** → may buy Core, Supplement 1, or 1A; eligible **2020 or later** → **Core or 1A only** (Supplement 1 covers the Part B deductible, so it's barred). **Prime exam trap.**
- MA also bars providers from charging **Part B excess charges**, so MA plans don't need (or offer) excess-charge coverage.

## Part 2: MA Medigap enrollment & consumer protections — 176K; 211 CMR 71.10/71.20 (verified)
- **Guaranteed issue, always:** no issuer may **at any time** deny, condition, or price a MA Medigap policy based on **age, health status, claims experience, receipt of health care, medical condition, or genetic information** (71.10(1)) — MA Medigap is effectively **community rated**; genetic tests can never be required.
- **No pre-existing condition limitations or waiting periods — ever** (71.10(2)). (National materials describing 6-month pre-ex waits do not apply in MA.)
- **Required Part B open enrollment** (71.10(3)): the federal-style **6-month window** at 65+/Part B is codified as a floor.
- **Continuous open enrollment** (71.10(9)): issuers may hold year-round open enrollment (filed 30 days before starting; 60 days' notice to stop) — and per the Division's chart, MA carriers currently all do, so applicants can enroll or switch any time. Surcharges permitted only for **late enrollees** without continuous prior creditable coverage and for upgrades (71.20).
- Marketing protections under 211 CMR 71: marketing/advertising standards, **appropriateness of recommended purchase**, required disclosure and **buyer's guide**, **right to return** (free look), replacement rules, multiple-policy reporting, compensation limits.

## Part 3: MassHealth (c. 118E)
Massachusetts Medicaid. Income/asset-based eligibility; for "dual eligibles" it pays Medicare premiums and cost-sharing, and is the **payer of last resort** for **long-term custodial care** Medicare excludes — after the person spends down assets.

## Part 4: MA long-term care regulation — 211 CMR 65.00 (verified)
- **Inflation adjustment benefit must be offered** (65.06(1)) — buyer may decline.
- **Nonforfeiture benefit must be offered** (65.06(2)).
- **"Your Options for Financing Long-Term Care"** consumer guide (65.09), **outline of coverage** (65.101), and **policy illustration** (65.100) required.
- **Suitability** of recommended purchase; agent training/marketing standards (65.08).
- **Unintentional lapse protection (65.10):** no policy issued until the applicant designates **at least one person besides themselves** to receive lapse notices or signs a dated **written waiver** declining. No lapse for nonpayment unless the carrier notifies the insured **and the designee at least 30 days before** lapse, by first-class mail (deemed given 10 days after mailing), and not until the premium is 30 days overdue. Every policy must include **reinstatement** if the policyholder proves **cognitive impairment or loss of functional capacity** before the grace period expired.
- **Minimum standards (65.05):** elimination periods may not exceed **365 days**; benefits may NOT be conditioned on **prior hospitalization**, Medicare ineligibility, "steady improvement," or (narrow exceptions) medical-necessity standards. Post-claims underwriting prohibited (65.11). Benefit triggers align with the federal tax-qualified **2-of-6-ADLs / 90-day** standard (65.05(1)).
- **Right to return (free look) — c. 176U § 3(e):** return an LTC policy within **30 days of delivery** for any reason, premium refunded **within 30 days** of return; the right must be **prominently printed on or attached to the first page**.
- **MassHealth exemption (c. 118E § 33; 130 CMR 515.014):** a permanently institutionalized person's **home is protected from MassHealth liens/recovery** if, when purchased, their LTC policy met 211 CMR 65 standards — in practice at least **730 days (2 years)** of nursing-facility benefits, at least **$125 per day**, and an elimination period of **no more than 365 days**. The state's concrete incentive to buy private LTC coverage.

## Exam traps (MA-specific)
- *MA uses **Core / Supplement 1 / Supplement 1A**, never the lettered A–N plans* — the single most-missed MA fact.
- *Supplement 1 vs 1A differ only by the **Part B deductible**;* a 2020+ eligible can buy Core or **1A**, never Supplement 1.
- *MA Medigap has **no pre-existing waiting period, ever*** — a national 6-month pre-ex rule is wrong for MA.
- *The required MA LTC **offers** are inflation protection, nonforfeiture, and the third-party lapse designee* — there is no MassHealth premium discount; the MassHealth tie is the **lien/recovery exemption**.
