# A&H Producer Exam — National Core
## Module 9: Insurance for Senior Citizens and Special Needs Individuals
**Scope: national (applies to all states) | Source: federal Medicare statute/CMS, federal MACRA, the NAIC standardized Medigap framework, and the federal tax-qualified LTC standard. State Medigap designs (waiver states), state LTC regulations, the state LTC free look, and Medicaid specifics live in the per-state overlay (e.g., `ah/MA/ah-MA-section-9-seniors-ltc.md`)**

> **How to study this module:** Three blocks. Medicare itself is pure federal content — memorize the Part A/B structure and the enrollment windows. Medicare supplements (Medigap) are standardized into lettered plans nationally, but **three waiver states design their own** — so check the overlay if your state is one. Long-term care runs on the federal tax-qualified triggers plus state regulations. Medicare dollar amounts reset every year, so this teaches the *structure* — the exam tests structure, not this year's dollar figures.

## Part 1: Medicare (Outline 9.1)

Federal health insurance for people **65 and older**, plus younger people receiving **Social Security disability benefits (after 24 months)** and people with **end-stage renal disease (ESRD)** or ALS. Administered by **CMS**; funded by payroll taxes (Part A), premiums, and general revenue.

### Part A — Hospital Insurance
- **Premium-free** for those with **40 quarters (10 years)** of Medicare-covered employment (or a spouse with them); others may buy in.
- Organized around **benefit periods**: begins on inpatient admission, ends when the patient has been out of the hospital/SNF for **60 consecutive days**. The Part A deductible applies **per benefit period** — not per year. A patient can pay it more than once in a year.
- Inpatient hospital: after the deductible, days **1–60** fully covered; days **61–90** require daily coinsurance; beyond 90, the patient draws on **60 lifetime reserve days** (one lifetime pool, never renewed), each with a higher daily coinsurance.
- **Skilled nursing facility:** requires a prior **3-day inpatient hospital stay**. Days **1–20** fully covered; days **21–100** daily coinsurance; **nothing after day 100** per benefit period. Custodial-only care is NOT covered.
- Also covers: **hospice** (terminally ill, electing comfort care), **home health care**, and blood **after the first 3 pints**.

### Part B — Medical Insurance
- **Optional**, funded by a **monthly premium** (income-adjusted) plus general revenue.
- After an **annual deductible**, Medicare pays **80%** of the approved amount; the patient owes **20%** — with **no out-of-pocket cap** under Original Medicare. That missing cap is the reason Medigap exists.
- Covers: physician services, outpatient care, durable medical equipment, lab work, preventive services.
- Key exclusions: most dental, vision, hearing aids, and — critically — **long-term custodial care**.

### Enrollment windows
- **Initial Enrollment Period:** 7 months — the 3 months before the 65th-birthday month, the birthday month, and the 3 months after.
- **General Enrollment Period:** January 1 – March 31 each year, for those who missed the IEP.
- **Special Enrollment Period:** for those who kept working with employer group coverage — 8 months after that employment/coverage ends, penalty-free.
- **Late penalties:** Part B premium rises **10% for each full 12-month period** of delayed enrollment (lifetime); Part D carries roughly **1% per month** delayed.

### Part C — Medicare Advantage
Private plans (mostly HMO/PPO) that replace Original Medicare's payment structure: must cover everything A and B cover (hospice stays with Original Medicare), usually bundle Part D, often add dental/vision, and typically use provider networks with an annual out-of-pocket maximum. The enrollee still pays the Part B premium.

### Part D — Prescription Drug Coverage
Private, optional drug plans (standalone PDP or inside Medicare Advantage). Formularies and pharmacy networks apply; a federal **annual out-of-pocket cap on covered drugs ($2,000, indexed)** applies as of 2025. Late enrollment penalty applies without creditable drug coverage.

## Part 2: Medicare Supplements — Medigap (Outline 9.2)

### The standardized national system
In **47 states**, Medigap policies are standardized by NAIC into **lettered plans (A–N)** — every Plan G is identical no matter which carrier sells it, so consumers compare on price and service. Plan **A** is the basic/core benefit set every issuer must offer; richer letters add the Part A deductible, SNF coinsurance, foreign-travel emergency, and (for pre-2020 eligibles) the Part B deductible. **Massachusetts, Minnesota, and Wisconsin hold federal waivers and design their own plans** — if you're prepping for one of those states, use the overlay, not the lettered plans.

### MACRA — the Part B deductible cutoff (federal, applies everywhere)
Federal law (**MACRA**) bars anyone **newly eligible for Medicare on or after January 1, 2020** from buying any Medigap plan that **covers the Part B deductible** (nationally, that retired Plans C and F for new eligibles; equivalents in waiver states). So: eligible before 2020 → may buy a Part B-deductible plan; eligible 2020 or later → cannot. **This is a prime exam-trap fact in every state.**

### Federal consumer protections (universal floor)
- **Open enrollment:** a **6-month window** beginning the first month the person is **65+ and enrolled in Part B** — guaranteed issue, no health underwriting, during that window.
- **Free look / right to return,** required disclosure and **buyer's guide** delivery, replacement rules, suitability of the recommended purchase, and limits on multiple/duplicate policies are all standard Medigap consumer protections (exact free-look length is state-set — see overlay).
- Outside open enrollment, an issuer may medically underwrite unless a guaranteed-issue situation applies (states may broaden guaranteed issue — see overlay).

## Part 3: Other Options for Individuals with Medicare (Outline 9.3)

- **Working past 65 with employer group coverage (20+ employees):** the **group plan pays primary, Medicare secondary** (Medicare Secondary Payer). The employee may delay Part B penalty-free (SEP applies later).
- **Disabled employees and ESRD:** employer plans coordinate with Medicare under the secondary-payer rules (ESRD: group plan primary for the initial coordination period).
- **Medicaid** — the joint federal/state program. Income/asset-based eligibility; for "dual eligibles" it pays Medicare premiums and cost-sharing, and it is the **payer of last resort** for **long-term custodial care** that Medicare excludes — after the person has spent down assets. (State program names and specifics in the overlay.)

## Part 4: Long-Term Care Insurance (Outline 9.4)

### What it covers
The care Medicare doesn't: extended **custodial, intermediate, and skilled care**, delivered in nursing facilities or alternatives — **home health care, assisted living, adult day care,** and **respite care** (temporary relief for a family caregiver — a favorite exam definition).

### Benefit structure
- **Benefit period** (years of coverage or a dollar pool), **daily/monthly benefit amount**, and an **elimination period** (waiting days before benefits start) set the price.
- **Benefit triggers (federal tax-qualified standard, HIPAA):** inability to perform **2 of 6 Activities of Daily Living** — bathing, dressing, toileting, transferring, continence, eating — expected to last **at least 90 days**, OR **severe cognitive impairment**, certified by a licensed health care practitioner.
- Optional benefits: **guarantee of insurability** (buy more coverage later without underwriting) and **return of premium**.

### Standard LTC consumer protections (NAIC LTC model — states adopt with variations)
- **Inflation protection** and a **nonforfeiture benefit** must generally be **offered** (the buyer may decline).
- Required **consumer guide**, **outline of coverage**, and **policy illustration** delivery.
- **Suitability** review of the recommended purchase; agent training and marketing standards.
- **Unintentional-lapse protection:** designation of a third party to receive lapse notices (or a signed waiver), advance notice before lapse, and **reinstatement** if the lapse resulted from cognitive impairment / loss of functional capacity.
- **Minimum standards:** caps on the elimination period; benefits may **not** be conditioned on prior hospitalization, Medicare ineligibility, or "steady improvement"; **post-claims underwriting prohibited**.
- **Free look / right to return** the policy for a refund (length state-set — see overlay).
- (Exact day-counts, dollar minimums, and the citations are state-specific — see overlay.)

## Exam traps
- *A Part A benefit period ends after **60 consecutive days out** — a readmission later in the same year starts a new period and a new deductible* (deductible is per benefit period, not per year).
- *SNF coverage requires a prior **3-day inpatient hospital stay**; custodial-only care is never covered by Medicare.*
- *MACRA bars **new** (2020+) eligibles from any Part B-deductible Medigap plan* — applies in every state, lettered or waiver.
- *Original Medicare Part B has **no out-of-pocket cap*** — the reason Medigap exists.
- *LTC trigger is **2 of 6 ADLs for 90+ days** or severe cognitive impairment* — not 1, not "any duration."
- *In 3 waiver states (MA, MN, WI) the lettered A–N plans do not apply* — use the state overlay.
