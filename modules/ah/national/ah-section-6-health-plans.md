## Module 6: Health Insurance Plans

## Part 1: Medical Plan Concepts

The vocabulary pairs the exam tests:
- **Fee-for-service vs. prepaid** — traditional insurers reimburse per service rendered; HMOs collect a fixed periodic payment and provide care.
- **Limited (specified) vs. comprehensive** — limited-benefit coverages (dread disease, hospital indemnity) vs. comprehensive medical care.
- **Benefit schedule vs. usual/reasonable/customary (URC)** — a schedule pays fixed dollar amounts per procedure; URC pays what area providers typically charge.
- **Any provider vs. limited choice** — indemnity plans let the patient pick anyone; network plans trade choice for lower cost.
- **Insureds vs. subscribers/participants** — commercial insurer customers are *insureds*; service-plan (BCBS) and HMO members are *subscribers*, because the plan contracts with providers to deliver care rather than just paying claims.

## Part 2: Types of Providers and Plans

### HIPAA (federal)
The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act guarantees: **eligibility** rules that can't single out individual health status, **guaranteed issue** in qualifying group situations, **creditable coverage** (prior coverage counts toward any waiting limitation), and **guaranteed renewability** of group coverage.

### Blue Cross Blue Shield (service plans)
Contracts **both with subscribers and with providers**. Providers accept negotiated reimbursement and are paid **directly by the plan** — the subscriber receives service, not a reimbursement check.

### Commercial insurers — open network
Traditional indemnity: the insured may see **any provider**, the insurer reimburses, cost-sharing through deductibles and coinsurance.

### Health Maintenance Organizations (HMOs) — closed network
The exam's favorite. Characteristics:
- **Closed panel/network** — members must use plan providers (except emergencies)
- **Prepaid basis** — fixed periodic payments, care provided rather than claims reimbursed
- **Gatekeeper model** — a **primary care physician (PCP)** coordinates care; specialist visits require a **PCP referral**
- **Preventive care emphasis**
- **Emergency care** is covered even out-of-network
- *Exam trap:* the gatekeeper controls **referrals**, not emergencies. No HMO may require prior authorization for emergency services.

### Preferred Provider Organizations (PPOs)
The hybrid: an **open panel** with financial steering.
- In-network care costs less; out-of-network care is still covered at higher cost-sharing
- **Gatekeeper PPO** (PCP referral required) vs. **non-gatekeeper PPO** (self-refer)
- Retains **indemnity features** (deductibles, coinsurance), unlike a pure HMO
- *Contrast drill:* HMO = closed, prepaid, gatekeeper, subscriber. PPO = open panel, negotiated discounts, gatekeeper optional. Indemnity = any provider, URC reimbursement.

## Part 3: Cost Containment — utilization management

The three reviews:
- **Prospective review** — before treatment (precertification/prior authorization)
- **Concurrent review** — during treatment (ongoing stay monitoring)
- **Retrospective review** — after treatment (auditing appropriateness)

Adverse determinations and appeals follow a two-tier structure (internal grievance, then external review). The specific timelines, fees, and review body are **state-specific** — see the state overlay.

## Part 4: The ACA Layer (federal — PPACA / PHS Act)

Applies in every state:
- **Guaranteed issue** (PHS 2708): coverage must accept every applicant during enrollment periods.
- **Guaranteed renewability** (PHS 2703): renewed at the policyholder's option.
- **Preventive care** (PHS 2713): designated services with **no cost-sharing** in-network.
- **Emergency services** (PHS 2719A(b)): covered **without prior authorization** at in-network cost-sharing even out-of-network.
- **No health-status discrimination** (PHS 2705): no rating on health, claims history, or genetics.
- **Essential Health Benefits** (PHS 2707, 2711): individual/small-group must cover EHB categories; **no lifetime or annual dollar limits** on EHBs.
- **Cost-sharing limits** (2707(b)): annual out-of-pocket maximums.
- **Advance Premium Tax Credit** (PPACA 1401): income-based exchange subsidies (purchased through each state's marketplace).

## Part 5: Mental Health Parity (federal — MHPAEA 2008)

Plans offering mental health or substance use disorder benefits must provide them **at parity** with medical/surgical benefits: financial requirements and treatment limitations may be **no more restrictive** than comparable medical care, including non-quantitative limits like prior-authorization practices. (Many states **mandate** coverage on top of federal parity — see the state overlay.)

## Drill card (universal)
- Fee-for-service/indemnity = any provider, URC reimbursement. HMO = closed, prepaid, gatekeeper. PPO = open panel, in/out-of-network tiers.
- Gatekeeper controls **referrals**, never emergencies; emergency care needs no prior auth.
- HIPAA: creditable coverage, guaranteed issue/renewability for groups.
- Utilization review: prospective / concurrent / retrospective.
- ACA: guaranteed issue + renewability, $0 preventive in-network, no lifetime/annual limits on EHBs, OOP max, emergency without prior auth.
- MHPAEA: parity **if** mental health is covered (state law may mandate the coverage).

## Exam traps
- *Emergency care never requires prior authorization* (HMO gatekeeper or ACA alike).
- *ACA bars dollar limits only on **essential** health benefits; non-EHB benefits may carry limits.*
- *MHPAEA is a **parity** law, not a coverage mandate — the mandate (if any) comes from state law.*
- *HMO subscribers receive **care**; indemnity insureds receive **reimbursement**.*
